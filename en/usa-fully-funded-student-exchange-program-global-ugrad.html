Fully funded student exchange program - Global UGRAD

Date: February 28, 2021

Where: USA

For whom: Students studying any undergraduate major

What is the Global Exchange Program?

The Global Exchange Program (Global UGRAD) is sponsored by the US Department of State's Bureau of Educational and Cultural Affairs.

Global UGRAD is a one-semester study program for talented undergraduate students from around the world. program. During the semester, students can engage in social work, professional development, and cultural skills acquisition in the United States. Since 2008, more than 2500 students have won this scholarship. This is a great opportunity for you too, be sure to apply!

Financial Aid

Global UGRAD is a fully funded exchange program that includes a monthly stipend for student travel/return to the US, university tuition, room and board, health insurance, required books, and incidentals. Participants live in student housing with their American peers and are required to participate in twenty hours of community service.

Admission Requirements

The Global UGRAD Exchange Program selection is merit-based and open to the following students:

  • At least 18 years old at the start of the program;
  • Citizens of Uzbekistan currently living and studying in Uzbekistan;
  • Enrolled in full-time undergraduate programs in good standing at any accredited university in Uzbekistan;
  • Have completed at least the first semester of studies before starting the Global UGRAD program;
  • Complete at least one semester or equivalent at home after completing the Global UGRAD program. The Global UGRAD program may not be the final academic term of a student's undergraduate studies;
  • May demonstrate leadership potential through academic work, community involvement, and extracurricular activities;
  • Be proficient in written and spoken English. Finalists and alternates are required to take the TOEFL iBT test and obtain a minimum score of 48. The cost of TOEFL is covered by the program.
  • Eligible to obtain and maintain a US student exchange visa (J-1) required for the program;
  • Must return to their home country directly upon completion of the program.

Students studying in any undergraduate field may apply. Race, color, gender, religion, disability do not affect the admission results. Applicants are selected based on academic achievement, leadership skills, and readiness to study in the United States.

The following are not eligible for the Global UGRAD program:

  • US citizens and permanent residents of the United States;
  • Persons currently studying, living or working outside of Uzbekistan;
  • Former bachelors. persons participating in the global program of exchange;
  • local employees of US missions abroad working at the US State Department and / or the US Agency for International Development (USAID); employees
  • Family members (ie, spouses and children) of US State Department and USAID employees; also, the employee is not eligible for one year after dismissal;
  • Persons arrested, charged or convicted of a crime (excluding minor traffic violations).

2021-2022 application deadline

January 4, 2021: Application opens
2021 February 28: Application closes
March 1 - April 30, 2021: Applications will be reviewed and semi-finalists selected
April 1 - May 30, 2021: Semifinalists will be interviewed and take the TOEFL test
June 2021: Finalists and alternates will decide to apply

Applications must be in English and submitted online no later than February 28, 2021. Please note that you will not be able to add additional information to your application after the deadline!
