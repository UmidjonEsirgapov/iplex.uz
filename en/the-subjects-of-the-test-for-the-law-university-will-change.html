The subjects of the test for the law university will change

Main instead of the history subject in the block, an exam will be taken in law. This change will be implemented starting from the 2023 admission.

UTM has warned applicants who will apply in 2023 that they should now study the subjects of Basics of State and Law, Fundamentals of Constitutional Law.

For more information, please refer to the official information of Tashkent State University of Law below. we will do:

Today, when entering Tashkent State Law University, applicants will take tests from 5 subject complexes consisting of 2 departments. In this case, in the 1st section, 3 compulsory subjects for all directions - Mother tongue (Uzbek, Russian or Karakalpak language), Mathematics and History of Uzbekistan, in the 2nd section 2 subjects of the "jurisprudence" educational direction - History and Foreign Language subjects are collected.

This case, i.e. "jurisprudence" The designation of a non-specialist subject (history) in the department of subjects suitable for the educational direction leads to a lack of consistency between general secondary education and higher legal education, a decrease in interest in the subject of law at school, and as a result, a student entering higher education does not develop the necessary knowledge of legal subjects.

In this regard, the experience of foreign universities shows that when entering the relevant field of study, exams aimed at assessing preliminary knowledge of the field are held. For example, the National Admission Test for Law (LNAT) is required for admission to the University of Glasgow, King's College London, Nottingham and Oxford. on" Decree No. PF-5987 also defines the relevant task in this regard.

Based on the above, starting from the 2023/2024 academic year, when entering Tashkent State Law University, the 2nd block of tests, i.e. in subjects suitable for the bachelor's degree in "jurisprudence" The issue of introducing legal subjects instead of history was considered and approved by the University Council.

For information, today in general secondary education schools, the subjects of the state and the foundations of law and the foundations of constitutional law are taught in the upper classes for 4 years. Test questions will be prepared based on these school textbooks.

It should be noted that the introduction of this proposal serves a number of positive results, in particular:

  • school to teach children from an early age in-depth study of legal sciences, to study the Constitution and laws in detail;
  • to create high-quality legal textbooks in general secondary schools, in accordance with international standards;
  • regarding teachers who teach legal sciences as a result of the increase in requirements, a solid foundation will be created for the continuous improvement of their qualifications;
Representatives of the younger generation with basic legal knowledge will enter the Tashkent State Law University.
