Who will pay if a book is lost at school and how much?

Important news related to higher education institutions:

Summary:
Textbooks from the school library are provided to students for temporary use.
If the book is lost or becomes unusable, who should pay and how much? We will respond based on the regulation on the procedure for providing manuals.

Textbooks from the school library are provided to students for temporary use. If the book is lost or becomes unusable, who should pay and how much? If lost or becomes unusable, the following persons must cover the amount of damage:

  • The student's parents or their substitutes
  • Sponsors or teachers

The damage will be covered by the textbook or the invoice value of the book will be paid need.

How much should you pay?

The amount of payment varies depending on the class:

  • 1st grade students – one time of the book price
  • 2-4th grade students – twice the book price
  • 5-11 students of the class - four times the price of the book

Library supervision

The textbooks in the school library collection are supervised by librarians. Also, educational work is conducted among students about preservation of textbooks.
