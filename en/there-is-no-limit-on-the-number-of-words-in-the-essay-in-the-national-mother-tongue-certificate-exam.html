Words are not counted in the essay in the national certificate exam in mother tongue

According to the official attitude of the Education Evaluation Agency, there is no strict requirement for the number of words in the essay writing process in the national certificate exam in mother tongue. Also quoting great people is not mandatory. Applicants must present their arguments based on evidence within the topic.

The agency says that the practice of setting word counts forces the reader to artificially continue the essay. Therefore, there are no strict word limits.

Essays are checked via an automated platform, where the applicant's essay is submitted to the reviewers anonymously, with no name or surname. — Source
