Parents are obliged to ensure their children's education. This is defined in Article 51 of the Law "On Education", according to which, general secondary, secondary special or primary professional education of children of parents or their substitutes must monitor their receipt.

Legal liability and fines

If parents do not ensure their children's education, they may be fined according to Article 47 of the Code of Administrative Responsibility:

  • from 375,000 to 1,875,000 soums fine - if parents do not ensure that their children go to school regularly.
  • a fine from 1,875,000 to 3,750,000 soums - if this violation is repeated within a year.
  • a fine from 3,750,000 to 5,625,000 soums - parents in case of obstructing their children's education.
  • a fine from 5,625,000 to 9,375,000 soums or administrative imprisonment for up to 15 days - if this offense is repeated again.

Hindering teachers is also punished

In addition, if someone disrupts the service of school teachers if he illegally interferes with or obstructs the performance of his duties, the following fines are imposed:

  • For citizens, a fine from 1,875,000 to 2,625,000 soums
  • For officials, a fine from 2,625,000 to 3,750,000 soums
  • If the violation is repeated, the fine will be increased for citizens from 2,625,000 to 3,750,000 soums, for officials from 3,750,000 to 5,625,000 soums

Parents must monitor their children's regular school attendance, otherwise, may be held legally responsible.
