Japan: MEXT Scholarship Program for Bachelor's Degree. All expenses covered.

The latest news in the field of education is as follows:

Summary:
Grant Amount: Those selected will study Japanese for one year in Japan.
To pass the exam, you must know Japanese and English at least at a basic level.
This is enough to study Japanese at the basic level.

About the program: The MEXT program offers grants to international students to study at the undergraduate level in Japan.

Grant amount: Those selected will study Japanese for one year in Japan. After that, he studies for a bachelor's degree for four years. All tuition, living and travel expenses will be covered.

Requirements:

- 12 years of education (college or high school graduate; current undergraduate students may also apply);
- Basic Japanese;
- Intermediate English.

Apply procedure

You can save the questionnaire and the documents attached to it HERE.

Submitted documents:

- Questionnaire;
- School/lyceum/college or university grades application and a copy of certificate/diploma (translated copies must be certified by the seal of the place of study);
- Letter of recommendation from a teacher or school/college/lyceum director (Sample is attached);
- Medical certificate (Sample attached);

IMPORTANT: All documents must be filled out in English or Japanese and submitted to the Embassy of Japan in Tashkent in two copies (one original and one copy).

IMPORTANT: The list of those who have accepted the application form will be announced on August 4. In order to find out the result, you should apply to the embassy yourself.

IMPORTANT: Those whose applications are accepted will be examined in Tashkent in August-September. Applicants for the social and humanitarian field will take a test in Japanese, English and mathematics. Those majoring in natural sciences take an additional exam in chemistry, biology, or physics. To pass the exam, you need to know Japanese and English at least at a basic level.

You have at least 3-4 months before the exam. This is enough to learn the Japanese language at the elementary level.

Those who passed the written exam will be interviewed in September-October, and the final results of the competition will be announced in February 2021.

Embassy address: Sadiq Azimov Street, 1-proyezd, 28, Tashkent city

Document submission deadline: July 17, 2020
