If a student cannot transfer his studies - will he be expelled?

Students studying at state, non-state and foreign universities operating in the Republic of Uzbekistan can transfer their studies. However, in some cases, incorrect information is being spread about students being expelled if they fail to transfer their studies.

What does a student who fails to transfer study do?

If a student has applied for transfer to another higher education institution, but does not get enough points, he/she will be transferred to his/her previous - that is, he has the right to continue his studies at the university he is currently studying at.

This is a Constitutional right and cannot be abrogated by any internal order or regulation.

Is expulsion possible?

No, it is not possible. No institution of higher education - whether public, private or foreign - transfers studies. does not have the right to cancel the studies of a student who has not received it for this reason.

On the contrary, the student can legally continue his studies.

What if there is a threat based on the internal regulations?

Sometimes some universities threaten that "it is specified in the internal regulations" or "the student who cannot transfer will be expelled". Such actions are a violation of the student's legal rights.

If you encounter such a situation, you can contact law enforcement organizations, in particular the Ministry of Justice or Prosecution authorities.
