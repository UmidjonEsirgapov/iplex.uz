Higher education in Germany Absolutely free?

As we all know, German higher education is one of the few countries where higher education is free for national and international students. In addition, it is considered one of the most developed countries in the world. It is natural for any person to have the desire to study in Germany at least once. Now the question arises, "Is education in Germany really completely free?" The answer is "Yes and No". I will try to give a full explanation below for the answer.

Note:

Most universities in Germany (national and government funded) do not charge students a tuition fee. But students are required to pay the semester fee. The cost of the semester fee may vary from one university to another, but in most universities the amount of this fee does not exceed 250 euros per semester. We can say that education in Germany is free because it is not a big amount and this fee is charged for administrative work done by the university for the student. But not all universities in Germany are absolutely free. As an example, we can take universities in the cities that are part of the German Baden-Wurttemberg region. These universities have started demanding tuition fees from international students for some courses from the 2017/18 academic year. That is why we cannot say that higher education is absolutely free in all regions of Germany. But education in most universities is free, as we said above.

As in many developed countries, German universities offer courses taught in English. Ulm University in brief:

Ulm University was founded in 1967 as the "Ulm University of Medicine and Natural Sciences" and is the youngest state university in Baden-Württemberg. The university had approximately 10,000 students enrolled in the 2019/20 winter semester. The university is known for its close cooperation with companies that support the development of science in its surroundings.
