The most responsible stage of secondary school is the 11th grade. Because this year your future path will be decided. All students have the same dream: to pass the exam successfully and become a 100% student. But to achieve this, it is necessary to start proper preparation now, not only in the last month.

We will list the most important steps for you below:

1. Determine your goal

First of all, you need to know what field you want to study. Do you want to be a doctor? Or a programmer? Maybe an economist? If the goal is unclear, the preparation will be confusing. Therefore, "Which direction should I study?" find the answer to the question.

2. Choose a subject and study deeply

General knowledge of all subjects is important when preparing for DTM or university exams, but focus more on core subjects. For example:

  • For medicine - biology and chemistry.
  • For engineering - mathematics and physics.
  • For economics - mathematics and English.

Remember the rule: the most points in the exam come from your major.

3. Follow a daily plan

Read a little bit every day. Studying 5-6 hours in one day and then resting for the next 3 days will not give good results. On the contrary, regular preparation for 2-3 hours every day is more effective.

4. Work with tests

Just studying theory is not enough. Tests should become a habit every day.

5. Learn from mistakes

Every wrong answer is an opportunity. Review where you went wrong on the test. If you don't understand the first time, read it a second time, a third time. As a result, mistakes are reduced, and knowledge is strengthened.

6. Think about universities now

Most applicants only think about which university to apply to after the exam. In fact, it is very important to make a choice now.

7. Don't forget a healthy lifestyle

Many students are only devoted to the book and shorten their sleep. This is a big mistake. Because for the brain to work well:

  • enough sleep,
  • do sports,
  • proper nutrition is very important.

There are frequent changes in the education system. It is a big risk to be unaware of exam dates, admission procedures, new opportunities.
