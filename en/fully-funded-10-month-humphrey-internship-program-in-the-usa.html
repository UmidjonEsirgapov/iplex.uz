General information:

The Humphrey Fellowship Program - One of the Fulbright programs, it offers 10-month graduate courses to active, interested young people from around the world working in the public or private sector.

Note: Since this program is not a graduate degree program, depending on participants experience and skills.

Candidates must have:

  • Bachelor's degree;
  • At least five years of full-time, professional work experience;
  • Limited or no experience in the United States;
  • Demonstrated leadership qualities;
  • Proficiency in English. ability;

Required fields of study:

  • Human and institutional capacity;
  • Rights and freedoms;
  • Agriculture;
  • Similar fields;

The following candidates are not eligible for this program:

    • Recent university graduates (even if they hold senior positions);
    • Academic researchers without university faculty or management responsibilities (except in the fields of substance abuse education, prevention, and treatment and teaching of English as a foreign language);
    • For one or more academic years during the seven years prior to August 2023 Individuals who have attended graduate school in the United States;
    • Individuals with any type of US work experience lasting more than six months during the five years prior to August 2023;
    • Individuals with dual US citizenship or US permanent resident status are not eligible to apply for the program; they do not have a J visa.

Required documents:

  • 2 letters of recommendation (The letters of recommendation should be obtained from your current employer. Recommendations are submitted online by the recommender, but if this is not possible, they are requested to be downloaded here and sent to the official email of the American Embassy.)
  • Official grade book and diploma translated into English;
  • The English evaluation form should be left blank. Currently, IELTS and TOEFL certificates are not required, if available, please send them here;

Benefits provided by the scholarship

  • Cost of standardized tests required for admission to these universities will be covered
  • J-1 visa assistance (Exchange Program visa);
  • In economy class airline tickets;
  • Medical insurance;
  • Living expenses vary depending on the destination city. The scholarship does not cover the expenses of accompanying family members of the grantee.
  • Tuition and fees at the designated host university.
  • Professional development allowance for professional activities such as field trips, professional visits and conferences.

Application deadline: June 10, 2022
