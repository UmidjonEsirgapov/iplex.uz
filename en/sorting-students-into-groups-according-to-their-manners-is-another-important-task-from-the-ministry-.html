Jalilov also added that such an initiative of the ministry does not comply with the norms of the Constitution and asked the Ministry of Justice to respond to the situation. "Everyone has the right to freedom of thought, speech and belief." "Every person accused of committing a crime is not considered guilty until the case of a person accused of committing a crime is considered in court in a legal manner and publicly, and his guilt is determined. "The state ensures the rights and freedoms of citizens established in the Constitution and laws." These are the articles of the current Constitution of the Republic of Uzbekistan.

Adding a student (in general, a person) to lists such as "prone to crime", "on the verge of being influenced by foreign ideas", and in general, to what extent is the formation and maintenance of such lists by a state body consistent with the above-mentioned norms of the Constitution and the presumption of innocence? - writes a public activist.
