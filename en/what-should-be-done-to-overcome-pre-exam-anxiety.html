What should be done to overcome pre-exam anxiety?

Important news for students and applicants.

Summary:
Practice breathing techniques: Deep breathing exercises, meditation can help calm your mind and reduce anxiety.
Talk to yourself warmly: Remind yourself of your capabilities and that you have prepared well for the exam.
Good nutrition and hydration will have a positive effect on your focus and cognitive function.

Prepare seriously:

Making and sticking to a preparation schedule will help you feel more organized and confident. If you do not prepare well for the exam, if you are not prepared for the questions, you will not be able to stay calm.

Practice breathing techniques:

Deep breathing exercises, meditation will help calm your mind and reduce anxiety. Spend a few minutes each day doing breathing exercises to relieve stress.

Get enough sleep:

make it a priority to get a good night's sleep the night before the exam. Lack of sleep reduces attention, memory and has a negative effect on general performance. Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep to ensure a well-rested brain.

Exercise regularly:

Physical activity helps reduce stress. Get regular exercise to relieve stress and boost your mood.

Avoid trying to remember more at the last minute:

trying to remember a lot of information at the last minute can increase stress levels and is usually ineffective. Give your brain a break before the exam.

Talk to yourself warmly:

Remind yourself of your capabilities and that you have prepared well for the exam. Believe in yourself and your abilities.

Drink water and eat well:

follow a healthy diet and drink plenty of water. Eating well and staying hydrated will have a positive effect on your focus and cognitive function.

Remember, it's normal to feel nervous before an exam. Implementing these strategies can help you manage and reduce pre-exam anxiety, allowing you to perform to the best of your ability. Good luck! — Source
