How is the rating formed?

A national rating will be created for non-state universities

For the first time in Uzbekistan, a single national rating system covering both non-state and state higher education institutions will be introduced. This is specified in the decision of the Cabinet of Ministers PQ-200 (source).

How is the rating formed?

The rating is determined based on the following criteria:

  • Quality of education and the potential of professors and teachers;

  • Student achievements and employment indicators;

  • Research activity and international cooperation;

  • Number of accredited educational programs;

  • Effectiveness of infrastructure and management system.

Overall rating for public and private universities

According to PQ-200 decision:

  • State and non-state HEIs are evaluated in a single ranking;

  • The rating is updated every year and is publicly announced.

Higher positions in the rating give privileges

  • HEIs that have been in the top 10 in the rating for the past 5 years are next the complex is exempted from state accreditation;

  • This privilege encourages universities to maintain the quality of education at a constant high level.

Conclusion

The new national rating system serves to increase the prestige and competitiveness of non-state universities. The rating criteria allows for a more transparent and efficient assessment of the activities of HEIs. — Official Data
