Do the officials of OTM have the right to exclude those who have not paid the contract?

Useful information for applicants and students:

Brief content:
No, there is no right to do so, non-payment of the contract cannot be the reason for not including the student in the final and midterms.
If the student has not paid the contract at all, he can only be excluded from the ranks of students.
Student to the midterm or final It is absolutely impossible not to admit, to drop a student from the subject on purpose, to leave a student without admitting him to the class, and to perform other illegal actions. If the student has not paid the contract at all, he can only be expelled from the ranks of students.

It is absolutely impossible not to include the student in the mid-term or final, to deliberately fail the subject, to leave the student without entering the class, and to perform other illegal actions.

Terms of contract payment:

- at least 25% of the specified payment amount for applicants recommended for studentship until September 15 (or until the deadline set by the State Admissions Commission) and for students of the second and subsequent stages until October 1 (or until the deadline extended by the management of higher educational institutions);

- at least 50 percent of the specified payment amount until January 1, 75 percent until April 1, and 100 percent until July 1 (or until the deadlines extended by higher education institutions);

- for foreign citizens until the terms set by the management of Higher Education Institution; — Official Data
