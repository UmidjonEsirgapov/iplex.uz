TASIMO (Tashkent International Mathematics Olympiad) held in Tashkent is one of the largest international mathematics competitions in Central Asia. This Olympiad aims to discover young mathematicians, develop their potential and prepare them for the global STEM field. Every year, students come from different countries and test their knowledge.

Basic information about TASIMO

  • Organizer: Nordic International University

  • Partners: UNICEF, World Bank, Ministry of Higher Education, Science and Innovation, IDC training center, Rowan and Finland's Karelian Universities of Applied Sciences

  • Participants: More than 75,000 3-11th grade and academic high school students

  • Countries: Participants from 43 countries

  • Phases: 3 phases - online (December), offline (February and May).

  • Development of the STEM field: The Olympiad serves to train not only mathematical skills, but also mature specialists in the fields of science, technology and engineering.

  • International experience of students: During the competition, participants exchange knowledge and experience with peers from different countries.

  • Career and academic opportunities: TASIMO winners will have an advantage in further education or the job market.

    • Results of TASIMO-2025

    The TASIMO-2025 competition held in Tashkent on May 18-21, 2025:

    • The most talented young mathematicians from 14 regions and 48 foreign countries participated.

    • Welcoming the participants, cultural events, sports competitions and a trip to Green Hills Resort were organized.

    • At the final stage, the international jury determined and awarded the winners.

    Conclusion

    TASIMO not only develops students' mathematical potential, but also makes them globally recognized STEM experts. prepares This Olympiad creates international experience, knowledge exchange and career opportunities for young people.
