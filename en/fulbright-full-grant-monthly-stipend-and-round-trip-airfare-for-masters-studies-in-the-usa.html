Fulbright: full grant, monthly stipend and round-trip airfare for master's studies in the USA!

Brief content:
The Fulbright Foreign Student Program grant program invites young people from Uzbekistan who want to study at the master's level in the universities of the United States to apply for participation in its next grant program.
Within the program, an average of 2-4 grant places for Uzbek students each year

Fulbright Foreign Student Program grant program invites young people from Uzbekistan, who want to study at the master's level in US universities, to apply for participation in its next grant program. Applications are currently being accepted from candidates who wish to study in master's courses starting in 2024. Scholarships are awarded for all fields except medical fields that require direct patient care. Medical graduates can apply to this program for a master's degree in fields such as chemistry, biology, public health or hospital management.

On average, 2-4 Uzbek students are allocated grant places within the program every year. The number of Uzbeks who apply for this grant every year is on average 130-150.

Who is the grant for?

For those who want to study at the master's level

Link for more information

Benefits of the grant:

Those who won this grant the following benefits are provided:

  1. Tuition and all other fees are fully covered.
  2. Students are provided with a monthly stipend.
  3. Students are provided with medical insurance.
  4. Help is provided in obtaining a J-1 visa.
  5. Funds are provided for books and other educational materials
  6. Going from Uzbekistan to the USA and a return air ticket will be provided.

Requirements:

To win the grant, the candidate must meet the following requirements:

  1. The candidate must be a citizen of Uzbekistan and reside in Uzbekistan during the application and selection process.
  2. Must have a bachelor's degree.
  3. Have at least 2 years of work experience must be.
  4. Candidate must be fluent in English. Candidates are required to have TOEFL and GRE/GMAT certificates. However, during the application process, those who do not have such a certificate can also apply. Those who pass the application stage will be asked to take the required certification exam and submit the certificate. If you have the certificate requested during the application process, you can submit it at once.
  5. The program is designed for study at the master's level only.
  6. Candidates who have previously completed the master's level can also participate in the program.

Program admission process:

Applications for the 2024-2025 academic year Must be submitted by May 25, 2023, 6:00 p.m.. For this, it is enough for the candidate to fill out an online application and upload the requested documents to the required places (first, you need to open an account to apply; if you have already opened an account, you can submit it through your account). Only online applications are accepted for the program.

Within the program, 2-4 Uzbek students are given the opportunity to study at US universities in a 2-year master's course. The university where the winners of the program will study is determined by the Institute for International Education (IIE) and the Fulbright Foreign Student Program. Main documents required:

  1. Bachelor diploma; if applicable, graduate degree.
  2. Grades/diploma applications.
  3. 3 letters of recommendation.
  4. TOEFL certificate (if applicable).
  5. GRE/GMAT certificate (if applicable).
  6. CV.
  7. Study Plan.
  8. Personal Statement Essay).
