Hong Kong PhD Scholarship Program to study at one of the most prestigious universities in Hong Kong in China

General Information: — Official Data

The Hong Kong PhD Fellowship Scheme (HKPFS) , established by the Research Grants Council (RGC) in 2009, aims to attract the world's best and brightest students to pursue doctoral studies at Hong Kong universities.
Candidates requirements:
Candidates admitted to full-time PhD courses at the following 8 universities are eligible to apply, regardless of background, previous work experience and ethnicity.
  • City University of Hong Kong;
  • Hong Kong Baptist University;
  • Lingnan University;
  • The Chinese University of Hong Kong;
  • The Education University of Hong Kong;
  • The Hong Kong Polytechnic University;
  • The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology;
  • The University of Hong Kong.
Note: Candidates must be of high academic standing and possess sufficient knowledge and skills to conduct scientific research.
Awards:
The scholarship is HK$322,800 (approximately US$41,400) annually for up to three years for each awardee and conference and research-related travel. Provides stipends of HK$13,500 (approximately US$1,730). 300 PhD scholarships will be awarded in the academic year 2022/23. Additional support may be provided by selected universities for PhD degree recipients. For details, please contact the relevant universities.
In Hong Kong universities usually start their academic year in September.
Admission Criteria:
  • Applications shortlisted will be reviewed by one of the following two selection committees, made up of experts in the respective council fields, depending on the fields of study: 1) Sciences, Medicine, Engineering and Technology 2) Humanities, Social Sciences and Business Studies
  • Candidates' academic background Although worthy of attention, Selection Committees consider the following factors:
1) Must have high academic performance;
2) Must have research ability and potential;
3) Must have leadership skills.
Note: Please read the detailed information before applying for the program.
