In Uzbekistan, starting from April 1, the salary of some pedagogues will be increased by 20%

Brief content:
The draft of the presidential decree on the implementation of the development strategy of Uzbekistan for 2022-2026 in the "year of human attention and quality education" has been submitted for public discussion. the project provides for the following: starting from the 2023-2024 academic year, all regions of the republic and the city of Tashkent will provide free meals to primary school students; Starting from April 1, 2023, the monthly salaries of teachers of 27 professional educational institutions will be increased by 20% and will be equal to 3 million soums on average. Starting from June 1, 2023, the maximum speed of vehicles will be reduced from 70 to 60 kilometers per hour in all populated areas of the republic; • in order to further strengthen the legal protection of business entities and liberalize their liability, a one-year moratorium will be established on the introduction of new types of liability and punishment measures against business entities from March 1, 2023; • In 2023, the annual volume of new housing construction in 53 "New Uzbekistan" areas and 161 other areas, including private contracting organizations, will be doubled to 90,000; • Sufficient funds will be allocated from the State budget to allocate subsidies to cover part of the initial contribution and interest payments to 26 thousand citizens; • In 2023, 8 trillion soums will be allocated from the State budget for financing projects formed on the basis of public opinion, of which 4 trillion soums will be allocated for the "Prosperous village" and "Prosperous neighborhood" programs; • From July 1, 2023, through the implementation of the "Neighborhood Budget" system in all districts and cities, up to 800 billion soums will be allocated to the neighborhoods to independently solve their problems and implement social projects; • it is determined that technical conditions are not required for connecting solar, wind and biogas power plants with a capacity of 20 kW and below to low (0.23-0.4 kV) voltage networks; • From September 1, 2023, budgetary organizations will be obliged to install solar panels and hot water collectors, and until 2026, 60 percent of the energy consumption of their buildings will be gradually transferred to "green energy". The draft of the Presidential Decree on the implementation of the state program in 2023 was submitted for public discussion.
