The educational processes of every person's life consist of different stages, each of which brings its own difficulties and joys. Below we provide detailed information about various stages of education and their features.

1. The happiest and innocent period: School education

School years are one of the most cheerful and simple periods in a person's life. During this period:

  • Children learn basic life values.
  • The process of friendship, creativity and discovery of new knowledge takes place.
  • The educational process is relatively less stressful because teachers and parents are supportive.

2. The most difficult and difficult period: Matriculation

Matriculation is preparatory process for finishing school and entering higher education, and it is the most stressful and difficult period. At this stage:

  • Applicants face strong competition to enter higher education institutions.
  • A lot of time and effort is required.
  • Issues such as exams, tests and choosing a future profession make this period one of the most difficult.

3. Higher education: the period of independence and development

Student years are one of the most independent stages in a person's life, in which:

  • Academic and professional knowledge is formed.
  • Students learn to plan their lives independently.
  • During this period great responsibility and difficulties also exist:
    • Responsibility for independent study and learning increases.
    • Projects, research and exams force students to work seriously.

4. Post-secondary education: Stage of professional development

Post-secondary education (postgraduate, professional development or doctoral studies) serves to develop a person's professional and scientific activity. During this period:

  • People gain deep knowledge and experience in their field.
  • New discoveries are made through scientific research.
  • Educational areas are chosen based on personal interests and needs.

Conclusion

Each the educational stage brings specific challenges and opportunities. The application period is the most difficult, and successfully passing this stage requires will, planning and strong preparation. — Official Data
