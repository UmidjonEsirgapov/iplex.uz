Working a lot does not always produce efficient results. Therefore, one of the most popular time management methods in the world - the Pomodoro technique was developed. This technique helps to focus, reduce fatigue and increase productivity.

What is the Pomodoro technique?

This method was developed by the Italian Francisco Cirillo in the 1980s. Its essence is that you work with full attention for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. After repeating this process 4 times, a longer break of 15-30 minutes is recommended.

Benefits of the Pomodoro Technique

  • Easier concentration
  • Increases work productivity
  • Reduces fatigue
  • Helps to plan tasks gives

How to apply?

  1. Choose a task – identify the work you need to do.
  2. Set a timer for 25 minutes – focus only on this task during this time.
  3. After the timer goes off, take a 5 minute break – time for a short rest
  4. Repeat the process 4 times, then take a longer break of 15-30 minutes.

The Pomodoro technique is an effective method for studying, work and other activities, helping to divide work time and prevent fatigue.
