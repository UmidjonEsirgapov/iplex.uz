Contract prices in private universities are expected to increase due to the closure of correspondence education

Today, the most relevant news in the field of education was:

Brief content:
From the 2025/2026 school year, correspondence education will be completely canceled in the higher education system of Uzbekistan. Enrollment in full-time, evening, and distance education will be limited—which will reduce financial flow.
Expected consequences: Private universities will revise contract rates; Changes affect the choice of applicants and the balance of the education market; In the conditions of competition, attention to the quality of education may increase.

From the 2025/2026 academic year, the form of external education will be completely abolished in the higher education system of Uzbekistan. This change is expected to significantly influence the activity of private universities.

Why will the prices increase?

Currently in Uzbekistan:

  • 71 private universities are operating;

  • 508,720 people are studying there. 71% of students (that is 361,461) are studying part-time.

As a result of the closure of part-time education:

  • Private universities are more likely to increase contract prices to cover costs;

  • Day, evening and the possibility of admission to forms of distance education will be limited — which will reduce the financial flow.

What restrictions will be introduced?

  • Universities may accept up to 50% of full-time students in the evening or distance form;

  • This limit is the previous part of private HEIs. will force them to give up their major streams in education.
