What benefits does an applicant with a disability have?

Privileges and opportunities for applicants with disabilities

Various benefits for citizens with disabilities when entering higher education is available. In this article, you will get complete information about facilities, fee exemptions, special quotas and exams specifically for applicants.

Entrance Exam Fee Exemption

Applicants with disabilities are exempted from paying the fee to participate in the tests. This privilege applies to all persons with disabilities, and it must be specified separately when applying.

Special grant quotas for persons with disabilities of groups I and II

Citizens with disabilities of groups I and II are allocated a state grant in the amount of 2 percent of the general admission quota. This quota is distributed within the scope of OTM courses.

  • To use the privilege, the applicant must score at least 30% of the maximum points. Link

Special exam for blind applicants

Higher education institutions organize special tests for blind applicants. These tests are carried out according to their condition.

Master's degree in the field of "Special pedagogy, defectology" based on a grant

Citizens with disabilities have the opportunity to study in the master's degree in the field of "Special pedagogy, defectology" on the basis of a full state grant.

Helping relatives of disabled students opportunity

In order to help students with disabilities studying in HEIs, conditions are created for one of their relatives to be with them in HEIs. This is done to facilitate and support the educational process.

Conclusion

The conditions created for applicants with disabilities serve to fully ensure their right to education. Every citizen can realize his knowledge and potential through opportunities such as quotas, special exams and fee exemptions allocated by the state. — More information
