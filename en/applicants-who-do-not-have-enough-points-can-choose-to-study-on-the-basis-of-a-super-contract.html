Applicants who do not have enough points can choose to study on the basis of a super-contract

Brief content:
Applicants who did not score high enough in the entrance tests to higher education institutions have the opportunity to study on the basis of a super-contract.
This system gives applicants the right to continue their studies in the direction of their choice - only the payment amount is higher than a regular contract.

What is the Super-contract and who is it for?

There is a super-contract option for applicants who did not score enough points in the entrance tests to higher education institutions. This system gives applicants the right to continue their studies in the field of their choice - only the payment amount will be higher compared to the regular contract.

How is the super-contract fee calculated?

üéØ If 0.1 - 1.0 points are not enough:

The applicant will pay 1.5 times of the regular contract.

üéØ If 1.1 - 2.0 points are not reached:

The amount of payment is 2 times of the normal contract.

üéØ If 2.1 - 3.0 points are not reached:

Super-contract amount is 2.5 times of the normal contract.

üéØ If 3.1 - 4.0 points are not reached:

In this case, it is required to pay 3 times of the normal contract.

What happens if you do not get more than 4.0 points?

If the applicant did not get more than 4.0 points in the test, then he can be admitted to study on the basis of graded payment-contract. Depending on the direction, the amount of this contract can be from 8 times to 25 times of the normal contract.

Note:

This procedure has been in effect for several years and no significant changes are expected for the 2025/2026 academic year.
