14 new educational areas in "Jurisprudence" in 2025

For the 2025/2026 academic year, applicants can apply for 14 expanded and specialized educational areas within the "Jurisprudence" specialty. These directions are aimed at deepening legal knowledge in the country, introducing modern approaches to the field, and training specialized specialists.

Main and new directions of jurisprudence

1. Jurisprudence: by types of activity

In this direction, general legal training covering all the main areas of law is given.

2. Jurisprudence: Islamic law

The foundations of Sharia law, jurisprudence and their application in modern legislation are studied.

3. Jurisprudence: agricultural law

Specializes in regulation of legal relations related to property, land and production in agriculture.

4. Jurisprudence: international legal activity

Knowledge of international law necessary for working with diplomatic and international organizations is given.

5. Jurisprudence: international transport law

The basics of international legislation in the field of transport, cargo transportation and logistics are taught.

6. Jurisprudence: business law

It deals with relations between business entities, contracts and company law.

7. Jurisprudence: state legal activity

Legislation, management and legal practice necessary for working in state bodies are taught.

8. Jurisprudence: criminal-legal activity

Criminal Code, criminal responsibility, punishment system and judicial practice are studied in depth.

9. Jurisprudence: transboundary water relations

Water resources, ecology and legal relations on water with neighboring countries are analyzed.

10. Jurisprudence: international law and comparative law

Direction on comparing the laws of different countries and researching international norms.

11. Jurisprudence: land relations

Land ownership, land lease and legal resolution of land disputes are taught.

12. Jurisprudence: civil and business law

Civil law and commercial law are studied together. Prepares for notarial and property relations.

13. Jurisprudence: investigative activity

Basics of pre-trial investigation, crime detection, evidence collection and procedural law are taught.

14. Jurisprudence: prosecutorial activity

Skills for working in supervisory bodies, ensuring legality and acting as a public prosecutor are formed.

Conclusion

From 2025, the "Jurisprudence" areas have been significantly expanded, and each area provides a deeply specialized approach to a certain field. This allows applicants to apply in the most suitable field based on their interests and career plans.
