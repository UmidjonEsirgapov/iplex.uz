Has the obligation to work for 3 years of those who graduated from the university with a grant canceled?

Today, the most relevant news in the field of education was this:

Brief content:
By the decision of the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan dated 02.06.2005 No. PQ-92, starting from the 2005/2006 academic year, graduates of higher education institutions who studied on the basis of state grants in all fields of education must work for at least three years the practice of distributing work to educational institutions, enterprises and organizations was introduced under the condition.
However, this decision has become effective in accordance with the list of certain decisions of the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan that are recognized as having lost their effectiveness, approved in Appendix 3 to the decision of the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. PQ-279 of June 15, 2022 has lost.
So, now the obligation of the persons who graduated from higher education institutions on the basis of a state grant to work for 3 years to get a diploma has been canceled.

By the decision of the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan dated 02.06.2005 No. the practice of assigning work to educational institutions, enterprises and organizations to graduates of higher educational institutions with the condition of mandatory work for at least three years was introduced.

However, this decision is in accordance with the list of certain decisions of the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan that are recognized as invalid, approved in Appendix 3 to the decision of the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. PQ-279 dated June 15, 2022 accordingly, it has lost its validity.

Therefore, the obligation of those who have completed higher education institutions on the basis of a state grant to work for 3 years to receive a diploma has also been canceled. Therefore, this year, those who graduated from higher education institutions on the basis of a state grant can receive their diplomas without the obligation to produce them. — Official Data
