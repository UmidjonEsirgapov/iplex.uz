In one case, a student set himself on fire because he failed an exam

Important news for students and applicants has been announced.

Summary:
A student in Namanganda set himself on fire because he failed to pass his exams and had to retake them.
According to medical information, X.X. has deep burn wounds on 50 percent of his body from the flames.
Despite the urgent medical treatment provided by the doctors, he died in the hospital yesterday, February 13.

In Namangan, the student set himself on fire because he failed to pass his exams and had to retake them. As a result, he died. Hayat Shamsutdinov, the press secretary of the Prosecutor General's Office, informed QALAMPIR.UZ about this.

On February 10 of this year, Kh.Kh., a third-year student of the Namangan Institute of Engineering and Construction, lived in Fergana region. because he failed to pass the intermediate exams in 5 subjects at the university, he had to retake them. The group leader called the student's father and warned that his son failed to pass the intermediate exams in the above 5 subjects. In turn, the father reprimanded his son over the phone. After that, the student X.Kh. In a mental depression, he brought gasoline to the field-adirlik field in Yangikurgan district and set himself on fire.

Citizens who saw Kh.K. with burning injuries helped him and brought him to the hospital.

According to medical information, it was shown that Kh.K. had deep burns on 50 percent of his body from the flames.

Despite urgent medical treatment provided by doctors, he was hospitalized yesterday, February 13. died.

On the same day, the Yangi-Kurgan district prosecutor's office opened a criminal case under Article 103, Part 1 of the Criminal Code.
