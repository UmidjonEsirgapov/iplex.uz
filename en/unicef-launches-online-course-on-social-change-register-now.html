UNICEF is launching an online course on social change: Register now!
University of Pennsylvania and United The United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF) is jointly launching a public open online course on social norms and social change. The full course, consisting of one theoretical and one practical part, 4 weeks of coursework, is taught in English. Students who have attended previous sessions of the course rated the first part of the course a 4.5 and the second part a 5.0 out of 5.

The first course teaches students how to distinguish between social norms and social structures, such as customs or conventions. These differences are critical to effective policy interventions aimed at creating new, beneficial norms or eliminating harmful ones.

The course teaches how to measure social norms and the expectations that support them, and how to decide whether they lead to certain behaviors. The course discusses several issues closely related to human rights, such as child marriage, gender-based violence and sanitation practices.

The second part of the course looks at social change, the tools that can be used to effect change and the practical application of everything learned in the first part of the course.

Both parts of the course are free however, those who wish to become Penn-UNICEF certified can obtain a verified certificate for a fee.
