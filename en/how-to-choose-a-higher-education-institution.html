Which profession do I like?

Choosing a higher education institution depends on what profession you want to pursue. Let's say you decide to become a lawyer. Make a list of universities that train such specialists and analyze the pros and cons of each. Find out where the teaching staff is strongest, how training (internships) and internships take place, and what format lectures are held in. Pay attention to the requirements imposed by the management of the higher education institution on the discipline and educational process. The best way to get all the necessary information is to talk to graduates or current undergraduate students. Student brothers are very friendly and open-minded and can never hide the existing problems. Unfortunately, places for the state grant are limited, and there are always many people who want to study. If you are accepted to study on the basis of a fee-contract, think about how you will pay it in advance and keep in mind that its price will increase at least once a year. Consider the fee-contract amount when choosing a higher education institution and a field of study.
