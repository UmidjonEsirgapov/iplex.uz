Essay Writing Phrases and Clichés

It is very important to express your thoughts clearly, logically and clearly in the process of writing an essay. Therefore, the phrases and clichés used in essay writing help the writer to make the text structured and fluent. The following is a collection of the most commonly used phrases according to the structure of the essay.

Phrases for the introduction

The role of the introduction is to reveal the topic of the essay and prepare the reader for the topic. The following phrases will help to start the introduction effectively.

Ready phrases for introduction

  • "Today the issue of ... is relevant in our society."

  • "It is no secret that the problem of ... directly affects all people."

  • "Many scientists and experts ... express different views."

  • "There are different opinions on this topic."

  • "First, let's look at the roots of the issue of ...."

Phrases for expressing first-person opinions

It is important to clearly state the first point of view throughout the essay.

Phrases expressing the first opinion

  • “Some people think that …”

  • “A group of experts say that …”

  • “Some people think about this: …”

  • “They think that … and the most important way."

  • "It must be recognized that there are opinions against ...."

Phrases for expressing the opinions of the other side

Showing opposing opinions gives balance and a critical approach to the essay.

Phrases for opposing opinions

  • "Those who do not agree with this opinion emphasize ...."

Expressions for expressing personal opinion

When writing an essay, the author's personal position should be clear and reasonable.

Expressions for personal opinion

  • "Personally, I think about this issue as follows approach: …”

  • “In my opinion, …”

  • “Based on my own experience, I can say that …”

  • “Taking everything into account, I think the following: …”

  • “The most I consider the correct approach to be ….”

Phrases used to give evidence and examples

Evidence strengthens the idea of ​​the essay and increases its credibility.

Phrases for evidence and examples

  • “For example …”

  • “Looking at statistical data, ..."

  • "Studies show that ..."

  • "It also appears from life experience that ..."

  • "This can be explained by the following example: ..."

Phrases for the conclusion

The conclusion summarizes the content of the essay and presents the final idea.

Phrases for the conclusion

  • "From the above considerations, it can be concluded that ..."

  • "Thus, the issue of ... occupies an important place in our society."

  • "In general, …”

  • “As a result, it can be said that …”

  • “In conclusion, … will be a decisive factor for future development.”

Common clichés used in essay writing

These clichés are used in different places throughout the essay It is possible.

Common clichés

  • "It is no secret that ..."

  • "According to the demand of the times..."

  • "One of the most urgent issues of today is ..."

  • "Humanity development on the way ..."

  • "It should be emphasized that ..."

  • "Many studies prove that ..."

  • "One of the factors that plays an important role in the life of society is ..."

  • "For every person ..."

Conclusion

In writing an essay, expressions and clichés are the main language tools of the writer. They help to convey the idea in an orderly, logical and fluent manner. However, excessive use of these expressions can make the content of the essay artificial. The most important thing is not through clichés, but through expressing your opinion clearly and effectively is to write a quality essay. — Read on infoedu.uz
