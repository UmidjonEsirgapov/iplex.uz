Brief about the grant program:

Asian Development Bank Japan Scholarship Program since 1988, the grant program has been supporting talented and excellent students from countries in Asia and the Pacific Ocean in the developed countries of the world. and has been providing the opportunity to study for free in master's degree courses in the fields of technology, economics, business and management, development studies, law and public policy. Between 1988 and 2015, the Asian Development Bank allocated funds in the amount of 170 million US dollars for the JSP grant program. The main goal of the program is to provide financial support to students with high scientific potential and to create an opportunity for them to return to their country as mature personnel and contribute to the development of their country. So far, 3,500 candidates from 37 partner countries have been awarded this grant, and 40% of them are women. will be done.     Benefits of the grant:

    This grant program allocates funds for the following expenses:

    • Study funds;
    • Monthly stipend and housing rent funds;
    • Funding for textbooks and study materials;
    • Medical insurance expenses;
    • Travel expenses;
    • Students interested in research can write a research paper with a special grant. In addition, in certain cases, a scholarship may be allocated for computer literacy, language course and other such courses.

    Grant requirements:

    1. Candidates must be citizens of the countries listed above;
    2. Applicants participating in this program must have initially been admitted to a master's degree at one of the partner universities;
    3. Candidates must have a bachelor's degree;
    4. Candidates must have at least 2 years of full-time professional work experience prior to application;
    5. Continue reading must be able to speak and write in English fluently;
    6. Must not be over 35 years old at the time of application. In exceptional cases, in the programs for senior staff and managers, the applicant is allowed to be under 45 years old;
    7. Candidates must be in good health;
    8. Must agree to return to their home country upon graduation;
    9. Executive directors, assistant directors, management staff, consultants and their relatives and family members of the Asian Development Bank are eligible to participate in this program.
    10. Local employees of this program are also not eligible to participate in the program;
    11. Applicants living or working in a country other than their own country are not eligible to participate in this program;
    12. Candidates who are studying at the master's level are not eligible to apply for this program;
    13. This program is not applicable to study for a bachelor's degree, distance learning programs, short-term training, conferences, seminars, research writing and research does not sponsor research projects.

    Document submission and acceptance process:

    • Applications should be sent directly to one of the educational institutions participating in the grant program here ;
    • The procedure and deadline for submitting documents may be different for each university. The admission process is carried out in the following stages:
    1. Candidates should apply to one of the partner higher educational institutions for admission information and an application form;
    2. Candidates must download all the documents required by the university for the study program and the grant program application in MS WORD/PDF together with other documents. fill in;
    3. Applications are sent to the selected university in preparation;
    4. The university to which the documents are submitted will review them and decide whether to accept the candidate;
    5. The list of candidates selected by the university will be sent to the Asian Development Bank;
    6. The Asian Development Bank will review the list sent by the university;
    7. The Asian Development Bank will send the candidates to Japan. director for approval;
    8. Asian Development Bank selects candidates and informs universities about it;
    9. Selected universities inform candidates about this.
