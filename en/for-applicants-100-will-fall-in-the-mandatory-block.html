Who were the prime ministers after independence?

The number of prime ministers who led the country's government after the independence of the Republic of Uzbekistan is four. is enough. Each of them led important political and economic reforms in their time. Below is detailed information about them.

1. Abdulhashim Mutalov (1992–1995)

After independence, he served as the first prime minister of the Republic of Uzbekistan from 1992 to 1995. He played an important role in the country's transition to a new political system.

2. O'tkir Sultanov (1995–2003)

From 1995 to 2003, he served as prime minister. During his time, economic stability and the formation of the management system were among the main tasks.

3. Shavkat Mirziyoyev (2003–2016)

Shavkat Mirziyoyev, who was later elected to the post of President of Uzbekistan, worked as prime minister for 13 years. During his administration, great changes were made in the infrastructure, agriculture and social sectors.

4. Abdulla Aripov (2016-present)

From 2016 until now, he has been working as the prime minister. During his time, digital economy, electronic government and innovative projects were developed. The current stage of reforms corresponds to this period.
