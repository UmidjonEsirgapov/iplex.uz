It is very important to have versatile knowledge and skills in the teaching profession. In particular, the ability to teach several subjects creates a wider range of opportunities for teachers. According to the President's Decree on the approval of the national program for the development of public education in 2022-2026, starting from the 2022/2023 school year, teachers have been given the opportunity to teach several subjects. This innovation allows teachers to obtain additional specialization in related fields of study.

Opportunity to Teach Two Subjects

Starting from the 2022/2023 academic year, the procedure for providing basic and additional specialization for teachers in related fields of undergraduate education has been introduced. It includes the following educational areas:

  • Mathematics and Informatics:

    • Main specialty: Mathematics
    • Additional specialty: Informatics teacher
    • Teachers in this field have the opportunity to teach mathematics and informatics at the same time. The purpose of this direction is to familiarize students with modern technologies and mathematical knowledge.

  • Pedagogy:

    • Main specialty: Pedagogy
    • Additional specialty: Pedagogical teacher
    • Teachers in the field of pedagogy will also have the opportunity to teach the subject of education. It is aimed at strengthening the moral and cultural education of students.

  • Biology:

    • Main specialty: Biology
    • Additional specialty: Teacher of natural sciences
    • Biology teachers will now have the opportunity to teach natural sciences, including chemistry and physics. This allows students to get to know the natural sciences more widely.

  • Special Pedagogy:

    • Main specialty: Sign language pedagogy
    • Additional specialty: Sign language interpreter
    • Sign language pedagogues can now also work as sign language interpreters. This creates great opportunities for deaf and hard of hearing students.

Conclusion

These new procedures allow teachers to expand their skills and provide a broader knowledge to students. The opportunity to teach two subjects greatly helps not only the professional growth of teachers, but also the comprehensive education of students.
