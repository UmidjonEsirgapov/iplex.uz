Friedrich Ebert Stiftung offers scholarships for international students to study in undergraduate, graduate and doctoral programs. You can apply for different academic fields. High academic performance, fluent knowledge of English and German are required to apply.

Academic requirements:
-Study or enrolled in a public or state-recognized higher education institution in Germany.
-Sufficient knowledge of German, even if the courses are conducted in English. This means at least level 2 from "Deutsche Sprachprüfung für den Hochschulzugang", level 4 from "Test Deutsch als Fremdsprache", "Sertifikat C1" by the Goethe Institute, "Deutsche Sprachdiplom der Kultusministerkonferenz" II or Telc C1 Hochschule level.

Number of grants:
-Up to 40 scholarships per year.

Duration:
-The maximum funding period is determined according to BAföG regulations.

Grant value:
-International students receive a scholarship in the amount of 861 euros per month. In addition, health insurance costs are covered and candidates with children receive an additional family allowance of 276 euros.
