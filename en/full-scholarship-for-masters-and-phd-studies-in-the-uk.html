Full scholarship for Masters and PhD studies in the UK

Useful information for applicants and students:

Summary:
The Doctoral Center at King's College London announces a fully-funded grant program to support international students in pursuing a full-time PhD in the following faculties: Life and Medical Sciences; Psychiatry, psychology and neurology; Natural and mathematical sciences; Nursing, midwifery and palliative care; Dentistry, oral and cranial sciences.
The number of grants is 10.
Distance education programs are not supported by grants. These scholarships cannot start before or after June 2022.

The Doctoral Center at King's College London offers full-time doctoral scholarships to foreign students in the following faculties announces a fully funded grant program to support their degree in:

  • Life and Medical Sciences;
  • Psychiatry, Psychology and Neuroscience;
  • Natural and Mathematical Sciences;
  • Nursing, Midwifery and Palliative Care;
  • Dentistry, Oral and cranial sciences.

Program participants can conduct research along with free tuition for 4 years. The number of grants is 10. Including a stipend of £17,609 per year and up to £5,000 for research.

IMPORTANT! Scholarships are only for students starting study or research on 1 June 2022.

Requirements:

  • Candidates must not be a UK citizen and have a thorough knowledge of English;
  • Must have applied to study at one of the highly awarded faculties of King's College London by October 1, 2021;
  • Be able to start an MPhil / PhD degree at the university in June 2022 must plan;
  • must have an offer of admission to obtain an MPhil / PhD scientific degree by January 31, 2022;

Note! Students starting their PhD at King's College are not eligible for the programme. Distance learning programs are not funded. These scholarships cannot start before or after June 2022.
