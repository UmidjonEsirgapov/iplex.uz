Is the number of education courses unlimited this year? DTM replied

A briefing was held in AOKA with the participation of Orzikul Boboyev, head of the information service of the State Test Center. A number of questions were answered in it.

Has the national testing system been fully implemented? Has the certificate been issued in all subjects?

To date, it has been introduced in the subjects of mother tongue and literature, mathematics, chemistry and biology. A test of these subjects is conducted and a national certificate is given to applicants who have achieved the required results.

In addition, although physics is scheduled to be introduced in 2022, since it is included in the priority subjects this year, certification in this subject is planned to be launched this year.

DTM has developed and sent proposals to the necessary organizations to remove restrictions on the choice of educational fields of applicants.

If this proposal is accepted and approved on the basis of a regulatory legal document, it can be implemented. For now, there is no change in this regard. Therefore, up to 5 choices are valid.

New test tasks in the form of maps, tables, and pictures without writing were used in the tests conducted in previous years. Will it be used this year as well?

Yes, of course. In order for the applicants to quickly understand the test tasks, to think and save time, this year there will be test tasks in history, geography, as well as English, German, French, biology and chemistry without writing in the form of maps, tables, and pictures. Last year, 3 hours were given for 2 subjects?

This temporary procedure was introduced last year in view of the pandemic conditions in our country. This year, the conditions are more stable than last year, therefore, according to the regulation approved by the decision of the Cabinet of Ministers dated April 3, 2018 No. 261, 1 hour will be allocated for every 30 test tasks.

Where and how will the tests be conducted?

According to the state test center, in this regard, work is being carried out on 2 different scenarios in cooperation with the Council of Ministers of the Republic of Karakalpakstan, the regions and the hokims of the city of Tashkent.

  1. The tests will be held in a traditional format. holding in capacity buildings.
  2. Conducting test tests in open areas like in 2020 under pandemic conditions.

"For this, test test locations were selected, plans and layouts were prepared. These include 96 large-capacity indoor buildings for 46,830 applicants, 65 stadiums and avenues for 59,040 applicants.

The list of large-capacity buildings and open spaces where tests will be held has been submitted to the State Commission.
