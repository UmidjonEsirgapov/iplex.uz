For applicants: how are questions distributed in majors?

Useful information for applicants and students:

Brief content:
In the entrance exams to higher education institutions, the specialty subject block tests are distributed according to subjects in exact percentages.
This information is important for the applicants to correctly guide their preparation and for strategic planning.
On specialty subjects distribution: 1.

In the entrance exams to higher education institutions, tests of the specialty subject block are distributed by topics in certain percentages. This information is important for proper orientation and strategic planning of preparation for applicants.

Distribution by specialization:

1. Native language and literature

  • Native language — 60%

  • Literature — 40%

2. Mathematics

  • Numbers and operations on them — 25%

  • Functions and algebraic expressions — 35%

  • Derivation, integral and their applications — 10%

  • Geometry — 30%

3. History

  • Political history — 50-60%

  • Socio-economic history — 10-20%

  • Cultural life — 10-15%

  • Practical assignments — 20-25%

4. Physics

  • Mechanics — 35%

  • Molecular physics and thermodynamics — 25%

  • Electromagnetism — 30%

  • Atomic and nuclear physics (optics, theory of relativity) — 10%

Why is this distribution important?

This distribution is for applicants:

  • Clearly defining the direction of preparation,

  • The correct distribution of time,

  • Enables achieving high results in tests.

For example, if functions and algebraic expressions have a share of 35% in mathematics, it is important to pay special attention to it. — Source
