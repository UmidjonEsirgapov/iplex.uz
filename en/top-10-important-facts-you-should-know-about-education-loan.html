TOP-10 important information about the education loan

The education loan is allocated under special conditions in order to ease the study expenses of students. Below you can familiarize yourself with the main terms and benefits of the education loan.

1. Preferential interest rates:

  • Educational loans are allocated to women and girls without interest.
  • Boys are provided based on the main rate of the Central Bank (13.5%).

2. Who is it intended for?

  • The loan is allocated to state and non-state high school students.
  • Only students studying in full-time education can use this opportunity.

3. Impact of grades:

  • If the grades of students above the second year are below the average of "3", they will be allocated educational credit in the amount of only 80% of the contract.

4. Dynamics of interest rates:

  • If the central bank's main rate decreases, the loan percentage will also decrease.
  • If the rate increases, the loan percentage will not change, that is, it will not be increased.

5. Available every year:

  • Students have the opportunity to receive educational loans every academic year.

6. There is no privilege for "Supercontract":

  • Students studying on the basis of "Supercontract" cannot use education credit.

7. Preferential payment period:

  • The loan repayment period starts 7 months after the end of official studies and is carried out for 7 years.
  • For example, if a loan is allocated to a 1st year student, he can pay it back in 11 years and 7 months.

8. Participants of the "Unified Register of Social Protection":

  • No pledge or guarantor is required for education loans for the children of families included in this register.

9. Online applications:

  • starting from the 2024/2025 academic year, applications for obtaining an educational loan:
    • "Educational loan" information system (talim-krediti.mf.uz)
    • my.gov.uz portals.

Conclusion:

Educational loan allows students to reduce the cost of education. You can reduce your education costs by taking advantage of the benefits and conditions. — Source
