SSV published a list of prohibited foods in school canteens

The latest news in the field of education is as follows:

Summary:
The Ministry of Health of the Republic of Uzbekistan (MoH) has published a list of food products that are prohibited for sale in school canteens. (hamburgers, cheeseburgers, hot dogs).
Confectionery products with artificial flavors and fragrances, dyes, flavorings.

The Ministry of Health of the Republic of Uzbekistan (MSH) has published a list of food products prohibited for sale in school canteens. This list is based on the recommendations of the World Health Organization (WHO) and aims to protect children's health.

Prohibited products include common products such as hamburgers, cheeseburgers, hot dogs, chips, sodas, and energy drinks. WHO recommends removing these types of products from the daily diet. According to the list published by the SSV, these products are prohibited from being sold in school canteens of Uzbekistan.

Based on the sanitary rules and hygiene regulations of the SSV, the following products cannot be prepared and sold in school canteens: — Official Data

  • Bread products with meat, mayonnaise, ketchup and vegetables (hamburgers, cheeseburgers, hot dogs).
  • Artificial flavors and aromas Confectionery with additives, dyes, flavorings.
  • Kirishki, "khrustyashki" with artificial preservatives, chips, worms, pistachios and home-made chewy sweets.
  • Colored and colorless carbonated drinks.
  • Meat, fish, poultry that have not passed veterinary control; as well as eggs that do not meet the necessary sanitary requirements.
  • Canned food that is swollen, rusty, or has no expiration date.
  • Creamy pastry products (cakes and pastries).
  • Raw smoked meat products and sausages, including boiled sausages.
  • Unheated milk and milk products, kimchi and other fermented milk products.
  • Rapidly fermented milk products. prepared foods based on dry food concentrates and other dangerous food products.
