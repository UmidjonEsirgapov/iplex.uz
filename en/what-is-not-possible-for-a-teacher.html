Teachers may expel students from class or hold them in the classroom when the break bell rings, and even on the school grounds. Smoking is not allowed.

Well, what else is not allowed?

This is clearly stated in the order of the Minister of Public Education No. 246 dated July 28, 2022, on the rules of internal order and etiquette of general secondary education institutions. — Details here

According to Clause 11, teachers are prohibited from:

  • Degrading the personality and dignity of team members and others;
  • Unreasonably expelling students from classes;
  • Keeping students in the classroom after the "exit" bell rings;
  • Conducting religious propaganda during classes;
  • Coming to the educational institution and classes without having consumed alcohol, not tidying up the hair and beard, and not observing the dress code;
  • consumption of alcohol and tobacco products in the territory and building of the educational institution;
  • Various activities unrelated to the education of students in the territory and building of the school (birthday, jubilee, iftar and other events related festive tables).
