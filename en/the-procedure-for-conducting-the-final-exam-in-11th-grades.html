1. Examination procedure for mother tongue and literature.

Students who have completed the 11th grade will have a certain level of competence in the educational program of the mother tongue and literature. In order to determine the knowledge, skills and qualifications of the students, the final exam will be held in the 11th grade in the 2023-2024 academic year. The topics of each essay will be the mother tongue and covering the subjects of 5-11 grades of general education schools in the field of literature. Students are presented with 3 topics in one ticket, and one of the students chooses one of these topics, and all students write an essay on this topic. 120 minutes will be allocated to write the essay and it will be written in a 12-page notebook prepared in advance by the school (with a school stamp).

2. The procedure for conducting an exam in mathematics.

In order to determine the knowledge, skills and qualifications of students, the final exam will be held in the 11th grade in the 2023-2024 academic year. The questions and tasks of each exam ticket cover the topics of the 10th-11th grades in mathematics. Also, the recommendation includes evaluation criteria for knowledge questions, application and reasoning tasks. Students are provided with 2 tickets consisting of the proposed base questions. The student is asked 10 questions (6 algebra, 4 geometry) in the ticket. 3 of the questions (2 algebra, 1 geometry) will be knowledge, 5 (3 algebra, 2 geometry) will be applied, and 2 (1 algebra, 1 geometry) will be reasoning. In order to write the answers to the questions, they will write them on the A4 format papers prepared by the school (with the school stamp). They will be given a total of 180 minutes to answer the ticket questions.

3. Examination procedure for the state language (for classes where the language of instruction is not Uzbek).

During the preparation of materials for the final state certification for the 11th grade, the subjects covered in the Uzbek language in the 10th-11th grades were taken as a basis. Each option consists of 3 questions. 1 of the questions is reading literacy, 1 is reasoning, and 1 is application. To write the answers to the written questions, they write on the A4-format papers prepared by the school in advance (with the school stamp printed on them). 120 minutes will be given according to the tasks given in the exam.

4. Foreign language exam procedure.

For 11th graders of secondary schools, the final control exam in foreign language is conducted to determine listening comprehension, reading comprehension, writing, and speaking skills. The exam is organized in a hybrid form. A total of 105 minutes will be allocated for the first part. Students are offered 2 pre-selected options for the written part of the exam. In the oral exam, the student chooses one assignment from 10 options, prepares for 10 minutes and speaks for 3 minutes about the question in his option.
