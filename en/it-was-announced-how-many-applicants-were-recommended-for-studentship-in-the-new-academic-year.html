It was announced how many applicants were recommended for studentship in the new academic year

Important news for students and applicants has been announced.

Brief content:

In the 2023-2024 academic year, 193,421 applicants were recommended for studentship. Of these, 42,655 were recommended for studentship on the basis of a state grant, and 150,766 on a fee-contract basis. This was announced at the press conference dedicated to the results of the tests for the new academic year.

Today, on August 23, at the press conference held by the Agency for the Assessment of Knowledge and Skills, information was provided on the results of the entrance tests for the bachelor's degree programs of higher education institutions for the 2023-2024 academic year. As it turned out, 888,660 of the 948,000 applicants who were scheduled to take part in the tests this year took part, while 59,831 did not. Of these, 42,655 were recommended for studentship on the basis of a state grant, and 150,766 on a fee-contract basis. Here:

- the average score on the basis of the state grant was 119.3 points;

- the average score on the basis of the payment-contract was 93.3 points.

If we look at the applicants recommended for studentship in the section of education forms, 129,927 applicants went to full-time education, 49 852 applicants were recommended for part-time education, 10,752 applicants for evening education, and 2,890 applicants for distance education. recommended within the parameters. Including:

− persons with disabilities - 3,500 people;

− real orphans who are graduates of the "Mehribanlik" House and Children's Town, SOS - graduates of children's neighborhoods and family orphanages, as well as orphans taken into guardianship, foster care or family upbringing (patronage) - 261 people;

− 4,200 people who have completed military service and have recommendations from the command of the military unit;

− children of military servicemen - 2,100 people;

− children of employees of internal affairs bodies - 720 people;

− women from needy families - 1,900 people;

- 351 women with at least 5 years of work experience. — Source
