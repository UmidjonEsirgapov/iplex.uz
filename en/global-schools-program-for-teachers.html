Global schools program for teachers

Useful information for applicants and students:

Global Schools Curriculum.

Global Schools Program Applications for the fourth cohort of the Global Schools Program are now open. Selected teachers will undergo a month-long training program on the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and Education for Sustainable Development (ESD). The training program will be conducted online, with teachers having the opportunity to interact with a global team of teachers.

During the comprehensive training program, teachers will learn about the Sustainable Development Goals and will be introduced to action-based pedagogy and ideas for integrating ESD, Global Citizenship Education, and 21st Century Skills into classrooms. Upon successful completion of the training, teachers will earn the title of Global Schools Advocate, as well as receive the Global Schools Advocate Toolkit and begin their journey to integrate ESD into schools. They need to inspire their colleagues and school leaders to work towards the SDGs, as well as to integrate sustainable development into their curricula. Advocates serve as a driving force within their school community to ensure the implementation and acceptance of sustainable development into school curriculum and practices.

Benefits of participation:

-Free online training developed by the Global Schools team to increase sustainability literacy and provide teachers with new, proactive strategies to implement in the classroom;

-Teachers Monthly online seminars and talks on sustainable development to connect with other education leaders and SDG Advocates;

-Access to a network of like-minded teachers working to implement activities and lessons on global issues in their classrooms;

-To share their advocacy work at the International Conference on Sustainable Development, Vatican Youth Symposium, ECOSOC Youth Forum and Practical Guide for Global Schools teacher participation opportunities and participation in conferences can be selected;

-Mentoring program with an experienced teacher who has already completed the Global Schools Advocate Program;

-Official certificate of completion.

Requirements:

-Applicants are primary or secondary school teachers worldwide (UN Volunteers, Fulbright, etc. teachers who work through volunteering programs can also participate).

-Candidates must have a high level of English to complete their lawyer training and communicate with the global network.

-Willingness to devote at least 5 hours per week to this role. Global schools do not require you to follow a prescribed curriculum. 5-10 hours per week should be spent studying, discussing with other teachers, experimenting with integrating sustainability into your lesson plans, and creating a movement for sustainability in your school.

-Enthusiasm for the Sustainable Development Goals and the mission of the Paris Climate Agreement. No SDG experience required!

-A passion for learning and a willingness to join a global community of teachers.

-Ability to inspire others and a willingness to encourage other teachers and educators in your school to join your ESD journey!
