Instructions for the regional stage of the 2024 Science Olympiad

On November 4-9, 2024, among schoolchildren of the Republic of Uzbekistan, in the fields of mathematics, physics, chemistry, biology, informatics, and information technology. Olympiads will be held at the regional stage. This instruction was developed to manage the processes of organizing, evaluating and calculating the results of the Olympiad.

The purpose of the Olympiad instruction

The Olympiad is organized in accordance with the order of the Ministry of Pre-school and School Education of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. 331 of October 11, 2024. This stage is aimed at determining the level of knowledge of schoolchildren in subjects and selecting the most talented young people.

Format of the Olympiad in mathematics, physics, chemistry and biology

Olympiad in mathematics, physics, chemistry and biology will be held in two rounds. Each participant must complete the following tasks:

  • Written tasks: Participants in each subject are given 5 written tasks of different levels of difficulty. These tasks are evaluated in the following order:

    • Task 1 and 2: 7 points each
    • Task 3 and 4: 10 points each
    • Task 5: 16 points Written tasks are evaluated with a total of 50 points and are allotted 180 minutes.

  • Test items: The test items are divided into three types:

    • The first 10 easy-level closed tests, valued at 0.9 points each.
    • The next 10 medium-level closed tests, valued at 1.5 points each.
    • The last 10 open-ended tests, each It is rated with 2.6 points. In this way, a total of 50 points can be scored on the tests. 90 minutes will be allocated for completing the test tasks.

Participants in mathematics, physics, chemistry and biology can collect 100 points from both rounds.

Format of the Olympiad in informatics and information technologies

The Olympiad in informatics and information technologies will be held in only one round. Participants must meet the following requirements:

  • Problem Solutions: 5 problem solutions will be provided on the online portal. Problems are graded with 7, 7, 10, 10 and 16 points depending on the level of difficulty. Participants can score a total of 50 points.
  • They have 180 minutes to solve the problems. Program codes are required to be written in C++, Python, or Java only. Each question is automatically checked in the system and the results are calculated.

How are the scores equal?

The 1st, 2nd and 3rd places are determined according to the scores of the participants in each subject and class section. If several participants score equal points, the winner will be determined based on the following criteria:

  • The participant who has scored more points in the written work will have an advantage.
  • If there is a tie in the written work, the student who has scored more points in the complex tasks will be the winner.

If there are equal scores in IT and IT, the system will automatically give preference to the participant who worked quickest, that is, the time spent working on the problems is also taken into account.
