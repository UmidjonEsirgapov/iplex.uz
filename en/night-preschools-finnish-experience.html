Preschools are important in many countries, but in Finland they are special and unique. In this article, you will find detailed information about night preschools in Finland.

Preschools in Finland: General Information

Preschools in Finland accept children from 9 months to 6 years old. The Finnish education system is world-renowned for its high-quality education, but there are no uniform standards for preschool education. Each institution creates its own curriculum.

Daily Routine and Learning Process

The daily life of children in Finnish preschools is enriched with various activities. These activities include:

  • Daily Walks and Outings: Kindergartens take children outside every day. These walks and visits to various cultural institutions are an integral part of the lessons.
  • Group Size and Teachers: Groups usually consist of 12 to 20 children of various ages. One teacher can handle a maximum of 4 children, which is set by law.

Working Hours and Night Working

The working hours of preschools in Finland are usually from 06:30 to 17:00. However, parents have the right to leave their children for 4-5 hours. In addition, some kindergartens work at night. These institutions are especially designed for situations where parents have to travel on business or work at night.

Compulsory Kindergarten Attendance

Kindergarten attendance is not compulsory in Finland. A third of preschoolers do not attend these institutions. In large settlements, there are often not enough places for kindergartens, so parents who raise a child at home are paid an allowance of 500 euros.

Conclusion

The preschool education system in Finland strives to create quality and flexible conditions for children. Night-time institutions are an important part of this system and serve to meet the various needs of parents. This system can be an interesting experience for other countries. — Official Data
