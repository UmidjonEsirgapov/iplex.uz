What is a Schengen visa, its types and possibilities

In order to ensure internal security and facilitate travel, several European countries have increased control at the Schengen external borders, and all member states follow a single visa policy system.

Schengen member states:

Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Croatia, the Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Greece, the Netherlands, Iceland, Italy, Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Hungary, Malta, Germany, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland.

You can travel in the entire Schengen area with a visa obtained from any of these countries.

Where to apply for a Schengen visa?

- If you are traveling to one of the Schengen member states, you should submit the application to the diplomatic mission (embassy) of this member state. - If you need to visit several member states during the trip, the application should be submitted to the diplomatic mission of the main destination member state (the country of long-term stay). apply?

The type of visa you need depends on the duration and purpose of your visit. A visa can be issued for one, two or more entries. The decision on the validity period of the visa, the period of stay and the number of entries is made by the diplomatic mission in accordance with the Schengen rules.

Visa types:

Airport transit visa (A visa):

This visa is intended for passing through the international zone of the airport without entering the Schengen area. Valid for 24 hours.

Short-term visa (C visa) :

Allows you to travel to Schengen countries for up to 90 days in any 180 days.

National long-term visa (D visa) :

Study, work or stay in the Schengen country for more than 90 days given to those who intend to live. Validity and terms may vary depending on the country of issue. — Source
