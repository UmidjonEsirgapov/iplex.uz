"High quality, innovative approach, free choice" - about the Swiss education system

Swiss education system is one of the most advanced education systems in the world is considered It is known for its high quality education, innovative approach and freedom of choice to students.

Swiss education system aims to give students freedom of choice. Students can choose the fields of study that suit their interests and abilities.

Compulsory education

The total duration of compulsory education is eleven years does. The primary stage includes, among other things, two years of kindergarten or the first school year (eight years). Lower secondary education lasts three years. Compulsory education for children applies from the age of four.

State compulsory education is free for all children. 95% of children attend state institutions. The obligation to attend school also applies to children who do not have legal residence status. Students with similar developmental statuses, abilities, social backgrounds, linguistics, and behavioral characteristics attend the same school. Diversity of students' abilities and talents, cultural diversity in classrooms creates serious problems for the school.

The school guarantees that all students are taught according to their abilities. Pupils receive differentiated, individualized education and are supported by regular school teachers or specialists (eg special needs teachers) as needed. In a normal school, it includes support for gifted students, development of language skills, development of language skills of native speakers of foreign languages, assistance in individual subjects, speech and language therapy, psychomotor therapy, etc.

Education after compulsory education

After compulsory education teenagers enter upper secondary education and can choose between general education programs (baccalaureate schools or upper secondary special schools) and vocational education programs.

Most teenagers enter vocational training after upper secondary education. Adolescents graduate from high school at the age of 18-19 and receive an appropriate certificate. More than 90% of young people complete the upper secondary education program.

Various measures (individual support, case management, etc.) have been implemented to optimize the transition from compulsory education to upper secondary education. Yearly courses are available as an intermediate solution for teenagers who do not go straight to secondary school after compulsory education.

Higher education

Swiss higher education system in universities or vocational educational institutions offer a wide range of programs that can be completed.

Universities offer a variety of academic or practice-oriented degree programs. University admission requires a bachelor's degree, a federal vocational bachelor's degree, a specialized bachelor's degree, or an additional exam, depending on the institution.

There are twelve recognized universities in Switzerland: ten cantonal (state) universities and two Federal Institutes of Technology (FIT). These are traditional academic universities that primarily conduct basic research, unlike universities of applied sciences.

This system focuses on preparing students for life and gives them the opportunity to choose fields of study that match their interests and abilities.
