The Gilbert and Marcia Kotzen Scholars Program at Simon University in the USA : The Gilbert and Marcia Kotzen Scholars Program is aimed at financial support for young people with high potential who plan to study at Simmons University. The grant is for 4 years study and is valid for all undergraduate programs.

Benefits of the grant:

  • The contract fee is fully covered;
  • Accommodation is provided;
  • An annual stipend of $3,000 USD;

The main grant requirements:

  • Must have been accepted to Simon University for Level 1 (Apply here);
  • ACT 28 or SAT 1300 required;
  • Non-native English applicants must have TOEFL or IELTS scores (Min: 5.5-6.0);
  • School/High School/College must have excellent grades (GPA 3.3 or higher);
  • Must have good math knowledge and skills;

Candidates are required to write 2 essays:

  • Answer a short question (no more than 250 words): What would you spend the extra $3,000 on? (to study abroad, to various courses, for practice programs, research, etc.)?;
  • Academic essay ("Some people argue that leaders are born, not made, while others defend the opposite view, namely that leaders are "made." Write an essay in which you defend one view or the other" They must write an essay on this topic not exceeding 500 words);

Program admission process and required documents: Send the above mentioned 2 essays and your CV (various trainings and achievements) in PDF or Word doc format to this email address under the subject "Kotzen Application". Each page of the documents should contain the student's full name and date of birth

Note: All documents must be sent by December 1. The finalists will be announced in the middle of January, and they will be interviewed in February, and the final results will be announced by March 1. Read more about this grant on the university's official page, as well as additional questions (617) 521-2000 .
