Changes have been made to the procedure for admission to joint education programs

The latest news in the field of education is as follows:

Brief content:
From January 1, 2022, the procedure for examination of joint educational programs by the Ministry of Higher and Secondary Special Education will be introduced, and it will be allowed to organize educational activities only on the basis of joint educational programs with a positive expert opinion.
Foreign language Applicants who have at least B1 level in the bachelor's degree, and at least B2 level in the master's degree and who have a national and equivalent international certificate of the same level are exempted from the exam held in a foreign language in the second stage and they are given the maximum score for this stage.

The Cabinet of Ministers approved the regulation on the procedure for organizing educational activities on the basis of joint educational programs of the Republic of Uzbekistan and foreign partner higher education organizations.

From January 1, 2022, the procedure for examination of joint educational programs by the Ministry of Higher and Secondary Special Education will be introduced, and only a positive expert opinion will be introduced. It is allowed to organize educational activities on the basis of joint educational programs.

On the basis of joint educational programs, starting from the 2021/2022 academic year, admission to study will be carried out in two stages:

In the first stage, entrance exams will be held in accordance with the admission procedure developed in agreement with the partner higher education organization;

In the second stage, the higher education of Uzbekistan will be held. on the basis of the organization's order, an entrance exam for a foreign language taught in a joint educational program will be held by the State Test Center in the agreed terms.

In this case:

  • applicants who have scored 30% of the maximum possible points in the entrance exams in the first stage will have the right to participate in the exam held in the second stage;
  • foreign language in the second stage Applicants who have scored at least 30% of the maximum possible points in the entrance exams will participate in the competition held under the joint education program and will be admitted to study based on the admission parameters. Applicants who have a foreign language certificate of at least B1 level in the bachelor's degree, and at least B2 level in the master's degree in a national and equivalent international certificate are exempted from the foreign language exam in the second stage and they are given the maximum score for this stage.

    Graduates who have successfully completed higher education institutions on the basis of joint educational programs will be presented with a diploma recognized in the country where the foreign partner higher education institution is located and modeled after the state model of the higher education institution of the Republic of Uzbekistan. — Source
