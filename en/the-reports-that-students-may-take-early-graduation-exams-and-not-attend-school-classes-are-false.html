The reports that students may take early graduation exams and not attend school classes are false

About the expected changes in the education system:

Brief content:
A message spread on social networks that "Now schoolchildren can take graduation exams in advance and not attend school classes."
The Ministry of Preschool and School Education announced that this is a fake. The message regarding the application of external exams by citizens who have not completed school, that is, those who have passed school age, was misinterpreted. In accordance with the Law "On Education", in order to ensure the right of citizens to general secondary education, only citizens over the age of 18 who could not complete general secondary education on time due to various reasons, more precisely, citizens over 18 years of age, participate in external exams.

The news spread on social networks that "Now schoolchildren can take graduation exams in advance and not attend school classes." The Ministry of Pre-school and School Education announced that this was a fake.

It was reported that the message about citizens who could not finish school on time, i.e., citizens who have passed the school age, apply for an external exam, was misinterpreted. Only citizens over 18 years of age who could not complete general secondary education on time for various reasons, or more precisely, those over 18 years of age, who did not complete school, participate in the exams.

External exams have nothing to do with students studying at school and are not applied to them," the ministry said. — Official Data
