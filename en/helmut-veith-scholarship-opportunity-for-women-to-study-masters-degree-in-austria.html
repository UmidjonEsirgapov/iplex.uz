Helmut Veith Scholarship: Opportunity for women to study master's degree in Austria

The purpose of the grant is to encourage women to study computer science.

Helmut Veith Master's Scholarship is for women who wish to pursue a master's degree in computer science. The goal of the scholarship is to encourage women to study computer science.

The Helmut Veith Master's Scholarship is dedicated to the memory of a distinguished scientist who worked in the fields of logic in computer science, software development and computer security. Helmut Veit was an advocate and mentor for women in computer and information science.

This scholarship is awarded to female students who are enrolled or intend to enroll in one of the computer science master's programs at TU Wien (Vienna University of Technology) taught in English. You can apply for a scholarship before or after applying for university, but only students who have been unconditionally admitted to a master's degree can receive a scholarship.

Amount of the scholarship. €6,000 per year (paid in installments) plus tuition fees. The scholarship can be paid for 2 years.

Requirements:

  • Women of all nationalities can apply for the scholarship;
  • Bachelor degree in computer science, informatics or mathematics is required;
  • Knowledge and interest in the following areas (at least one) is required: logic, formal methods and verification in computer science, computer security, algorithms and complexity theory, databases and knowledge presentation, artificial intelligence, software engineering;
  • perfect knowledge of English is required.

How can I get a scholarship? The application is sent by e-mail and consists of the following documents:

  • A cover letter describing the type of scholarship the student is applying for (Helmut Veith Stipend) and other aspects that the student considers relevant to the application; certificate of completion with seal and stamp;
  • Contact information of 2 recommenders;
  • Language certificate of at least B2 level;
  • Copy of passport;

A certified translation is required if the language of the documents is not English or German .
