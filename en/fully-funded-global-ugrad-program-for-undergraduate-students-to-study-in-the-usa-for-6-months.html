General Information:

Global UGRAD

The Global Undergraduate Exchange Program (Global UGRAD) mainly sends 2nd and 3rd year undergraduate students to a US college or university for 1 semester provides an opportunity to receive academic education. The program is sponsored by the US Department of State. Selected candidates will have the opportunity to get to know American society, culture and academic institutions in depth, as well as to improve their professional skills. In addition, there will be an in-person workshop on arrival orientation and program completion.

Note: The Global UGRAD program does not assist participants in credit transfers between their host and American institutions. Any credit transfers are the sole responsibility of the participants.

All academic majors are available. Under J-1 visa regulations, applicants interested in medicine, nursing, veterinary medicine, pharmacology, or other clinical fields of study must note that direct patient care, including animal care, is prohibited and therefore must focus on academic course and/or program activities when participating. US institutions may be limited.

Note: Students cannot choose the host institution and will be placed at the most appropriate host institution based on the student's academic interests.

Program Goals:

  • To promote mutual understanding between the people of the United States and other countries.
  • The Global UGRAD program is for students from different countries. provide a comprehensive exchange experience to develop academic, intercultural and leadership skills for students;
  • Enhance students' academic knowledge and professional skills necessary to achieve their long-term academic and future career goals;
  • Develop students' comprehensive and nuanced understanding of the US;
  • Create opportunities for students to establish social networks with US host institutions and local communities;

Important: Global UGRAD is a fully funded exchange program that includes a monthly stipend for students travel/arrival, university tuition, room and board, medical insurance, required books and incidental expenses. Participants live in campus housing with their American peers and are required to participate in twenty hours of community service.

Eligibility requirements:

The Global UGRAD exchange program selection is merit-based and eligible for students who meet the following requirements:

  • At least 18 years old at the start of the program;
  • Resident of Uzbekistan and Citizens of Uzbekistan who are studying;
  • Those who are currently studying in the 1st, 2nd, 3rd stages of full-time undergraduate programs of any accredited university in Uzbekistan;
  • Have completed at least one semester before starting the Global UGRAD program;
  • At the end of the Global UGRAD program, they must return to their institution and resume their studies for at least one semester or its equivalent (i.e. 4th grade students cannot apply);
  • Young people who can demonstrate leadership potential through academic work, community involvement, and extracurricular activities;
  • Proficient in written and spoken English is required. Finalists and alternates are required to take the TOEFL iBT test and obtain a minimum score of 48. The cost of the TOEFL is covered by the program;
  • Able to begin studies in the US in August 2022 (fall semester) or January 2023 (spring semester);
  • Eligible to obtain and maintain a US student exchange visa (J-1) required for the program;
  • Must return directly to their home country after completing the program;

Note: Preference is given to candidates with little or no experience in the US or outside their home country. Applicants who have participated in an exchange visitor program sponsored or funded by the US government for more than six weeks are not eligible to apply.

Persons in the following situations are not eligible to participate in the Global UGRAD program:

  • US citizens and permanent residents of the United States;
  • Currently studying, living or working outside of Uzbekistan individuals;
  • graduates of the Global UGRAD exchange program;
  • local employees of US missions abroad working for the US Department of State and/or the US Agency for International Development (USAID); employees are also ineligible for one year after termination;
  • Immediate family members (ie, spouses and children) of US State Department and USAID employees. Family members also do not have the right to submit for one year after the employee's dismissal;
