DAAD scholarships: the opportunity to study in Germany on the basis of a full grant for master's and doctoral studies

About the expected changes in the education system:

Brief contents:
Requirements: - To have completed a four-year bachelor's degree with high marks within six years in the field related to the field you want to study;- To have at least two years of work experience;- To have at least a B1 certificate for studying in German takes a six-month German language course and takes an exam);- To study in English, you need to have at least 6 IELTS or minimum TOEFL scores (550 paper-based, 213 computer-based, 80 internet-based). information will be on the university website).
If you have additional questions about submitting documents, you can contact and ask them through the university website.

About the program: The DAAD program, which is funded by the German government, annually offers full grants to international students in various fields to study Masters or PhD degrees in Germany

For whom: Those who have completed their bachelor's degree no more than six years ago and have at least two years of work experience.

Grant amount: Tuition is free; 961 euros for master's degree, 1000 euros monthly scholarship for doctoral studies; medical insurance and travel expenses will be covered.

Requirements:

- Not more than six years after completing a four-year bachelor’s degree with high marks in the field you want to study; takes a six-month German language course and takes an exam);
- To study in English, you need to have at least 6 IELTS or TOEFL scores (550 paper-based, 213 computer-based, 80 internet-based).

IMPORTANT: You can apply for three courses at the same time. Documents must be submitted directly to universities participating in the DAAD program. The list of universities and fields participating in the DAAD grant: HERE (for the 2022-2023 academic year)

Application procedure

Enter through the link given above and choose the field and university that suits you. You should go to the university website via the link attached to the field name and submit the following documents directly to the university (details on where and how to submit documents are available on the university website).

- Completed and signed DAAD application;
- Signed CV (must be in Europass format: http://europass.cedefop.europa.eu/);
- Objectives reflected and signed; maximum two-page essay (Letter of Motivation);

IMPORTANT: If you are applying to more than one course (you can apply to a maximum of three programs), in this one essay you should explain in order why you chose these courses and which of these courses you prefer.

- Letter of recommendation from your supervisor. The recommendation must be written on a special form of the workplace and have a signature and seal;
- Copy of the work record confirming that you have worked for at least two years (with a notarized translation);
- Documents confirming your knowledge of the language (German or English);
- Copies of the bachelor's degree and grade sheet (with a notarized translation).

Note: Some universities add may request information or documents. You can find detailed information about this on their website.

Deadline for submitting documents

The date of submitting documents for the 2022-2023 academic year may vary depending on the university. You can see the complete list by deadlines HERE.

Selection process

1. You submit all documents through the university website before the deadline.
2. The selection committee recommends potential candidates for the DAAD grant.
3. The DAAD panel will contact potential candidates and ask them to upload their documents to the DAAD website.
4. The documents will be reviewed again and the final results will be communicated to the candidates selected for the grant.

IT IS IMPORTANT: Please keep a copy of the documents prepared by the university (PDF files) so that you can resubmit the documents to DAAD if you are selected as a potential candidate.
