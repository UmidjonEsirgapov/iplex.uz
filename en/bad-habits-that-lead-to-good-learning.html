Bad Habits That Lead to Good Learning

Important news for higher education institutions:

Summary:
Everyone's learning style is different, but some bad habits can interfere with studying for everyone.
Tip: Put your phone on Do Not Disturb mode or put it in another room while studying.
Tip: When you read, try to read more slowly and with more focus.

Everyone learns differently, but some bad habits can hinder reading for everyone. Effective study can be achieved by identifying and breaking these habits. Below we will provide information about the main bad habits that will hinder you.

1. Having Distractions

The phone, TV or other electronic devices can always be a distraction. Constantly checking your phone or leaving the TV on in the background will prevent you from concentrating on studying.

Tip:

  • When studying, put your phone on Do Not Disturb mode or leave it in another room.
  • Try to make the study environment quiet and quiet.

2. Procrastination

Putting important information off until the last minute can have a negative impact on productivity. It is very difficult and rarely successful to absorb information in one day.

Tip:

  • Use a strategy of breaking it down into small parts and learning little by little each day.
  • Create and stick to a study routine for a certain amount of time each day.

3. Failure to Focus on Time Allocation

Failure to allocate time effectively and plan ahead for what to study at what time leads to chaos. This makes the study process difficult and reduces efficiency.

Advice:

  • Make a clear study schedule for each day.
  • Try to balance study and rest time.

4. Too Fast Reading

Reading texts too fast can hinder memorization and full understanding of the meaning.

Tip:

  • Try to read more slowly and carefully when reading.
  • Note or highlight important places as a reminder.

5. Avoid Mastering Complex Sections

Skip the complex topics and study only the easier ones will harm the overall knowledge level. This leads to unpreparedness for difficult questions that are important during the exam. — Source

Tip:

  • Study difficult topics slowly and more carefully.
  • Ask for additional resources and help for difficult sections.
