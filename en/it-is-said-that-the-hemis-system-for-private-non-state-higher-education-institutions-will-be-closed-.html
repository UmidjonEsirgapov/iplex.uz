Will the Hemis system be closed for private non-state higher education institutions?

Recently, news has been circulating: Starting from October 1, the Hemis system may be closed for private non-state universities. This directly affects student admission processes.

What will happen if the Hemis system is closed?

If the system is closed, private HEIs:

  • Can not accept new applicants;

  • Register transfer students as well cannot;

  • Because student rights and documents are not registered in the electronic database, problems arise in the educational process.

Consequences for students and applicants

For applicants

  • It will not be possible to apply to private universities;

  • Only the opportunity to study through public universities will remain.

For students

  • Internal and external transfer processes will stop;

  • The opportunity to move to non-state universities will disappear.

Impact on private universities

Closing the Hemis system is a big loss for private HEIs. In universities that cannot accept students:

  • Financial problems will appear;

  • Educational processes may be reduced;

  • Some HEIs will be in danger of closing.
