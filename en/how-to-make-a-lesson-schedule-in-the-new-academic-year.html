How to make a lesson schedule in the new academic year?

With the beginning of the new academic year, it is important for schools to make a correct and effective timetable. The correct preparation of the lesson schedule helps to ensure the effective study of students, to make it easier for them to learn subjects, and to balance the load of teachers. In this article, we will consider which subjects should be placed in which order when creating a lesson schedule.

Guide to Creating a Lesson Schedule

When creating a lesson schedule, it is recommended to take into account the degree of complexity of the subjects. The subjects are listed below in order of difficulty:

  1. Mathematics and Computer Science: Mathematics and computer science are considered difficult subjects for students. Placing these subjects at the beginning of the lesson schedule helps students to use their attention and energy more efficiently in the morning.

  2. Mother Tongue and Literature, Foreign Languages: Mother tongue, literature and foreign languages ​​(Russian, English, French, German) take the second place in terms of complexity. Placing these subjects at the beginning of the curriculum helps to strengthen students' knowledge and develop language skills.

  3. Physics, Chemistry, Astronomy: These natural sciences take the third place in terms of complexity. It is recommended to be placed in the morning, when students' mental abilities are at their highest.

  4. Basics of History, Law, Economic Knowledge: These subjects are in the fourth place in terms of complexity. These subjects require students' understanding and analysis skills, so they can also be placed in the middle of the lesson schedule.

  5. Natural Sciences, Geography, Biology: Natural sciences (biology), geography are subjects of medium complexity that can arouse students' interests. It is advisable to place them in the middle of the lesson schedule.

  6. Basics of Education and Spirituality: These subjects are aimed at the moral and cultural development of students, are average in terms of complexity, and can be placed in the middle of the lesson schedule.

  7. Physical Education: Physical education lesson putting it at the end of the schedule allows students to do physical exercises after classes. It is useful to increase the physical activity of students and prevent fatigue.

  8. Technology: Technology subjects require practical training. Placing them in the middle of the lesson schedule will help students learn practical skills.

  9. Drawing: It is appropriate to place the subject of drawing in the last part of the lesson schedule. It helps to develop students' creative abilities.

  10. Fine Arts: Placing fine arts subjects at the end of the curriculum allows students to have a creative approach and engage in art.

  11. Music: Music is aimed at developing students' musical abilities, making it a part of the curriculum. putting it at the end helps to increase the creative potential of students.

Conclusion

Taking into account the level of complexity of subjects when drawing up a lesson schedule helps to make the process of students' learning more effective. This procedure will help students to use their attention effectively and make it easier for them to study. — Details here
