Here's what you need to know about your GPA

About the expected changes in the education system:

Brief content:
GPA (Grade Point Average) is an indicator that shows the level of mastery of subjects by the average score of students.
This system works on a scale of 1-4 and is based on diploma grades.
GPA is calculated based on the following formula: GPA = (K1×U1 + K2×U2 + ... + Kn×Un) ÷ (K1 + K2 + ... + Kn) K is the amount of credits allocated to each subject/module. This system works on a 1–4 scale and is based on degree grades.

Why is GPA important?

  1. Evaluation of study: GPA is a clear reflection of your academic performance.
  2. Admission to foreign universities: GPA is often the main requirement for continuing studies at the undergraduate or graduate level.
  3. Grants and financial aid get: Higher GPA in universities increases the possibility of receiving scholarships and other financial support.

How is GPA calculated?

GPA is calculated based on the following formula:

GPA = (K1×U1 + K2×U2 + ... + Kn×Un) ÷ (K1 + K2 + ... + Kn)

  • K is allocated to each subject/module number of credits.
  • U is the grade earned by the student in each subject/module.

For example, if you studied 3 subjects, you can find the GPA by multiplying the credits and grades and dividing their sum by the total credits.
