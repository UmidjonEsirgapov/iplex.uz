If the day the salary is paid falls on a holiday, delay is not allowed

According to Article 253 of the Labor Code, if the day the salary is paid falls on a day off or a non-working holiday, the employer must pay salaries before these days. This rule was introduced to ensure timely financial support of employees.

If payment of salary is delayed, the employer is required to pay compensation for each day of delay based on Central Bank refinancing rate. According to the current legislation, this amount of compensation is based on the refinancing rate of the central bank and is 10% of it.
