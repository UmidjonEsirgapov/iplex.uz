At the time of the adoption of the legislation on the provision of educational loans, there was no such thing as an additional contract. Therefore, this issue is not clearly specified in the regulatory documents.

According to the decision of the Cabinet of Ministers No. 318 of July 26, 2001, educational loans can be granted by commercial banks for the study of students admitted to full-time departments of higher education institutions on a fee-contract basis.

The word "admitted" is important here. In the additional contract, 100% payment will be made first, after which a decision will be made to accept those applicants by October. Until they make the payment, they are not considered to be accepted as students.

Accordingly, the provision of educational loans for the super-contract is not defined in the current legislation.
