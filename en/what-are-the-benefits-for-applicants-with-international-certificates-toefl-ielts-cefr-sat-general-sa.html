What are the benefits for applicants with international certificates (TOEFL, IELTS, CEFR, SAT General, SAT Subject, etc.)?

Today, the most relevant news in the field of education was:

Brief content:
According to the decision of the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan "On measures to implement certificates of national and international assessment systems for admission to higher education institutions" dated May 13, 2019 No. 395, tests (professional (creative) exam) to applicants who have applied for undergraduate courses in which a foreign language (English, German, French, Spanish, Turkish, Arabic, Persian, Dari, Hindi, Urdu, Chinese, Korean, Uyghur, Italian, Japanese) (hereinafter referred to as a foreign language) is included as the first (main) subject, and who have international certificates such as TOEFL, IELTS, CEFR, SAT General, SAT Subject, etc. the maximum score will be given.
starting from the 2020/2021 academic year, applicants who have applied for all undergraduate education courses in which foreign language is included in the set of tests (professional (creative) exam) and have the following levels of national or international certificate will be given the maximum score in this subject without exams: - for English - national certificate or International English Language Testing System (IELTS 5.5 (for Tashkent State Law University) 5.5 points from the writing module)), Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL IBT 72), Cambridge Assessment English FCE international language proficiency levels B2 and above; - for Japanese language - national certificate or Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT No. 2) language proficiency levels B2 and above according to international exam systems; - for German language - national certificate or Goethe-Zertifikat, Deutsches Sprachdiplom (DSD), Test Deutsch als Fremdsprache (Test DAF) language proficiency levels B2 and higher according to international examination systems; - for French - national certificate or Diplôme d'Etudes en Langue Française (DELF), language proficiency levels B2 and higher according to international examination systems Test de Connaissance du Française (TCF4); - for other languages - language proficiency levels B2 and higher according to national certificate or international examination systems. the privilege of the certificate is determined to be preserved during the period of validity of these certificates.

In accordance with the decision of the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan "On measures to implement national and international evaluation system certificates for admission to higher education institutions" dated May 13, 2019 No. 395, a foreign language (English, German, French, Spanish, Turkish, Arabic, Persian, Dari, Hindi, Urdu, Chinese, Korean, Uyghur, Italian, Japanese languages) (hereinafter referred to as foreign languages) applied to undergraduate courses of study as the first (main) subject, applicants who have international certificates of TOEFL, IELTS, CEFR, SAT General, SAT Subject, etc. points will be awarded.

Starting from the 2020/2021 academic year, applicants who have applied for all undergraduate education courses in which a foreign language is included in the set of tests (professional (creative) exam) and who have the following levels of national or international certificates will be awarded the maximum score for this subject without exams:

- for English — national certificate or International English Language Testing System (IELTS 5.5 (including 5.5 points from the writing module for Tashkent State Law University)), Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL IBT 72), Cambridge Assessment English FCE language proficiency levels B2 and higher; - for Japanese — national certificate or Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT No. 2) language proficiency levels B2 and above;- for German language — national certificate or Goethe-Zertifikat, Deutsches Sprachdiplom (DSD), Test Deutsch als Fremdsprache (Test DAF) language proficiency levels B2 and above;- for French language — national certificate or Diplôme d’Etudes en Langue Française (DELF), Test de Connaissance du Française (TCF4) language proficiency levels B2 and higher; - for other languages — national certificate or language proficiency levels B2 and higher according to international examination systems.

  • Until 2019, the privilege of the national certificate issued by the State Test Center is determined to be preserved during the validity period of these certificates.
