If the student cannot continue his studies at the higher education institution, will the contract money be returned?

About the expected changes in the education system:

Brief content:
If a student cannot continue his studies at a higher education institution (HEI), there is a possibility of partial refund of the paid contract money.
Relevant articles of the Civil Code of the Republic of Uzbekistan explain this situation as follows: According to Article 703 of the legislation, the HEI and An educational contract between a student is a service contract for a fee.
Article 705 stipulates that if the executive (university) fails to provide the service, the customer will return the remaining part of the contract sum after deducting the costs incurred for the service. if he is unable to continue his studies at a higher education institution, there is a possibility to recover part of the paid contract money. The relevant articles of the Civil Code of the Republic of Uzbekistan explain this situation as follows:

Legislative grounds

  1. Article 703, the educational contract between the higher education institution and the student is a service contract for a fee. The student, as a customer, enters into a contract to receive educational services and pays the contract money.

  2. Article 707 stipulates that the customer (student) has the right to cancel the service contract upon full payment of the service price. In this way, the student can request to cancel the contract.

  3. Article 705 provides that if the executive (university) is unable to provide the service, the customer will return the remaining part of the contract sum after deducting the costs incurred for the service. if he does, the university may deduct the expenses and return the remaining funds. When the contract is canceled, the following can be deducted:

    • Expenses spent on education;
    • Other direct costs arising from the provision of services by the university.
