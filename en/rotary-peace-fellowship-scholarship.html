Rotary Peace Fellowship

Each year, Rotary selects up to 130 peace and development leaders from around the world who are committed to peace and conflict resolution and receive a fully funded academic scholarship to pursue a prevention-related professional development certificate program or master's degree.

Countries: USA, Japan, UK, Australia, Sweden, Thailand, Uganda.

Host Institution(s):

• Duke University and University of North Carolina Chapel Hill, USA: Master's Program in International Development Policy (MIDP) or various degrees offered by the University of North Carolina;

• International Christian University, Japan: Master of Peace Studies;

• University of Bradford, England: Advanced Practice MA in Peacebuilding and Conflict Resolution;

• University of Queensland, Australia: Master of Peace and Conflict Studies (MPACS);

• Uppsala University, Sweden: Peace and Conflict study (Masters);

• Chulalongkorn University, Thailand (Certificate Program): Professional Development Certificate (PgCert) in Peace and Development Studies;

• Makerere University, Uganda (Certificate Program).

Degree/Field of Study: Professional Development Certificate Program or Masters Program in areas related to peace and conflict resolution and prevention.

Target Group: Anyone approved by their local Rotary District.

Number of Grants: Up to 50 graduate and 80 certificate scholarships are awarded each year.

Grant Value/Extras: Grants cover tuition, room and board, transportation, and all internship and other study expenses. covers.

Eligibility: The Rotary Peace Fellowship is designed for professionals with work experience in international relations or peace and conflict prevention and resolution. Applicants must also meet the following requirements:

• Three years of relevant work experience for the Master's program;
• Five years of relevant work experience for the certificate program and be able to explain how their plan to promote peace aligns with Rotary's mission (Candidates to Makerere University must be from Africa, have worked in Africa or worked with African communities or initiatives);
• Good English knowledge;
• Possess a bachelor's degree;
• Demonstrated leadership skills.

Instructions: The deadline for candidates to apply to their Rotary District is May 15, 2022. Regions must submit approved applications to The Rotary Foundation by July 1, 2022.
