"FTxSDG Challenge" international startup competition with $500,000 funding

Important news for students and applicants has been announced.

Summary:
The international startup competition "FTxSDG Challenge" organized by The Financial Times in cooperation with Seedstars to support those with new ideas has been launched.
The purpose of the competition is to support entrepreneurs in developing markets.

The Financial Times in cooperation with Seedstars launched the "FTxSDG Challenge" international start-up competition to support those with new ideas. support. The competition is divided into topics based on the Sustainable Development Goals of the United Nations.

Owners of developed startups, as well as those who do not have a ready-made product and want to start their own project, can participate in the competition. education;

  • decent work and economic education;
  • reducing inequality;
  • good health and well-being.

    • Individual participants work in groups for a month to solve real problems in different areas. At the end of the course, groups will have a pitch.

    Startup prizes include:

    • Short track in Seedstars Investor Fund (investment amount of USD 500,000);
    • Free participation in the 3-month Seedstars growth program (value of USD 80,000).
    • Rewards for members include:
    • Coaching sessions;
    • Free access, plus discounts on over 50 tools including AWS, Hubspot, Miro, Twilio and more (over $49,000 in total);
    • Certificates and awards;
    • PR support;
    • Placements on the FT Talent website;
    • Exclusive workshops entry.

    Deadline for accepting applications: November 1, 2021
