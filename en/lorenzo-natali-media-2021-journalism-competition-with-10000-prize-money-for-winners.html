Lorenzo Natali Media 2021: Journalism competition with €10,000 prize money for winners

Useful information for applicants and students:

Summary:
What topics should journalism cover?
For nearly three decades, the Lorenzo Natali Media Prize has been awarded for quality and bold reporting on topical issues such as: Inequality, Poverty Eradication, Sustainable Development, Environment, Biological diversity, climate impact, Digital (space, connectivity, e-governance, entrepreneurship), Jobs and employment, Education and skills development, Migration, Health (access, aid) and Peace, democracy and human rights.
European Award: for reporting published by media in the European Union.

About the competition in brief:

Lorenzo Natali Media Award was established by the European Commission in 1992 to honor courage in journalism. It rewards the work of journalists who report on issues of importance in the areas of inequality, poverty eradication, sustainable development, environment, biodiversity, jobs and employment, education and skills development, migration, health and peace, democracy and human rights.

What topics should journalism cover?

For nearly three decades The Lorenzo Natali Media Award is awarded for quality and bold reporting on topical issues such as:

  1. Inequality,
  2. Elimination of poverty,
  3. Sustainable development,
  4. Environment, biodiversity, climate impact,
  5. Digital (space, connectivity, e-governance, entrepreneurship),
  6. Jobs and employment,
  7. Education and skills development,
  8. Migration,
  9. Health (access, aid) and
  10. Peace, democracy and human rights.

Who can participate?

If you are a journalist, these three You can submit one application if you choose one of the categories in this online form:

  • Grand Prix: For a report published by a media outlet in one of the EU member states.
  • European Award: For a report published by a media outlet in the European Union.
  • The "Best Young Journalist" Award: Under 30 for a report of a journalist published in the mass media located in the European Union or one of its member countries.
