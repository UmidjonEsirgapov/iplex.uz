Is it possible to set a trial period when hiring students?

Is it possible to use a trial period when hiring students? Many employers and graduates are interested in this question. The trial period is usually set for three months and is used to check the ability to work of the employee and to determine whether it is appropriate to continue the work agreed in the employment contract.

However, based on Article 129 of the Labor Code of the Republic of Uzbekistan, the trial period is not set when certain persons are hired. This includes the following categories:

  • Pregnant women;
  • Women with children under the age of three;
  • Persons sent to work at the expense of minimum jobs determined for the enterprise;
  • Employed for the first time within three years from the date of graduation from the relevant educational institution of secondary special, vocational and higher education institutions. graduates.

Thus, when students who have graduated from higher education institutions enter employment for the first time, a probationary period is not set. This law is aimed at creating additional guarantees for graduates entering the workforce and supporting them. — Details here
