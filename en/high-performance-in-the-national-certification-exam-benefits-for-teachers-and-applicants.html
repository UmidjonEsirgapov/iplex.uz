What benefits does the national certificate give?

Obtaining a national certificate creates great opportunities not only for applicants, but also for teachers. Below you can familiarize yourself with the benefits granted according to the results achieved in the national certification exams.

1. Benefits for teachers

If a teacher scores 80% or higher in the national certification exam, a 20% bonus will be added to their monthly salary. This is a way to encourage teachers to regularly update their knowledge, which serves to increase the level of quality education.

2. Benefits to applicants

If the applicant has 86% or higher in the national certificate exam, maximum score will be given in the entrance exams in the relevant subject. For example, if mother tongue and literature is a subject of block 1, the applicant will be awarded 93 points, and 63 points if it is a subject of block 2. Also, a maximum of 11 points is provided from the mandatory 10 tests.

If the applicant gets a result from 60% to 85% as a result of the exam, he is given proportional points in relation to the percentage. For example, for a result of 80%, the applicant is awarded 80% of 93 points (ie 74.4 points).

3. Compulsory subject concessions

If an applicant is preparing for a compulsory 10-point test in the subject of mother tongue and achieves 60% or higher in the national certificate exam, he will be awarded a maximum of 11 points in the compulsory subject of mother tongue.
