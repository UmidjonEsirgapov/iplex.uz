A forum of Central Asian universities was held in Tashkent

Useful information for applicants and students:

Summary:
A Central Asian Universities Forum organized by the Ministry of Preschool and School Education and other partner organizations was held in Tashkent.
More than 60 international organizations from countries such as Great Britain, Germany, China and about a hundred foreign universities, including Massachusetts Institute of Technology and Cambridge University, participated in the forum. During the forum, public lectures of a number of famous scientists and specialists were organized, including: Luciano Rezzola, an Italian scientist, a famous figure of computational physics and one of the creators of the first images of black holes.
The forum was attended by representatives of 60 international organizations and nearly one hundred foreign universities from countries such as Great Britain, Germany, China, including Massachusetts Institute and Cambridge University.

Open lectures of a number of famous scientists and experts were organized during the forum. including:

  • Italian scientist, famous figure of computational physics and one of the creators of the first images of black holes Luciano Rezzola.
  • 2014 Nobel Prize laureate and one of the influential economists Jean Tirol.
  • Massachusetts Institute of Technology for academic development chancellor, professor Eric Grimson.
