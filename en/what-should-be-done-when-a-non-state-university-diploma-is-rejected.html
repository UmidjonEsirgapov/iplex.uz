What should be done when a non-state university diploma is rejected?

Sometimes students or graduates studying at a private university have doubts about the legitimacy of the diploma. Leaders or officials of some organizations may make claims that a private university diploma does not pass. In fact, diplomas of non-state universities licensed in Uzbekistan have a legal basis and should be recognized. If you are subject to such a restriction, please follow these rules.

1. Ask for a reason

If someone tells you this university is private, its diploma will not pass, demand a legal reason. Any refusal must be based on official documents.

2. Submit a written appeal

Send a formal written appeal to the official and ask them to indicate in a written response the legal grounds for denying the diploma.

3. If you can't get an answer…

In most cases this question is not answered, or if it is answered, no legal basis is provided. This means that there is actually no official ban.

4. Ask your university to write a formal letter

Ask your university to prepare a formal response to the relevant organization or official. Your university can provide license and accreditation documents.

5. If there is an unreasonable refusal, go to court

If the official response lacks a legal basis, you can take legal action. You have the right to apply to the court and hold the responsible person accountable.

Important note

These recommendations apply only to students of private universities who have an official license in Uzbekistan. If your university is recognized by the state, no one can refuse your diploma.

Conclusion

If your private university does not recognize your diploma, know how to demand your rights. The most effective way to request any official refusal is to request a formal appeal from the university and, if necessary, go to court. — Official Data
