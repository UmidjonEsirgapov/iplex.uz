Brief:

The Knight-Hennessy Fellowship Program at Stanford University cultivates diverse, multidisciplinary teams of young leaders from around the world and guides them in collaboration, innovation, and communication to prepare them to solve complex challenges facing society. Through the King Global Leadership Program, fellows expand knowledge, develop skills, and strengthen character as scholars take advantage of community experiences, workshops, meetings with leaders, domestic and international study tours, and personal development opportunities. The program offers a platform for the purposeful development of leadership skills and gives you the opportunity to make a widespread positive impact on the world.

Level of study:

Masters and doctoral degrees in any discipline at Stanford (eg, DMA, JD, MA, MBA, MD, MFA, MPP, MS, or PhD) or 1 - graduate options from Stanford's many joint and dual schools (eg, but not limited to, MD + PhD, JD + MA, MBA + MS).

Number of scholarships: unlimited

Target group: Applicants from all countries - no restrictions on nationality, age, field of study, career aspirations, etc.

Scholarship Cost / Inclusion:

Knight-Hennessy Fellows receive funding to study at Stanford University for up to 3 years to pursue any graduate degree at Stanford University. If your study plan is longer than 3 years—for example, a master's program, a doctoral program, or a dual joint degree program at Stanford—your home department at Stanford will fund the remainder of your education to the extent that you meet standard funding requirements and require commitment to that program. (This means, for example, that your program will fund 1 additional year for some PhDs and 3 additional years for others).

Knight-Hennessy Scholarships for the first three years of postgraduate programs:

- a stipend used to pay direct tuition and related fees;

- a stipend for accommodation and academic expenses (such as room and board, books, study materials, local transportation, and personal expenses);

- A travel grant to cover economy class airfare to and from Stanford once a year.

Knight-Hennessy Scholars may also use additional funds to support academic enrichment (e.g., travel to conferences). After studying at Stanford, you can enroll in additional graduate programs. In this case, you can apply to participate in the Knight-Hennessy Fellowship Program for additional funding for up to 3 years.

Additional funding for special situations: if you participate in additional research projects during the summer, the Knight-Hennessy Fellowship Program may provide summer funding based on a separate application.
