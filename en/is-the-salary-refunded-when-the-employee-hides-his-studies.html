Is the salary refunded when the employee hides his studies?

Important news for students and applicants has been announced.

Summary:
Can overpayment of wages be refunded?
According to Article 279 of the Labor Code: The overpayment of wages paid on the basis of an arbitrary or wrongly applied law is not refundable, if this situation occurred due to an error in the calculation. Exception.
If the employee gave false information or submitted a forged document, his overpaid salary will be returned based on a court decision or sentence.

Can the overpaid salary be returned?

An employee working full-time in a state office went to graduate school and did not notify the management about it if so, can his overpaid salary be refunded? We answer this question on the basis of Article 279 of the Labor Code.

According to Article 279 of the Labor Code:

  1. Overtime paid on the basis of an arbitrary or wrongly applied law is not returnable if this situation occurred due to an error in the calculation. except.

  2. If the employee has given false information or submitted a forged document, his overpaid wages will be returned based on a court decision or sentence.

How is the situation in question evaluated?

This situation does not fall under paragraph one of the Labor Code, because it is neither a calculation error there is no wrongful application of the law. On the contrary, the employee may have provided false information due to the fact that he concealed his studies at a higher education institution.
