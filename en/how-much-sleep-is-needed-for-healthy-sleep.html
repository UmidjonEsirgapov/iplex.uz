How much sleep is needed for healthy sleep is it necessary?

Sleep quality directly affects human health and productivity. The recommended amount of sleep for children and adults differs depending on age. Generally, 7-8 hours of sleep per day is the norm for adults. Failure to follow this regimen while preparing for study or work will negatively affect a person's attention, memory and general condition.

Symptoms of lack of sleep

If a person does not pay attention to getting enough sleep, the following problems may occur:

  • Quick fatigue and lack of energy
  • Attention and memory loss
  • Weakened immunity
  • Mood swings, depression or nervousness
  • Increased risk of cardiovascular diseases

Recommendations for quality sleep

  • Regulation of night sleep - sleeping and waking up at the same time every day is good for the body is useful.
  • Avoid phones and screens - stop looking at the screen at least 1 hour before bedtime improves the quality of sleep.
  • Control the room conditions - a cool and dark environment is one of the important factors for good sleep.
  • Do not eat heavy food in the evening - the digestive process at night can be stressful.
  • Regular physical activity – physical activity during the day helps to improve the quality of sleep.

If sleep problems persist for a long time, it is recommended to see a doctor. — Source
