According to the Labor Code, employees may be granted additional annual leave in the following cases:

  1. Employees employed in unfavorable working conditions: If the working conditions at the workplace are found to be difficult or dangerous, these employees may be given additional holidays.

  2. Employees working in unfavorable natural and climatic conditions: Additional holidays are provided for employees working in areas where natural and climatic conditions complicate the work process.

  3. Employees with many years of work experience in the organization or network: Depending on the length of service, employees may be granted additional vacations.

  4. Other cases defined by legislation or internal documents: Additional vacations may be granted to employees in other cases stipulated by collective agreements, contracts or internal documents.

Procedure of granting additional vacations

According to Article 222 of the Labor Code, additional vacations are added to the main annual vacation. For example, if an employee has 21 days of basic leave, 4 days of additional leave for 10 years of service, and 5 days of additional leave specified in the collective agreement, the total leave is 30 days. It should not exceed 56 calendar days.
