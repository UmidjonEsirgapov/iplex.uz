To whom and in what order are additional holidays granted?

Important news for students and applicants has been announced.

Summary:
To whom and in what order are additional vacations granted?
According to Article 222 of the Labor Code, annual additional vacations are combined with the main vacation period.
For example, if an employee: Main vacation - 21 days of work experience additional vacation under the collective agreement - 4 days (for 10 years of work experience) Vacation under the collective agreement - 5 days In this case, the employee will be entitled to a total of 30 days of vacation. These vacations are usually related to working conditions or length of service and provide employees with additional rest.

To whom are additional vacations granted?

  1. Employees working in unfavorable working conditions - Article 481 of the Labor Code
  2. Workers in harsh climatic conditions - Labor Code Article 483
  3. Employees with many years of work experience - Article 220 of the Labor Code
  4. Vacations granted in accordance with collective agreements, internal documents or labor contracts

How is additional leave calculated?

According to Article 222 of the Labor Code, each annual additional vacations are combined with the main vacation period. For example, if an employee:

  • Main vacation – 21 days
  • Additional vacation according to length of service – 4 days (for 10 years of work experience)
  • Vacation according to the collective agreement – 5 days

In this case, the employee has the right to a total of 30 days vacation will have.
