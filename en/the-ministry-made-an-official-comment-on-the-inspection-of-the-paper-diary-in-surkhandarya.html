The ministry made an official comment on the inspection of the paper diary in Surkhandarya

Today, the most relevant news in the field of education was:

Brief content:
The Ministry of Preschool and School Education responded to this and informed that the news circulating about the introduction of traditional diaries and class journals in schools has no legal basis.
According to it, the introduction of traditional (paper) diaries and class journals in schools The reports about this do not have a legal basis. Also, the ministry informed that schools in the republic were not instructed to return to traditional diaries and class journals.

In social networks, news that paper diaries are being checked in schools in Surkhandarya was spread. The Ministry of Pre-School and School Education reacted to this and stated that the news that is being spread about the introduction of traditional diaries and class magazines in schools has no legal basis.

According to the document signed by the head of the secretariat of the regional administration, the composition of the working group, the areas to be studied and the deadlines have been determined. The main task of the group is to comprehensively check the activity of students and pedagogues, document management and educational work in schools.

Some social media users said that the clauses in the document could lead to excessive work and red tape in schools. noted that there are excessive requirements for teachers' summaries.

The Ministry of Preschool and School Education gave an official comment on these reports.

According to it, reports that traditional (paper) diaries and class journals are being introduced in schools have no legal basis. At present, in all general education schools, students' attendance and learning of subjects are assessed through the eMaktab electronic journal system.

As a result of the introduction of this system, parents have the opportunity to remotely monitor their children's mastery of lessons, and transparency is being ensured in the evaluation of students. Also, various red tapes related to the work of pedagogues have been reduced,'' the ministry said.

Also, the ministry informed that schools in the republic were not instructed to return to traditional diaries and class journals. — Official Data
