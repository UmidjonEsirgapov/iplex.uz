Is it legal to put 30 hours of class into 3 days? Important information for teachers

Important news related to higher education institutions:

Brief content:
If you are thinking about how to divide your working time and class hours as a teacher, this article will be important for you.
According to Article 184 of the Labor Code, a teacher working at the same rate should work 36 hours a week and more than 6 hours a day.
If you spread 30 hours of classes over 3 days, you will have to work 18 hours a day, which is not only illegal, but also physically taxing. The question arises: Is it legal to spend 30 hours of classes on 3 days and rest on the remaining three days?

According to Article 184 of the Labor Code, a teacher working at the same rate must teach 36 hours a week and no more than 6 hours a day. This means that, taking into account the teacher's pedagogical workload and other duties, it is not legally correct to distribute 30 hours of lessons over 3 days and leave the rest of the days off. This download includes the educational process, methodological support, and organizational and pedagogical tasks. If you put 30 hours of classes in 3 days, you have to work 18 hours a day, which is not only outside the legal norms, but also physically burdensome.

At the same time, if in an educational institution 30 hours of classes are placed in 3 days, this will lead to a violation of the law. If such cases are detected by the state authorities, measures of responsibility may be taken against the school administration. — Source
