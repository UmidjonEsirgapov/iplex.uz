What are the subjects and difficulty levels of the specialty science block tests?

Important news for students and applicants has been announced.

Test topics and their percentage distribution in specialized subjects

The level of knowledge of applicants in entrance tests to higher education institutions is evaluated depending on their chosen specialized subjects. Within each subject, there is a percentage distribution of important subjects, and the test questions are prepared in this proportion.

Mother language and literature

If the subject of mother tongue and literature is selected in the specialty subjects, tests will be prepared from the following subjects:

H3: Distribution by subjects

  • Mother language – 60%

  • Literature – 40%

This distribution requires deep knowledge of language structure, methodological analysis and the content of literary works.

Mathematics

Test questions in mathematics are formed based on the following topics:

By topics distribution

  • Numbers and operations on them – 25%

  • Functions and algebraic expressions – 35%

  • Derivation, integral and their applications – 10%

  • Geometry – 30%

Questions test number theory, algebraic manipulations and geometric thinking.

History

Applicants who have chosen history will complete test tasks in the following main areas:

Distribution by topics

  • Political history – 50–60%

  • Socio-economic history – 10–20%

  • Cultural life – 10–15%

  • Practical tasks – 20–25%

These questions are historical analysis, requires an understanding of inter-period comparisons and causation.

Physics

Physics tests emphasize mechanics and electromagnetism in order of complexity:

Subject Breakdown

  • Mechanics – 35%

  • Molecular physics and thermodynamics – 25%

  • Electromagnetism – 30%

  • Atomic and nuclear physics (optics, theory of relativity) – 10%

The questions require practical and logical thinking along with theoretical knowledge.

Conclusion

Tests for specialized subjects are made in certain percentages on important topics in each subject. This distribution clearly shows the applicants which topics they should focus more on during their preparation. Thorough mastery of each subject based on the planned syllabus is the key to success in the exam. — Official Data
