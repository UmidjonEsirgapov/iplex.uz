Commentary on National Certificate Examinations in Chemistry and Biology

Important news for students and applicants has been announced.

Brief content:
Official response to national certificate exams The results of the national certificate exams in chemistry and biology, held on January 25-26, caused discussions on social networks. according to information, the test tasks are developed by subject experts and: are based on the curriculum, not textbooks, consist of test tasks with different levels of complexity, are analyzed using scientifically based evaluation methods, including the Rasch model.

The results of national certificate exams in chemistry and biology held on January 25-26 caused discussions on social networks. In this regard, Knowledge Assessment Agency issued an official statement.

Legal basis of exams

National certificate exams are regulated based on Decision No. 646 of the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan dated October 12, 2020. In accordance with paragraph 3 of this decision, test questions from general education subjects:

  • Developed in accordance with state educational standards
  • Based on curricula
  • Aimed at assessing the knowledge level of applicants

How are the test tasks formed?

According to the knowledge assessment agency, the test tasks are developed by subject experts and:

  • Based on educational programs, not textbooks
  • Consists of test tasks with different levels of complexity
  • Scientifically based evaluation methods, including the Rasch model are analyzed

According to the theory of pedagogical evaluation, it is impossible to accurately assess the student's knowledge level with only easy tests. For this reason, questions of different levels are distributed in the tests in a balanced manner.

Test specifics in chemistry and biology

On December 24, 2024, the Scientific Methodological Council of the Agency for the Evaluation of Knowledge redeveloped and approved the test specification for national tests in chemistry and biology. According to the new system, the test is structured as follows:

  • Open tests requiring short answers
  • Written tests requiring extended answers
  • Closed test tasks

The duration of written tasks is 80 minutes based on international experience and the results of previous exams.

Test results and analysis process

After each national certificate exam, test results are scientifically analyzed.

  • The level of knowledge of applicants is studied based on Wright's map
  • The effectiveness of test tasks is analyzed
  • Discussions on improvement are held in the Scientific and Methodological Council of the Knowledge Assessment Agency

All analyzes of test results Official of the Knowledge Assessment Agency will be announced on the website.

Conclusion

National certificate exams are based on educational standards and international assessment systems. Test tasks have different levels of complexity and are developed using scientifically based methods.
