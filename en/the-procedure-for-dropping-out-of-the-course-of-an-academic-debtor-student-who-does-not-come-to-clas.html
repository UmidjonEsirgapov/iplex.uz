The consequences of not coming to classes for students studying on the basis of the credit-module system in higher education are clearly defined. This procedure is aimed at ensuring that students are disciplined in their education.

Basic rules

Credits allocated for 1 subject:

  • 6 credits are allocated for each subject.
  • 1 credit equals 30 hours, and a total of 180 hours for a 6-credit subject.

Distribution of hours:

  • 40 percent of 180 hours or 72 hours will be allocated to classroom training,
  • the remaining 60 percent or 108 hours will be allocated to independent education.

Leave in classroom hours limit:

  • If a student misses ¼ of 72 hours (i.e. 18 hours), he is not included in the final inspection.

Academic indebtedness and suspension from the course

  • A student who is not included in the final inspection is considered academically indebted.
  • Academic delinquency will cause a student to drop out of a course.
  • A dropped out student can stay up to 8 years in the undergraduate program.

Conclusion

Regular attendance and compliance with educational requirements are important for students' academic success. These rules strengthen the disciplinary mechanism of the credit-module system and increase students' responsibility for the educational process. — More information
