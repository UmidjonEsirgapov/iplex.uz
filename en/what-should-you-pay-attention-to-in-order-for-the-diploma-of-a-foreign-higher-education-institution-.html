Question: The representatives of the consulting firm are offering me remote or part-time study at foreign universities without any exams, only by paying money.
The first company representatives After the course, they emphasize that I can transfer my studies to Uzbekistan without any exams, or that my diploma will be recognized in Uzbekistan without any exams. are offering distance or part-time study. The representatives of the firm emphasize that after the first course I can transfer my studies to Uzbekistan without any exams, or that my diploma will be recognized in Uzbekistan without an exam after I finish my studies. Can I believe what they say?

Currently, some individuals and legal entities providing consulting services are campaigning among our youth to submit documents for admission to foreign higher education organizations.

In order for the diploma of a foreign higher education organization offered to you to be recognized in Uzbekistan, pay attention to the following:

First of all, it is necessary to check whether it has a document (license) allowing to operate as a higher education institution.

The next most important requirement is related to the recognition of the document issued by the educational institution, that is, the diploma in the territory of the country where it is located. To do this, check whether the higher education institution and its educational programs have been accredited by the competent authorities.

In addition, the applicant should find out whether the chosen higher education institution's teaching format and educational programs comply with the legislation of Uzbekistan. For example, according to the current Law on Education, the duration of bachelor's degree is at least 4 years, part-time education is at least 5 years, and master's degree is at least 2 years.

Also, due to the pandemic, many consulting organizations advertise "diploma in Uzbekistan" under the guise of distance education in higher education institutions in our country. are promoting foreign higher education institutions that provide distance education with unsubstantiated information such as "directly recognized".

This leads to incorrect information being provided to the citizens of our republic and documents read in this way are not recognized in the Republic of Uzbekistan in accordance with the current legislation and causes objections from citizens.

Based on the above recommendations, carefully study the information about the university offered to you and then make a decision.
