General information:

IELTS (International English Language Testing System) and TOEFL (Test Of English as a Foreign Language) are both exams that evaluate the candidates' English language proficiency based on their own criteria. are international exams. So, it is not wrong to say that they are united by one goal. The language certificates provided by these organizations are one of the main keys that open the doors of opportunity for people who intend to study or work abroad.

The main differences between TOEFL and IELTS:

  1. TOEFL usually requires the use of only the US English version or the UK English version and their specific units and pronunciation forms during the exam, and not mixing the two, while the IELTS exam does not have a strict requirement in this regard. No matter which variant of the English language you use, you can use it incorrectly.
  2. TOEFL mainly consists of multiple choice questions (Multiple Choice), and IELTS differs more in terms of the types of questions and the ways to answer them.
  3. The IELTS exam takes less time than the TOEFL.
  4. IELTS Speaking section is conducted face-to-face with the examinee.

TOEFL iBT:

Universities that require a TOEFL score usually require you to have a certain final TOEFL iBT score. iBT stands for internet-based test (internet-based test) and is the latest type of TOEFL test.

It is mainly used as an admissions test for US universities and the most common English proficiency test for higher education.

TOEFL scores:

Typically, universities use the TOEFL test. asks for a certain overall score (overall score), but in some cases, universities may also set a minimum requirement for a score obtained from a certain skill. It mainly depends on the university and the selected study program.

TOEFL iBT test sections:

TOEFL iBT test evaluates language ability based on different sections. Candidates' reading, listening, writing and speaking skills are tested and an overall score is awarded for each section as well as the final score. See the table below:

TOEFL iBT takes four hours and consists of four skill sections. There is a mandatory break in the middle of the sections. The reading and listening sections may vary in length depending on the number of questions you are asked.

TOEFL Reading Section:

This section is computer graded and consists of 36-56 task questions based on your reading and understanding of academic texts.

TOEFL Listening Section:

This section is computer-graded and consists of 34-51 task questions based on listening to lectures, class discussions and conversations, and then answering questions based on your understanding.

TOEFL Speaking Section:

This section has six tasks that are specific to the exam. evaluated by the recipients. The section lasts 20 minutes.

TOEFL "Writing" section:

The written works written in this section are evaluated based on the test-taker's personal and computer rating for better understanding of the content. This section contains two tasks to be completed in approximately 50 minutes.

IELTS academic:

All UK universities accept IELTS academic test results as a certificate of English proficiency, but the level required varies by institution and their programs.

In addition, IELTS academic is recognized by educational institutions in Canada and European countries. obtained.

IELTS scores:

Candidates are given a whole score from zero to nine, inclusive of half, to represent their level of English.

IELTS test sections:

IELTS listening, reading and writing sections are held on the same day. The speaking test is held a week or so before the other section. According to the procedure, candidates have the option to change the test day of the IELTS speaking section.

IELTS Listening section:

In the listening section of the test, you will listen to four pre-recorded monologues or multiple conversations. About 30 minutes will be allocated for this, and 10 minutes for writing your answer on the answer sheet.

IELTS "Reading" section:

The reading part of the test lasts one hour; it consists of three long texts with different questions. The given texts are taken from books, magazines and newspapers, and in some cases they may be presented with graphs and illustrations.

IELTS "Writing" section:

In the first part of the writing section (Task 1), you must write an essay (report) of at least 150 words that reflects the information in the given graph, table or diagram. will be. In the second part (Task 2), you will be asked to write a short essay of at least 250 words in the form of an answer to a certain question.

IELTS "Speaking" section:

This section is relatively short and can last up to 15 minutes. This exam consists of three parts:

  • Introduction and Interview;
  • Independent Long Turn
  • Discussion

and each part has its own requirements.
