Middle and high school students know what an outline is, but do they know how to remember difficult dates, quotes, and facts?

Outlining is important. The ability to write activates the parts of the brain that help to remember information for a long time. When we outline a topic, we help ourselves create a visual range through which information can be absorbed more effectively.

By hand or on a computer. Both this method and that method have their pros and cons. While working on the computer, you can be distracted by games, the Internet or messengers. But on the other hand, if you use cloud resources, data is stored more reliably. Writing by hand is more useful, but in today's world, people write faster on computers. It doesn't matter what you prefer, even if it's a sticker album - isn't that a creative approach to synopsis? The main thing is that no matter what method you choose, it should be convenient for you.

General tips for taking notes: — More information

  • Note the main points of the lesson;
  • Don't write everything. Note only the important words and phrases of the teacher;
  • Note in your own words to make your notes understandable;
  • Record new words. Mark them with a text separator to make it easier to find;
  • If you missed something, ask the teacher to go back or repeat it. If you are shy, mark it and ask the teacher about it after the lesson;
  • If your handwriting is not clear to you in a hurry, it is better to rewrite the text cleanly after the lesson. Information will be absorbed more firmly;
  • Learn to shorten and use symbols and abbreviations. Try to start with easy and understandable things, but add new ones over time.
