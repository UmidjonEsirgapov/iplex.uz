Fees and contracts of private university students included in the youth register will be paid

Important news related to higher education institutions:

Brief content:
Private university students included in the youth register will be paid up to 17 million soums. According to Appendix 8 of the regulation, payment-contract money is paid for the following categories: Child of a family included in the iron book Child of a woman included in the Women's book Child of a family included in the Single Register of Social Protection Child of a family studying at a professional or higher education institution Student-student brought up in an orphanage or Children's town Low-income student-student deprived of parental care, placed under guardianship At least of family members A child of a low-income family, one of whom has a group I or both group II disabilities, who is studying at a professional or higher education institution. A student with a disability or a health problem. part-time and evening part-time students studying in the full-time Master's program are paid a part of their annual expenses on the basis of a fee-contract.
Payment of these expenses is carried out based on the positive recommendation of the youth leader up to 50% of the fee-contract, but not exceeding 50 times of the BHM (17,000,000 soums) will be increased.

Private university students included in the youth register will be paid contract money up to 17 million soums.

According to paragraph 35 of the decision of the Cabinet of Ministers No. 312 (7.06.2022) and appendix 8 of this regulation, contract money for the following categories is paid:

  • A child of a family included in the iron book
  • A child of a woman included in the women's book
  • A child of a family enrolled in the single register of social security, studying at a professional or higher education institution
  • A student-student brought up in an orphanage or Children's town
  • Parent A low-income student-student deprived of care, taken into custody
  • A child of a low-income family with at least one of the family members having a group I or both group II disabilities studying at a professional or higher education institution
  • A student-student with a disability or health impairment
  • Small family secured student
