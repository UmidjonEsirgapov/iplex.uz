In what cases can a student be expelled from a higher education institution?
A student can be expelled from a higher education institution in the following cases: – According to his/her own will; - In connection with the transfer of studies to another educational institution; - For violating academic discipline and the internal procedures and rules of etiquette of the higher education institution; - Due to missing classes for more than 74 hours during one semester without good reason; - Due to the failure to pay the tuition fee on time (for students studying on a fee-contract basis); - In connection with the student's deprivation of liberty by the court; - When, according to the court's decision, it is found that the procedure established by the entrance exams has been violated (in this case, those excluded from the ranks of students will not be reinstated in the ranks of students); - Due to death.
In addition, a student has the right to receive academic leave in cases such as military service, recovery of health, pregnancy and childbirth, childcare.
