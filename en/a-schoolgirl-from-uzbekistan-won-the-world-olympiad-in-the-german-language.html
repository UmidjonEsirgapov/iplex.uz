A schoolgirl from Uzbekistan became the winner of the World Olympiad in the German language

The names of the winners of the World Olympiad in the German language were announced in Gottingen, Germany. Students from 62 countries competed in it. Rayona Ibrahimova from Uzbekistan, Mahek Khan from Bangladesh and Ana Kotevska from North Macedonia qualified for the first place at the World Olympiad held on July 15-22.

According to the press service of the Goethe Institute, 16-year-old Rayona is studying at one of the universities in Germany. plans to study German studies and work as a translator in the future. In order to reach the final stage of the international competition, he had to win the national competition, like other participants.

In Gottingen, students competed in various subjects. The jury assessed not only the level of knowledge of the German language, but also creativity, as well as the ability to work in a team.

Silver and Bronze medals were awarded to visiting schoolchildren from Mexico, Italy, Slovakia, Poland and Great Britain. The results were announced by the Goethe-Institut in Berlin on Monday, July 22. More than 100 participants aged 14 to 17 took part in the final stage of the Olympiad.

The World Olympiad of the German Language is held every two years in one of the cities of Germany. The organizer is Goethe Institute. The event aims to increase interest in learning German in schools outside Germany. — More information
