Tuition fees:

Is higher education in Norway really free as rumored? Rumors are really true ... In Norway the tuition fee, or ``contract'' price in the vernacular, is zero. However, students must be able to cover the other expenses for studying and living in higher education institutions. Most universities charge an annual administration fee of around €80-100. In addition, the student pays an average of 500 euros per semester to be able to freely use the university's library, laboratory, educational materials and local transportation.

Living expenses:

In a developed country like Norway, it is natural that living expenses are high. Students spend an average of 800-1000 euros on monthly rent and living expenses. This amount is higher than in other European Union countries. Universities also provide cheaper accommodation for students, but it is usually very limited.

"Pain point":

In order to study in Norway, like in a number of other developed European countries, a student has to deposit NOK 123,519 (about €12,000) in a Norwegian bank for the first year's expenses. need This amount is full student and can be withdrawn for monthly living expenses during their studies in Norway. Through this, the government aims to ensure that the student gets education without distraction. But this aspect is a pain point for many students, as we said above. There is definitely a solution for this problem, and it is definitely provided by the country - grants.
