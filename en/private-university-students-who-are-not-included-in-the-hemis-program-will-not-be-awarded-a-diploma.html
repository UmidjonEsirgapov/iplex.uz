The topic "No diploma will be awarded to students of private universities not included in the Hemis program" is widely discussed in social networks. This is not news, our editors reported about it 1.5 years ago.

Hemis system and impact on students

Students of private universities not included in the Hemis information system are not only not awarded a diploma, but also not allocated preferential credit. This can be a big problem for students, because they may not have a diploma approved by the state after graduation.

Reason: Presidential Decree No. PF-81

According to the Presidential Decree No. PF-81, adopted on May 24, students enrolled in the Hemis information system will be allocated preferential loans and awarded a diploma. Based on this decree, private universities are required to enter the data of their students into the Hemis system.

Conclusion

Not only are students who are not included in the Hemis system not to be awarded diplomas, but also they are not allocated preferential loans, according to the Presidential Decree. This will encourage students to register in the Hemis system on time and help to make educational processes transparent and orderly. — Source
