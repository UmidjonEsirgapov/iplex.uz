Persons with disabilities do not require a certificate to enter master's degrees

In Uzbekistan, some persons with disabilities have been granted privileges to enter master's degrees. According to the decision of the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan No. PQ-407 of October 20, 2022, certain requirements for admission to master's degrees and post-higher education specialties of state higher education institutions have been eased.

For whom a certificate is not required?

The decision According to paragraph 4, the following persons are exempted from presenting a national or international certificate of knowledge of a foreign language when entering a master's degree:

  • Persons with visual impairment
  • Persons with hearing impairment
  • Persons with speech impairment

This privilege It does not apply to all persons with disabilities, but only to representatives of the above three categories.

What does this privilege mean?

  • There is no obligation to obtain a foreign language certificate when entering a master's degree
  • Educational opportunities for persons with disabilities will be expanded
  • In order to create equal opportunities in education additional conditions will be created

This ease of access to higher education is one of the important decisions aimed at strengthening inclusion in the education system. — Source
