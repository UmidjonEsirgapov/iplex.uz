Teaching profession seems interesting and glamorous to many, but the bitter truth behind it is unknown to many people. In this article, we provide complete information about the daily life of teachers and the problems they face.

Daily Activities of Teachers

  1. Time Spent Standing:

  • Teachers spend 79% of their time standing. This increases their physical activity and causes them to tire quickly.

  1. Speaking Loudly:

  • They have to speak loudly or very loudly every day for an average of 3.5-4 hours. This, over time, damages their vocal cords and health.

  1. Nominal and Real Working Time:

  • Although the teacher's working day officially lasts 6-7 hours, in practice they work 10-12 hours a day. This causes great pressure and overwork for them.

Health and Nutrition

  1. Vitamin Deficiency:

  • 80% of teachers suffer from partial vitamin deficiency because their salary and work schedule do not allow them to eat properly.

  1. Stress and Its Effects:

  • Two-thirds of teachers are under constant stress. The level of stress is highest among principals and their vice-principals, and above average among teachers of physical sciences, linguistics, and elementary grades. is made up of women. This means that in addition to the educational load, they also deal with household chores and child rearing.

  1. Sleep Time:

  • 50% of teachers sleep less than six hours a day instead of eight. This reduces their efficiency and health.

  1. Physical Activity:

  • Only 14 percent of teachers have the opportunity to do morning exercises. This has a negative impact on their physical health.

Food and Rest

  1. Diet:

  • Every fifth teacher eats only twice a day, every other three. However, it is recommended to eat four or five light meals a day.

  1. Opportunities for recreation:

  • 80 percent of teachers cannot relax at a sanatorium or by the sea. In fact, such leave is necessary for full recovery of health.

Conclusion

The teaching profession includes unique and important tasks, but there are also many problems associated with it. Teachers bear physical and mental burdens, many of them do not have access to adequate rest or proper nutrition. In order to solve these problems, every member of the society needs to pay attention and help. — Source
