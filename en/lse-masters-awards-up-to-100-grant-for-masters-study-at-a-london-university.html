LSE Master's Awards: Up to 100% grant for master's study at a London university

Here's today's top news in education:

Summary:
Grant Brief: The London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE) is offering LSE Master's Awards (LMA) to postgraduate students. It is designed to help students who have entered, but have little financial means to study.
For whom: For international students who have been invited to study on one of the master's programs taught at LSE.

Grant details:

London School of Economics and Political Science (LSE) graduate students. offers LSE Master's Awards Program Grants (LMA) for postgraduate studies. These grant awards are designed to help students who are enrolled in postgraduate studies at LSE but who are financially disadvantaged. for international students invited to study in one of the taught master's programs. Applicants from low- and middle-income countries have a greater chance of winning this grant.

Benefits of the grant:

The award-winning candidates will receive grants from £5,000 (about $7,000) to 100% of their tuition fees.

How to apply:

Candidates must first apply here for one of the university's master's degree courses scheduled to begin in September. After that, they will be able to submit a separate application for the grant program here.
