The number of higher education institutions training journalist personnel in Uzbekistan increased by one more. Starting from the 2020/2021 academic year, Tashkent State University of Oriental Studies accepts applicants for Journalism. Also, starting from the current academic year, in addition to undergraduate journalism, 3 new courses of study were opened at the university. Gulchehra Rikhsiyeva, rector of the University of Oriental Studies, informed about this at a briefing held at AOKA.

According to the conference, starting from the 2020-2021 academic year, 4 new undergraduate courses have been opened based on industry and labor market requirements and advanced foreign experiences.

Including:
• Textology and literary source studies;
• Journalism: international journalism;
• Guiding and interpreting activities (oriental languages);
• Tourism (by areas of activity)

The rector said that the admission quotas for 2 new specialties - Ethnography, ethnology and anthropology and Tourism (areas and types of activities) were formed at the Higher Education Institution. Starting from the 2020/2021 academic year, new curricula have been developed for students of the first stage in order to transfer the educational process to the credit-module system.

In these curricula, according to YeCTS (European Credit Transfer System), students will acquire 240 credits at the bachelor's level and 120 credits at the master's level.

Starting this year, Turkish, Arabic, Databases of test materials have been created in cooperation with the State Test Center in order to conduct tests in Persian, Dari, Hindi, Urdu, Chinese, Korean, Uyghur, and Japanese languages. Proposals on the organization of training of highly qualified scientific and scientific-pedagogical personnel in the fields of ethnology, ethnography and anthropology have been included.

Let us remind you that in accordance with the decision signed by the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan Shavkat Miziyoyev on April 16 of this year, Tashkent State Institute of Oriental Studies was granted the status of "university".
