The Weinstein JAMS International Internship Program is a grant that allows for an internship with the United States for from 1 to 6 months. This international internship program was established in 2008, allowing participants to come to the United States to study dispute resolution processes and practices with qualified professionals.

The JAMS Foundation provides scholarships of up to $20,000 to support projects nominated by scholarship holders. The program is designed for people active in the field of Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) who are active in the field of conflict resolution education and training in their own countries and abroad. months to four months;

  • Grant funds may only be used to support scholarship activities in the United States;
  • Proposals that help create an acceptable dispute resolution system or change dispute resolution methods will be given priority for winning a grant. Also, applicants with experience as a judge, attorney, law professor, court administrator, government official, or ADR practitioner are more likely to participate in this program;
  • In addition to other activities, scholarship recipients must also attend a fellowship meeting held in the San Francisco Bay Area in early September of the year the program begins;
