Uzbekistan ranks lower than Afghanistan in terms of English proficiency

Central Asian countries took the following places in the rating: Kyrgyzstan - 90th place; Kazakhstan is 104th place.

Uzbekistan took 93rd place out of 113 places in the English language proficiency index compiled by EF (Education First) company. The study, which covered 2.2 million participants worldwide, is based on the results of the EF Standard English Test, a free online language test conducted in 2023.

Notably, Uzbekistan dropped four places compared to the 2022 ranking. The report notes that the level of language proficiency is particularly low in Fergana and Andijan regions, and the lowest rate is also recorded in all other regions and cities of the republic.

"The level of English proficiency in Central Asia is low and stable, and the gender gap in favor of men is higher than average in almost all countries," the report concludes.

Central Asian countries ranked as follows:

  • Kyrgyzstan is the 90th place;
  • Kazakhstan is the 104th place;
  • Tajikistan is the 112th place.

Turkmenistan is not on the list. The ranking of Afghanistan is better than all the countries of Central Asia and is 88th. Among all Central Asian countries, positive trends were observed only in Kyrgyzstan and Afghanistan, and in other countries, a negative trend was observed. The Democratic Republic of the Congo rounded out the list, taking the lowest place. — Official Data
