  • General Information

OIST (Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology) is open to undergraduate students from around the world or recent university graduates. for those who have completed and wish to join the doctoral level program. OIST offers an opportunity to study as a scientific practitioner in the Internship program. The scientific internship at the OIST university gives students the opportunity to work and gain experience in a research environment under the guidance of the OIST faculty.

The process of accepting candidates into the program is carried out regardless of their age, sexual orientation, mental or physical disability, medical condition, race, nationality, ancestry, culture, national origin, religion and family status. Applications from women are especially encouraged.

For whom:

OIST students who are in the last 2 years of undergraduate or graduate programs at an international or Japanese university or who are graduates of similar institutions can apply for the OIST internship program. Candidates must obtain permission from their currently enrolled universities to study in the program.

Application Process:

To apply for the program, candidates will need to open an online account. Make sure you remember your username and password. You can fill in the requested information in the online window of the program at once or in several attempts. The following materials are required to apply for the program:

1. Curriculum Vitae (CV);

    • There is no mandatory format for this, but you can use the sample form provided by OIST;

2. Statement of Interest - Write a 400-word statement that includes answers to the following questions:

      • How does OIST and your desired field of study fit your current experience and skill level? If you are interested in more than one research unit, please explain your interest in a single statement.
      • What do you intend to achieve at OIST?
      • How does a research internship at OIST fit into your overall career plans/dreams?

3. Academic Transcript (Grades);

    • Scanned copies of your current academic transcripts and any other transcripts from your previous degree (undergraduate, graduate grades, etc.).

4. Letter of recommendation;

    • A recommendation letter must be provided by a teacher or research specialist closely familiar with your academic work. Each applicant is required to have a minimum of one and a maximum of three letters of recommendation from relevant recommenders. During the application process for the program, you leave the email address of the recommender, and the email address you provide will be requested directly by the organizers of the referral program. Get the referral's approval before submitting information on the form. Recommendations are not accepted from the candidates themselves. Applicants will be automatically notified in the online application system when OIST receives a letter of recommendation from the required persons.

5. Passport Photo - Upload a recent passport-sized front-facing photo of your face.

Admission Deadline:

Admissions for the program are usually held twice a year, and currently admission for Round 1 of the program began on March 15, 2021, and will continue until April 15, 2021. Stakeholders of the program:

OIST provides the following financial support for successful candidates:

  • Internship Stipend - YPY 2,400 per internship day - approximately US$22 (excluding tax and weekends);
  • Free Transportation - OIST Shuttle Bus Pass;
  • Travel Expenses - direct one-way plane ticket (travel date will be close to placement);
  • Accommodation - furnished apartment on or off campus in the form.
