What will be the changes in the activity of non-state OTMs from 2025?

Today, the most relevant news in the field of education was:

Brief content:
Important changes regulating the activities of non-state higher education institutions are being introduced from the 2025/2026 academic year. It is possible.
Quota restriction on student admission Starting from 2025, non-state higher education institutions will not be able to accept students for other forms (external, evening, remote) in excess of the number of students accepted for full-time education. is being introduced. Below are the main notes that applicants and universities should know.

1. Registration in the HEMIS system is mandatory

  • It is required to operate on the basis of the HEMIS program.

  • Students not included in HEMIS will not be awarded a diploma.

2. Contract agreements are concluded only through kontrakt.edu.uz

  • Contract agreements are concluded only through the kontrakt.edu.uz platform.

  • Educational credit is valid only for contracts issued through this system.

3. Admission processes are limited

  • Non-state universities can conduct admission processes only once a year (June-September).

  • The practice of admission can be canceled at any time.

4. Quota restriction on student admission

  • Starting from 2025, non-state HEIs will not be able to admit more students to other forms (part-time, evening, distance) than the number of students admitted to full-time education.

  • That is: day = part-time + evening + distance.

5. The accreditation procedure will be strengthened

  • Non-state universities must undergo comprehensive state accreditation every 4 years.

  • This process is aimed at improving and controlling the quality of education. — Read on infoedu.uz
