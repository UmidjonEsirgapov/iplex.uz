Water Crisis and Climate Change in Central Asia: 3-day all-expenses-paid conference in Kyrgyzstan

General information:

Kyrgyzstan C5+1 Youth Council is accepting applications for the conference on the theme "Water crisis and climate change problems in Central Asia and Afghanistan" started to do. The three-day conference "Water Crisis and Climate Change in Central Asia (CA) and Afghanistan" aims to raise awareness among high school and university students about water crisis and climate change issues. It will serve as a platform for California experts and environmental researchers to discuss potential solutions using the Model UN format.

The conference will include: presentations, workshops, debates. Participants will brainstorm ideas for project proposals to promote innovative approaches to peace-building dialogues, negotiations, conflict resolution and water solutions in their communities. The best ideas will be funded by C5+1 YC.

Requirements for candidates:

  • English-speaking high school (grades 10-11), college/high school students and university students can apply;
  • Candidates from Central Asian countries must be citizens (Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, Kazakhstan, Tajikistan, Afghanistan);
  • Must be interested in water crisis and climate change issues;

Benefits:

  • All expenses, including travel, accommodation and food expenses will be covered;
  • From Uzbekistan only Tashkent and Travel expenses to Osh will be covered through Namangan regions (residents of other regions must cover travel costs to Tashkent and Namangan themselves);
