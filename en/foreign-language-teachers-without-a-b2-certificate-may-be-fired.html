Foreign language teachers without a B2 certificate may be fired

A new procedure will come into force from the 2025/26 academic year

In order to improve the quality of the education system of the Republic of Uzbekistan, starting from the 2025/26 academic year, foreign language in all general secondary, secondary special and professional educational institutions it is mandatory for teachers who teach science to have a language certificate of at least B2 level.

Teachers without a certificate can be fired

According to official sources, if a teacher does not fulfill this requirement, the employment contract concluded with him may be terminated. These strict measures are aimed at improving the quality of foreign language education and training personnel according to international standards.

The requirement applies to all foreign languages

This mandatory B2 requirement includes English, German, French, Korean, Chinese, Arabic and other foreign languages. However, this requirement does not apply to second foreign languages. That is, this requirement is not imposed on non-specialized, additional studied foreign languages.

Current situation: the number of teachers with B2 certificate

Today, the number of foreign language teachers with a B2 or higher language certificate across Uzbekistan is 29,001. As this indicator is still insufficient, the remaining teachers will have to pass the exam and obtain the necessary certificate by the 2025/26 academic year.

Conclusion

The requirement for the B2 certificate for foreign language teachers serves to increase the quality of education. Therefore, it is necessary for teachers to prepare for exams and improve their skills. Otherwise, they may lose their jobs.
