Japan has allocated grants to educational institutions in Uzbekistan

On February 28, a ceremony of signing grant agreements was held in Tashkent within the framework of the program of the Japanese government "Supporting small social projects to ensure human security". According to the Japanese Embassy, the partner country is providing grants in the amount of $140,165 to three educational institutions in Uzbekistan.

The Minister of Preschool and School Education of Uzbekistan Hilola Umarova and the Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary Ambassador of Japan to our country Takashi Hatori took part in the signing ceremony of the grant documents.

According to the agreement, in the city of Urganch, Khorezm region 91,000 dollars will be allocated to improve the sanitary and hygienic conditions of school 31, and about 25,000 dollars will be allocated to provide educational facilities to schools 19 and 28 in Termiz, Surkhondarya region. He stated that he is determined to further strengthen cooperation.

"Japan will first of all fully cooperate in the socio-economic front so that Uzbekistan can develop in an open and stable way on the path it has chosen. "Grant agreements within the framework of the program "Supporting small social projects to ensure human security" are one direction of cooperation aimed at improving the welfare of the people of Uzbekistan," the diplomat said.

According to the Japanese ambassador, investments aimed at increasing human potential are common with the efforts of the Uzbek government in this regard.

As we previously reported, Ambassador T. Hatori also noted at the conference held yesterday in Tashkent that the Kunchikar region will continue to provide assistance to Uzbekistan in the fields of economy, education and human resources development. — Source
