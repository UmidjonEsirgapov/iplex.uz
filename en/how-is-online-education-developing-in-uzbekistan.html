Experts of the Institute of Macroeconomic and Regional Studies He studied the status and trends of online education in Uzbekistan.

The rapid transition to distance education due to the COVID-19 pandemic has led to an increase in demand for online education services.

In the United States, about 22% of graduate students and 11% of undergraduate students receive online education.

Online education revenue is expected to reach $74.80 billion in 2023.

A majority (75%) of academic managers agree that the learning outcomes of online courses are the same or better than those of classroom courses.

E-learning market share in the UK between 2021-2026 is $11.57 It is expected to reach billion.

The spread of micro-learning, in which all course topics are divided into smaller parts consisting of video, audio, text or infographics, is one of the main trends of the e-learning market in the UK and is driving the growth of the market.

Today, platforms such as Coursera and edX offer online courses in cooperation with more than 200 leading universities and companies around the world.

These online platforms allow you to get master's and bachelor's degrees from your hometown.

Also, those studying through these platforms have the opportunity to use the same curriculum as students on campus.

After the quarantine measures, online education in Uzbekistan began to improve rapidly.

Last year. in October, the government approved the regulation on the introduction of distance education in higher educational institutions.

From the 2023/2024 academic year, state and private higher education institutions were allowed to train personnel in the field of distance education.

In October 2022, between "Coursera" and Uzbekistan in the field of IT education a memorandum of cooperation was signed.

The platform will distribute about 1.2 million free coding courses available starting this year.

E-learning is effective for the following reasons:

  • increases the participation of women in the labor market;
  • provides opportunities for persons with disabilities to receive higher education;
  • covers higher education in the country.
