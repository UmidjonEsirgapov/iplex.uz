There are various types of grants for applicants planning to study at US universities. These grants can be full or partial, and they usually fall into 6 main types. is divided.

1. Merit-based scholarships

These grants are based on the student's:

  • Academic achievements (excellent grades, SAT/ACT scores),

  • Contribution to society and extracurricular activities,

  • Achievements in sports

They are in the following forms:

  • Academic Scholarships

  • Average Academic Performance Scholarships

  • Sports Scholarships

2. Need-based scholarships

Allocate for students with limited financial opportunities. These grants help cover tuition, accommodation and other expenses.

3. Country-based scholarships (Country-based scholarships)

These grants are intended for citizens of developing countries and are allocated to stimulate international students.

4. University Grants

Many universities in the USA allocate grants for themselves based on special criteria. For example:

  • Talented students in a certain field,

  • Candidates with the highest scores,

  • Applicants who fit the university strategy.

Find information about such grants in the "Financial Aid" or "Scholarships" section on the official website of the university. possible.

5. Private sector grants

Funded by various companies and non-profit organizations. Usually, they are:

  • Representatives of a certain group (for example, women, citizens of developing countries),

  • They are provided for students active in special social or scientific fields.
