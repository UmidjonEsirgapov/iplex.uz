What is a visa and how to get it?

Visa is an official permit that gives the right to enter, live, study or work in a foreign country, and it is often issued by the country's embassy or consulate. Visas are determined according to the internal policy of each country, and their conditions, duration and type may differ.

The main features of a visa:

  1. State permission - each country independently determines who to enter or not enter its territory.

  2. Term - a visa is usually for a certain period of time.

  3. Purpose – the visa is issued on the basis of tourist, work, educational, diplomatic or other purposes.

  4. Conditions – some visas impose restrictions on work or study.

How to get a visa?

  1. Embassy or Applying to the consulate is done online or offline.

  2. Submitting documents – passport, photo, bank statement, insurance and other required documents.

  3. Visa fee – the amount of the fee varies depending on the type of visa.

  4. Interview (if required) - in some cases, an interview will be held.

  5. Waiting for the result - your application will be reviewed and a positive or negative response will be returned.

Full information about visa types

Different countries offer different visa types. Below is a list of the main visas:

  1. Tourist visa – for short-term travel and recreation.

  2. Student visa – for studies, language courses or academic programs.

  3. Work visa – for legal work in a foreign country.

  4. Business visa - for investment or business trips.

  5. Permanent residence visa - for those who want to live permanently (for example, Green Card).

  6. Family/non-resident visa - for living with a foreigner.

  7. Humanitarian and asylum visa - from war or persecution for refugees.

  8. Transit visa – for a short period on the way to another country.

  9. Diplomatic and service visas – for civil servants and representatives of organizations.
