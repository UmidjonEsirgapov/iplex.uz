About the university and the grant program:

University of Southern Denmark In 1998, Odense University, Southern Denmark School of Business and Engineering and South Jutland University Center was established as a result of the merger. The university offers a number of joint programs in cooperation with the University of Flensburg and the University of Kiel.

The Danish Government Grant Program offered by the university provides financial support to deserving international candidates who intend to study engineering at the master's level.

Requirements for the candidate?

Candidates must be citizens of countries outside the European Union and the European Economic Area and to study at the master's level in the following engineering fields offered by the university must have been accepted for the autumn term (Sønderborg):

  • MSc in Engineering - Mechatronics

  • MSc in Engineering - Electronics (Sønderborg)

Grant application procedure:

A separate application for the grant program is not required. All applicants accepted for the scholarship will be automatically considered as candidates and will be offered to the most deserving.

Benefits of the scholarship:

  • The scholarship program will cover 100% of the winners' tuition fee
  • DKK 3,000 per month for living expenses. (=approx. USD 490)
