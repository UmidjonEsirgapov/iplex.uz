About the grant program in brief:

The Joint Japan/World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program (JJ/WBGSP) - The World Bank's Vice-President for Development Economics (DEC) has long awarded grants to international students and young researchers to support the World Bank's capacity-building and knowledge-sharing mission in developing countries. helped to form an international personnel department consisting of knowledgeable and experienced specialists and scientists.

Who can apply for the grant?

Candidates who meet the following requirements can apply for the grant:

  • Be a citizen of one of the developing countries on this list that is a member of the World Bank;
  • Any other developed country not have received the second citizenship of the country;
  • Be in good health;
  • Have a bachelor's degree (or its equivalent) obtained at least 3 years before the date of application;
  • Have 3 years of work experience after receiving a bachelor's (or its equivalent) scientific degree;
  • At the time of submitting the application for a grant, working in a permanent job with a salary in development-related fields be;
  • On or before the end of the scholarship application period, for the next academic year, the applicant must be unconditionally admitted (except for funding) to at least one of the JJ/WBGSP program's valid master's programs outside the applicant's country of citizenship and registration at that time. When the application for the grant is opened, the applicant's letter of acceptance to one of the universities where the grant is valid must also be uploaded;
  • Applicants must not be the Executive Director, his deputy and/or any type of employees of the World Bank, or be defined as a "close relative" of the aforementioned officials by blood or adoption: mother, father, sister, half-sister, half-brother, son, daughter, aunt, must not be described as an uncle or nephew;

Note: All eligibility criteria are strictly adhered to. No exceptions. Eligibility criteria will not change at all during the admission process for applications. However, this information may change between the closing of one application process and the opening of another.

Benefits of the grant:

The JJ/WBGSP program provides the grantee with the following benefits:

  • The grantee candidates will be eligible to receive a grant from their home country and host country immediately after the start of the program and after the end of the grant period. economy class air ticket for flight between universities and in addition to this, they will receive an additional stipend in the amount of 500 USD for each trip;
  • The cost of the master's program fee and the basic medical insurance costs obtained through the university will also be covered;
  • A monthly stipend will be allocated to cover all living expenses (accommodation, food, etc., including books) of the winning candidates during the period of the grant. The amount of the scholarship varies depending on the host country.

Note: This grant will remain in effect until the winners complete their chosen program or for 2 years.
