New requirement for private higher educational institutions in Uzbekistan: at least 5,000 square meters of space

Ministers According to the decision of the court, now the total area of private higher education institutions owned or leased in Uzbekistan must be at least 5,000 square meters.

This requirement is aimed at creating a quality, stable and safe educational environment for students and teachers.

Why is 5,000 square meters important?

For such a large area by having it, the higher education institution will have the following opportunities:

  • spacious and modern classrooms;

  • laboratories for practical training;

  • sports halls and halls for cultural events;

  • libraries and study centers convenient for students;

  • Special areas for recreation and social activities.

How does the infrastructure affect the quality of education?

The quality of education depends not only on the teaching personnel and methods, but also on the level of physical infrastructure. Comfortable and modern conditions are important for the effective work of students and teachers.

Laboratory, library, sports and cultural facilities ensure the development of students outside of the educational process.

What opportunities does this create for private HEIs?

The new demand encourages higher education institutions to take a more responsible and professional approach. Through this:

  • approaching international standards;

  • increasing the quality of education;

  • increasing competitiveness among students;

  • there will be an opportunity to develop the country's education system.
