The National Humanities Center Fellowship is an opportunity for researchers to conduct research in Canada

Important news related to higher education institutions:

Brief content:
Program overview: The National Humanities Center offers an internship at the University of Guelph, Canada, for scientists and researchers in the field of humanities, regardless of nationality or citizenship. accepts the applications of scientists if they are considered useful for the program.
Benefits: Participants will have the following opportunities: monthly salary up to $70,000, depending on the situation of the Practitioner and the Center; Travel expenses may be covered for the intern and his/her immediate family members.

General information about the program:

The National Humanities center offers an opportunity for scholars and researchers conducting scientific research in the field of humanities, regardless of nationality and citizenship, to conduct an internship at University of Guelph, Canada. In addition to the humanities, the Center accepts applications from scientists in the fields of natural and social sciences, arts, professions and social life if they are considered useful for the program.

Benefits:

Participants will have the following opportunities:

  • Up to $70,000 per month depending on the situation of the Practitioner and the Center. salary;
  • Travel expenses may be covered for the practitioner and his close family members.

IMPORTANT! The center does not cover travel, accommodation and other expenses during the research.

Requirements:

  • Candidates must have completed a doctoral degree (or equivalent);
  • Candidates must conduct research in the humanitarian field. (If the scientist's research is in fields such as natural and social sciences related to the humanities, art, profession and social life, they can apply).

Details about the requirements HERE.

Application procedure:
The center will accept applications for the 2022-2023 academic year 2021 Accepts from July 1 until October 7, 2021. Scholarship candidates are asked to complete the online application form and upload the following documents:

  • 1000 word project proposal;
  • Brief bibliography (up to 2 pages);
  • CV/Curriculum Vitae (up to 4 pages);
  • One page outline of the project structure (if the project is in the form of a book, provide an outline of the sections; otherwise, provide a brief description of the components of the project and their implementation to date);
  • Full name, contact information of 3 recommenders. A request will be sent to the person giving the recommendation to upload the recommendation letter by October 7;
