Attention teachers: The certification fee is non-refundable

When is the attestation fee not returned?

Important rules for the attestation process for teaching staff are established, and these procedures are approved on the basis of the decision of the Cabinet of Ministers No. 572 (17.09.2021).

When is the attestation fee non-refundable?

In the following cases, the amount paid for the attestation is not refundable:

  • If you have refused the attestation
  • If you have not been able to defend the qualification category or position
  • If you have not successfully passed the attestation

Cases in which certification can be postponed to another period

If the pedagogue cannot participate in the certification for valid reasons, it can be postponed to another period. The valid reasons are as follows:

  • Incapacity for work (must be confirmed by medical documents)
  • Performance of state or public duties
  • Participation in national or international competitions
  • Temporary stay in a foreign country
  • Death of a close relative
  • A valid reference letter from authorized bodies or heads of educational organizations

In such cases, the payment will not be made again, only the certification date will be moved to another period.

What should you pay attention to before submitting the document?

  • Check the validity period of your type certificate
  • Find out whether the current certification is mandatory or optional
  • If the current certification is mandatory, but you if you apply for extraordinary attestation, your application will be rejected and the fee will not be returned

In case of providing incorrect information

If the pedagogue provides incorrect information or forged documents during the attestation process, the following measures will be taken: — Official Data

  • Documents will not be accepted
  • The attestation process will be canceled
  • The amount paid is non-refundable

Conclusion

  • To avoid problems during the attestation process, make sure you fully understand all the requirements and conditions.
  • Check the accuracy of your documents and follow all the rules to avoid losing funds due to negligence.
