About the KOICA organization in brief:

Korea International Cooperation Agency KOICA is the government's grant aid and technical cooperation programs to developing partner countries. It was established in 1991 by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of South Korea as a state organization for the purpose of assistance.

The purpose of the grant:

The main purpose of the KOICA Scholarship Program is to help prepare personnel from among the public employees of partner countries who can contribute to the economic development of their country. Uzbekistan is also part of the partner countries.

Who can apply for the grant:

  • Candidates must be officially recommended by the government of the Republic of Uzbekistan to participate in the KOICA program;
  • The following citizens of Uzbekistan, officially recommended by the government of the Republic of Uzbekistan to participate in the KOICA program, can apply for the grant:
    • State employees;
    • Employees of government organizations and enterprises,
    • Employees of banks, research institutes,
    • Teachers of public schools;
  • Candidates must have at least two years of formal work experience;
  • Candidates must be mentally and physically healthy, but not too great in physical health. candidates with disabilities can also apply;
  • Candidates must have a bachelor's degree;
  • Candidates must be able to spoken and written English, be able to understand classes and academic lectures conducted entirely in English, and write theses; can participate if a letter of recommendation is presented;
  • Candidates must not have been expelled from the KOICA program before;
  • They must not have previously participated in master's degree grant programs organized by the government of the Republic of Korea;
  • It is recommended that you check the other requirements in the application form of your chosen master's degree program.

Program benefits:

  1. Tuition fee is fully covered;
  2. Monthly KRW 999,000 (approx. 880-890 USD) stipend;
  3. Free housing;
  4. Free insurance;
  5. A one-time KRW 600,000 (approx. 530 USD) stipend to cover the expenses of candidates entering and settling in Korea;

Guidelines for submitting documents to the program and deadlines:

The deadline for receiving approved applications is March 26, 2021, after which the process will continue in the following stages:

Stage 1 - at the representative office of KOICA in Uzbekistan interview: from 29.03.2021 to 02.04.2021;

Announcement of results - 05.04.2021;

Stage 2 - review of documents received by candidates by KOICA head office and universities - From 08.04.2021 to 21.04.2021

Announcement of results: 26.04.2021;

Phase 3 - Interview and written exam by universities - 28.04.2021 from to 12.05.2021

Announcement of results: 14.05.2021

Stage 4 - Medical examination of selected candidates - 17.05.2021 - 18.06.2021

Declaration of results: 23.06.2021
