If the former school director is not elected as a deputy, will he be reinstated?

Useful information for applicants and students:

Summary:
According to the law, the deputy has the right to return to the position he previously held.
Conclusion: If the deputy is not re-elected in the election, he can, according to the law, be returned to the position of school director or appointed to a higher position.
The termination of the deputy status protects the labor rights of the deputy. provides.

According to the legislation of the Republic of Uzbekistan, parliament deputies have the right to return to their previous jobs after the end of their term of office. This right is guaranteed by the "Law on the status of a deputy of the Legislative Chamber of the Oliy Majlis of the Republic of Uzbekistan and a member of the Senate". According to Article 16 of the law, deputies have the opportunity to return to their previous position if they are not elected.

Guarantees of the deputy whose term of office has expired:

  1. Return to the previous job: If the deputy does not get enough votes as a result of the election, he will be returned to his previous position with his consent. It can be, for example, the position of a school director. According to the law, the deputy has the right to return to the position he previously held.

  2. Opportunity to work in a higher position: At the request of the deputy, he may be given a job higher than his previous position. This is done taking into account his experience and work performance.

  3. Cancellation of the employment contract: If the deputy wants to return to his previous position, the employment contract with the person appointed in his place will be canceled in accordance with Clause 9 of Article 168 of the Labor Code. This ensures that workplaces are managed in a legal manner.
