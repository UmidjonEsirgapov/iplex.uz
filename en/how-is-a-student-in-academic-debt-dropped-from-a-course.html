How is a student in academic debt dropped from a course?

The credit-module system has strict requirements for student attendance. If the student does not attend the scheduled classroom sessions enough, he is not included in the final examination and is considered to be in academic debt.

Main requirements for the credit-module system

  • Credit allocated for one subject - 6 credits
  • Hour equivalent of one credit - 30 hours
  • Total hours of one subject - 180 hours
  • Auditory training allocated hours for - 72 hours
  • Allocated hours for independent education - 108 hours

Conditions for student withdrawal from the course

  • If the student misses a quarter of the 72-hour classroom training (i.e. 18 hours), he will not be included in the final inspection.
  • Fail the final inspection the student is considered to be in academic debt.
  • A student in academic debt is dropped from the course.

Result

If the student is dropped from the course, he/she will have to re-study the subject in order to continue his/her education. Thus, a bachelor's student can stay at a higher education institution for up to 8 years.
