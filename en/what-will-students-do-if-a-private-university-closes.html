What will students do if a private university closes?

Important news related to higher education institutions:

Summary:
Opportunities for students Students studying at a private university whose license has been canceled or closed have the right to transfer their studies to other state and non-state HEIs. If a private university is closed, students can continue their studies at other state or non-state HEIs.

What will be the fate of students if a private university is closed?

According to the new decision of the president, all educational organizations will be state accredited every 4 years. If a higher education institution (HEI) fails to pass accreditation, its license is revoked and its activity is suspended.

Opportunities for students

  • Students studying at a private university whose license has been revoked or closed have the right to transfer their studies to other state and non-state HEIs.

  • Students will be transferred to educational programs suitable for the field and specialization they are studying.

  • In order to transfer to the state higher education institution, it is necessary to pass the test and collect the necessary points according to the established procedure.

Governments of the state

This decision ensures that the students' studies are not left half way. Necessary conditions are created for every student to continue his education.

Conclusion

If a private university is closed, students can continue their studies in other state or non-state HEIs. The most important thing is that the rights of students are fully protected and the continuity of the educational process is guaranteed. — Official Data
