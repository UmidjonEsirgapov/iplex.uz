General Information:

Science without borders challenge: This annual competition helps students to further improve their creative skills, as well as to inform the public about the need to preserve, protect and restore the world's oceans and water resources. Winners of this competition will be awarded scholarships of up to $500, and elementary and high school students aged 11-19 years old can apply. Everything is connected from land to sea. For this year's competition, you will be asked to submit a piece of art about what governments, non-profit organizations, park managers, and all local residents can do to save coral reefs. Eligibility:
This international art competition is open to all students aged 11-19. Students must be equivalent to elementary or middle school or home school. College and university students are not eligible for this contest.

Entries will be judged in two age categories, with prizes for 1st, 2nd and 3rd place each:

  • Students aged 11-14;
  • Students aged 15-19;

Deadline:
All entries must be received by 11:59 PM Eastern Time (ET) on March 7, 2022.

APPLY

Judging Criteria:
A panel of artists, educators, and scientists from Science Without Borders judges the competition. The artwork will be judged based on the quality, originality and appropriateness of the artwork. Judges use a scoring rubric to evaluate artwork.

As you create your artwork, you should think about the following questions:

  • Does your artwork reflect your understanding of the competition theme?
  • Does your creator's speech also demonstrate your understanding of the competition theme?
  • Does your artwork demonstrate your understanding of the art and the theme effectively? Does it show that you use artistic elements such as line, shape, form, value, composition, and color to depict?
  • Does your artwork demonstrate your original, inventive, and individual style?
  • Is your artwork clean? Are there tears, smudges, and/or smear marks?
  • Does your artwork evoke emotion in the audience? Will it be amazing and memorable?
