Conditions for transition to the HEMIS system for non-state universities have been approved

Decision of the Cabinet of Ministers dated December 30, 2024 In accordance with the decision No. 913, new procedures and rules were established for non-state higher education institutions. These changes are aimed at improving the quality of education, regulating management processes and introducing modern technologies.

The main requirements of the new order

  • Integration into the HEMIS system: Non-state universities must have an information system to manage their educational, scientific, and administrative processes. This system should be integrated into the HEMIS system according to the requirements set by the Ministry of Higher Education, Science and Innovation.

  • Entering data into state systems: Within one month after receiving the license, the following information must be entered into state information systems:

    • Full information of the educational organization.
    • Students and accurate and complete information about pedagogues.
    • The entered information must be regularly updated.

  • License requirements: Information on the activities of licensees must be entered into the information systems of the Ministry of Higher Education, Science and Innovation.

Why Is this change important?

These changes serve to improve the activities of non-state universities, increase the quality of education, and form a management system that meets international standards.

With the help of new procedures, the digital management capabilities of educational organizations will expand and they will have the opportunity to organize their activities more efficiently. — Source
