Answers to some problematic questions about admission processes

Useful information for applicants and students:

Brief content:
- Stateless persons can go to the nearest state higher education institution for registration and register. They can choose up to 5 undergraduate courses of non-military higher education institutions with the same range and sequence of subjects.
There are a total of 5,179 full-time courses, of which 1,175 are based on a state grant, 4,004 are on a fee-contract basis, and 1 are on the basis of part-time and evening education, respectively. 568 and 251 additional quotas were allocated.

I registered through the website of the State Test Center. Can I change my chosen fields of study?

- Applicants can change their address of permanent residence, the educational institution they graduated from, their preferences, and their chosen fields of study until the end of the registration period (July 20). Stateless persons can go to any state higher education institution nearest to them and register. I registered through the website of the State Test Center. Where can I get a permit to participate in the test?

Applicants can download the "Applicant's Permit" to participate in the test from their personal cabinet through the abitur.dtm.uz site after the registration is complete.

Applicants with "Persons with disabilities", "Blind persons", "Recommendation from the command of the military unit" or "Foreign language certificate" enter their privileges during registration.

- If it appears that your privilege is not available when you register, you should contact the ministry or office that gave you a recommendation.

Is an additional quota allocated for part-time education to applicants with a recommendation letter from the command of the military unit? According to the Commission's Statement No. 1, 5% additional quotas were allocated for applicants with a recommendation letter from the command of the military unit in the section of higher educational institutions.
