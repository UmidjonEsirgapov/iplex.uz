Habits shape a person's life. Acquiring habits often takes time and persistence. In this sense, the 21/90 challenge is popular among many people and helps people to form new habits. According to this method, it takes 21 days to form a new habit and an additional 90 days to strengthen it.

Learning a New Habit in 21 Days

According to the 21/90 challenge, if a person wants to form a new habit (for example, reading a book every day, running in the morning, or giving up sweets), he should first try for 21 days. During this period, it will be easier to learn a new activity and include it in everyday life. Research shows that 21 days is a sufficient period of time and is a key step in forming a new habit.

Consolidating a New Habit: The 90-Day Process

After a successful 21-day process, it is necessary to continue this activity for 90 days to strengthen the new habit. During this time, the newly learned habit will become an integral part of your future lifestyle. Because of this, this method is gaining popularity among many people, and those who have tried it have seen many positive results.

Support Tools: Mobile Apps and Notepads

There are also various mobile apps to facilitate the new habit. With these apps, you will be able to track and control your habits. You can also simply make plans on a notepad and mark your daily tasks. With this method, you will get extra power to motivate yourself and move forward.
