Neighborhood "seven" and their main tasks

Useful information for applicants and students:

Summary:

The main goal of the neighborhood "seven" is to provide targeted assistance to citizens and ensure socio-economic stability in the neighborhood.

The neighborhood "seven" system was established by the order of the head of our state, and each member has important tasks aimed at improving life in the neighborhood. Below we provide information about the representatives of the "seven" and their main activities:

1. Mahalla chairman

  • Duty: To help needy families to improve their housing, control the general condition of the neighborhood.

2. Assistant Mayor

  • Duty: Helping unemployed citizens to find a job, solving employment issues.

3. Youth leader

  • Duty: To ensure meaningful leisure time by involving young people in sports, music and various clubs.

4. Women activists

  • Task: To improve the economic situation of women by involving them in entrepreneurship, homemaking and crafts.

5. Prevention inspector

  • Duty: Prevention of crimes in the neighborhood, maintaining public order and ensuring the safety of citizens.

6. Tax officer

  • Duty: Expanding the tax base, legalizing business activities and helping to transfer self-employed people to the small business category.

7. Social worker

  • Duty: To provide social services to the lonely elderly, disabled and other needy people.
