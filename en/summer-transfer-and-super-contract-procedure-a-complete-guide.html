Summer transfer and super-contract procedure: A complete guide

Summer study transfer process is the focus of attention of applicants and students. There are several alternative options for students who fail the exam. Based on their capabilities and financial situation, they can choose one of the following ways:

A) Continue studying at the current higher education institution

If the student does not pass the summer transfer exam, he can continue his studies at the higher education institution he is studying at the same time. This way is the cheapest and simplest option. B) Transfer of studies based on super-contract. The super-contract is divided into three categories:

Type 1 super-contract If the student fails to reach the passing score of 4.05 points, the following rates apply:

  • Up to 1.05 points: 1.5 times

  • 1.06-2.05 points: 2 times

  • 2.06–3.05 points: 2.5 times

  • 3.06–4.05 points: 3 times

Example: If a normal contract is 7,400,000 soums, 1.5 points are not enough. the remaining student pays 14,800,000 soums.

Example: If the normal contract is 10,500,000 soums, then 10 times the super-contract = 105,000,000 soums.

Type 3 super-contract If the student less than 56.7 points has collected, he can transfer his studies on the basis of a super-contract. In this case:

  • It will be 2 times the amount of the 2nd category contract.

Example: If the price of the 2nd category is 105,000,000 soums, the super-contract for this category = 210,000,000 soums.

Important note: Regardless of how much less than the passing score, super-contract of category 2 and 3 is determined based on the same rate.

The student can independently calculate the price of super-contract based on his/her points. — Source
