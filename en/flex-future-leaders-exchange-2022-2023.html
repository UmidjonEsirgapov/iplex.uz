FLEX - Future Leaders Exchange 2022-2023

About the expected changes in the education system:

Benefits: The FLEX scholarship provides: • Domestic and international travel (from home to the US and back);• A monthly stipend to help students participate in social activities and with personal daily expenses;• One year of free living in the home of a selected and vetted US host family;• study at a US high school;• receive program preparation instructions;• participate in program activities organized in local US communities;• receive health insurance.

FLEX (Future Leaders Exchange — Future Leaders Exchange) program is fully funded by the US government. Organizational issues of the program are carried out by the Bureau of Educational and Cultural Relations of the US Government Department. The winners of the contest will be given a scholarship to study in America for one year free of charge and an opportunity to live in a host family selected on the basis of volunteering. The goal of this project is to establish friendly relations between Americans and representatives of other nationalities.

Applications for the program for the 2022-2023 academic year will be accepted from September 1.

Who can participate?

The school's 9-10-11th grade. students or 1st-2nd year college/high school students can participate in the program.

Benefits:

The FLEX Scholarship provides:

• Domestic and international travel (from home to the US and back);
• A monthly stipend to help students participate in social activities and with personal daily expenses;
• One in a selected and vetted US host family household live for free;
• attend a US high school;
• receive program preparation instructions;
• participate in program activities organized in local US communities;
• are provided with health insurance.

Requirements:

  • Candidates in 2005 To be born between January 1 and July 15, 2007;
  • At the time of submitting the documents, to be studying at school (grades 9-10-11) or college/lyceum (years 1-2);
  • To have good grades;
  • To be able to speak English well and be able to demonstrate it during the selection process;
  • Having a passport to go abroad;
  • Able to meet American visa requirements;
  • Has not stayed in America for more than 90 days during the last 5 years;
  • The candidate's parents have not applied for departure to America or should not already be there.

Application deadline: October 18 It lasts until 8:59.

Qualification process:

The FLEX program selection consists of three stages:

Round 1: Online application stage. In this round, an online application is submitted and an essay is written on 3 topics. Essays must be submitted within 10 DAYS after the start of the application process (For example: if the application process starts on September 5th, the application essay must be submitted by September 15th).

Round 2: After submitting the online application, students will be invited to take an online English test.

Round 3: The third, final stage of testing will begin in November. This round includes an interview, essays, questionnaires and another English test. Students who advance to this stage will be contacted by the American Councils office in their country to provide detailed information.

Eligibility Criteria: The FLEX Scholarship competition is free and transparent to students who meet the following requirements. Students with disabilities can also participate in it. Approximately 20 students with disabilities are selected as FLEX finalists each year. They are subject to the same requirements as other participants of the program. Students can apply for the program only once.
