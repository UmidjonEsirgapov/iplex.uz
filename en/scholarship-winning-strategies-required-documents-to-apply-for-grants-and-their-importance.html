Now we will try to answer some of the most frequently asked questions.

  1. What grants are there?
  • There are many types of grants. In particular, government grantsand special grants organized by the university are the most common grants today. Government grants have more stakeholders than other grants, meaning government grants are fully funded. Recipients of government grants often:
    • tuition fee waivers;
    • travel, insurance and visa expenses covered;
    • a monthly stipend;
    • in some cases, free accommodation and food.

  • Chevening UK Government Scholarship - the opportunity to study a master's degree in Great Britain for free, all costs are fully funded. Acceptance for this grant is currently open. You can find out more through this link.

    • DAAD scholarships: The opportunity to study in Germany on the basis of a full grant for master's and doctoral studies. 2. What should be important to win scholarships?

This question has become one of the most frequently asked questions. To win a grant, the first thing you need to do is to learn more about the grant. What kind of grant are you applying for, what are its requirements, is your age, degree, major suitable for the program? The most basic documents that are constantly requested for grants:

  • CV - biography. This is quite different from our biography in Uzbek, which usually consists of one or one and a half pages. You can learn the most important information about writing a CV through this link ;
  • Motivational letter - an essay of maximum 2 pages detailing the reasons why you are applying for this grant. In the motivational letter, it should be stated that the candidate can reveal his knowledge, potential and leadership skills, why he is applying for this course, what he can give to the chosen university, and why this candidate should be selected for the grant. IMPORTANT When we talked with many grant winners, they mentioned that the most important factor in winning a grant is the motivation letter. It will clearly and in detail reflect the candidate's goals, the benefits he/she can get from the grant program, and how it will benefit the candidate's future career. When writing a motivational letter, it is necessary to speak clearly, with details and facts rather than with general information. Because it is not possible to determine the level of the candidate through general information. Set a clear goal, what field are you in, how is the grant program related to your field, why do you want to participate in this grant? Give everything in detail;
  • Recommendation - a letter of recommendation from the university or workplace expressing the good aspects of the candidate. A letter of recommendation is also important in winning a scholarship because it can be used to gauge the candidate's suitability for the program. That's why, when preparing a motivational letter and a letter of recommendation, never copy other people's work, do not allow plagiarism. This will be noticed by the members of the examining board;
  • Diploma/Grades- a document representing the candidate's academic performance. In some cases, the price may play a role. Although this document is requested for almost all grants, the grade is not very important in winning the grant. There are many students with 90% marks, IELTS 8.0, but who did not win a grant. Therefore, if your grades are not what you want, do not lose heart, try. You can be accepted for a grant if you explain why your grades are not excellent in your motivation letter. Of course, this does not mean that it will happen even if I do not study. The better the grades, the higher your chances of winning.
  • Language certificate is a required document for many grants. Usually IELTS in English is at least 6.0. TOEFL iBT 90 or equivalent is required. A high language proficiency certificate hardly plays a role in winning a scholarship. If the language certificate is sufficient to study at that university and to understand the knowledge given. Therefore, candidates who have obtained 7.0 in IELTS should write a good motivational letter clarifying their goals and directions rather than trying to get 8+. But getting a higher score from them gives an advantage for admission to the program. Another important factor in the scholarship process is that the candidate is socially active. Because behind all great things lies the cultivation of a useful person for the society and the establishment of mutual relations.
