Russia: Partial and full grants for undergraduate and graduate programs and a monthly stipend of 1,700 rubles

Important news related to higher education institutions:

Summary:
Selection process for the scholarship: Foreign students who intend to apply for the scholarship program are not required to submit a separate application for the program.
Admission will continue until the following dates: Undergraduate: August 14, 2022 Graduate: August 14, 2022 How the scholarship will be awarded: Scholarship program candidates will be automatically awarded tuition fee waivers based on the performance of the candidates after the end of the entrance tests.

About the University and the scholarship program Briefly:

National Research University Higher School of Economics (in Russian: Natsionalnyy issledovatelskiy universitet «Vysshaya shkola ekonomiki») is a public research university founded in 1992 and its main department is located in Moscow, Russia. At the same time, 2 different types of grant programs are offered:

1. The HSE Golden Scholarship program - selects excellent international students admitted to the university's bachelor and master's programs based on entrance tests, and provides for full exemption from tuition fees and a monthly stipend of 1,700 rubles.

2. Tuition fee waivers (partial grants): selects excellent international students admitted to the university's undergraduate and graduate programs on the basis of entrance tests, and provides a certain percentage of tuition fee waivers based on their results. They simply apply for the undergraduate (here) or graduate (here) courses of their choice. After that, they have to pass special entrance tests of the university according to their chosen fields. Candidates must score high scores in the test (for undergraduate programs) orportfolio selection and interview (for master's programs) when taking entrance exams. Candidates with the highest scores will be automatically selected for the HSE Golden Scholarship . The amount of partially provided grants is determined as follows based on the candidates' scores in the entrance exams, and the specified percentage of the grant value is deducted from the tuition fee:

  • Bachelor:
    • 140-149 points - 25%;
    • 150-169 points - 50%;
    • above 170 points - 70%.
  • Master's degree
    • 50-59 points - 25%;
    • above 60 points - 50%.

Requirements: Candidates are required to submit a document to one of the university's bachelor's or master's levels and collect high points. There are no separate tests or exams for the scholarship. It is enough to indicate that you are a candidate for the grant during the application process.

At the same time, admissions for the university's fall 2022 academic programs are ongoing. Admission will continue until the following dates:

  • Undergraduate: August 14, 2022
  • Master: August 14, 2022

How the grant will be awarded:

Tuition fee waiver for grant program applicants automatically provided based on the candidate's performance after the tests. You can apply here.

Note: Grants are not awarded for undergraduate study programs in "Physics".
