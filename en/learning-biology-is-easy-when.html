Today, the number of applicants studying biology has increased significantly. The number of young people interested in biology in our country is increasing day by day. However, the majority of schoolchildren view biology as a complex subject and have difficulty learning and mastering it. So, the reason for this is the teacher's lack of pedagogical skills or the students' failure to study? Now, with these and other questions, we turn to Nodir aka Oljayev, an experienced teacher who has been training students in biology for nearly 15 years, senior teacher of the "Registon" educational center, leader of biology teachers in the educational center. Thank you, the interview is waiting for you!

- What subject do applicants who study biology have difficulty mastering? And what method do you use to teach this subject in an easy and understandable way?

– In many places, biology is taught in a traditional question-and-answer format. This is a really effective method. However, most students get tired of the question-and-answer session, their brains get strained, and the level of boredom increases. In addition to this method, I also use methods that enrich students' imagination and expand their worldview.

For example, I encourage my students to express the situations presented in sentences through various pictures, because biology is the external world, the existence in which we live. If you imagine every living thing in it, you will quickly learn the information, besides, the lesson process will be more interesting. You or other applicants may not have heard of the Smart Scheme. We invented this term ourselves. A smart scheme is a process of expressing concepts, topics, processes, laws in the form of a scheme, hiding some part of it, leaving it down and finding it together with my applicants. Later, such schemes are prepared by the students themselves, this method in the form of a game is both effective and adds energy to the lesson. Because people differ from each other down to their fingerprints, the level of mastery and intelligence are the same. Let's say that if the topic of plants is difficult for you, it will be your friend's favorite topic. That's why I try to explain all topics as creatively and interestingly as possible, to organize lesson processes in an unusual way. It has been very useful during my many years of experience.

My advice is that you should slowly build your biological outlook. How will it be? For example, what is the difference between a wild plant and a cultivated plant?
