An International Faculty will be opened in Bukhara in cooperation with the Belgian "United Brussels Institutes".

The latest news in the field of education is as follows:

Brief content:
The delegation of Uzbekistan was headed by the Deputy Minister of Higher and Secondary Special Education U. Begimkulov.
Bukhara State University Rector O. Hamidov and teachers of this university also participated in the event.
UBI Managing Director J. Lim noted that the Belgian side is ready to consider the prospects of cooperation with Bukhara State University and resolve all related issues.

Representatives of the Ministry of Higher and Secondary Special Education and the Embassy of Uzbekistan in the Benelux countries held online negotiations with the leadership of the Belgian United Brussels Institutes (UBI), "Dunyo" AA reports. Reporter.

Belgium's managing director of the UBI Institute J.Lim, practice director L.Kazda and business school dean, our compatriot M.Aminova took part in the negotiations.

Uzbekistan's delegation was headed by the deputy minister of higher and secondary special education U.Begimkulov. The event was attended by the rector of Bukhara State University O. Hamidov and the teachers of this university.

The purpose of the meeting is to discuss the issues of organizing the activities of the International Faculty under Bukhara State University. Information was given on the main principles of the concept of the development of the sector until 2030, the level of inclusion of young people in higher education, the number of universities, including branches of foreign educational institutions.

As noted, the government is making every effort to turn Uzbekistan into a unique educational center for the youth of Central Asian countries, and to create the necessary conditions for attracting foreign universities.

UBI Managing Director J. Lim Belgium side He noted that he is ready to consider the prospects of cooperation with Bukhara State University and resolve all related issues.

In turn, it was noted that UBI has great prospects for developing cooperation with Central Asian countries, especially Uzbekistan.

In turn, UBI stated that it is ready to expand its participation in the country through student exchange and the organization of training courses together with partner universities in Uzbekistan.

At the end of the event, it was agreed to sign the relevant memorandum as soon as possible to continue discussing issues of establishing cooperation, to create a legal basis for further steps.

UBI was established in April 1992 in Brussels as a non-profit higher education institution. It is the first English-language business school in Belgium to offer undergraduate and graduate programs approved by the UK's Middlesex University. Students from 50 countries of the world study at the university and receive diplomas from UBI and Middlesex higher education institutions. — Details here
