Eurasia Foundation (YJ) is accepting applications for internships for young leaders of civil society (FJYL) from potential host organizations. announces that he will. This opportunity is open to youth-oriented civil society organizations (CSOs) in Kazakhstan, Turkmenistan and Uzbekistan whose main mission is to connect young people with new young leaders.
FJYL practice is an activity carried out within the framework of the Social Innovation Program of Central Asia (SICA), a five-year program funded by the United States Agency for International Development (USAID) and implemented by YJ. The goal of the program is to develop a dynamic and responsive civil society in Central Asia. It is designed to develop sustainable professional relationships between leaders and civil society specialists in the region, and their tasks are focused on youth. FJYL INTERNSHIP receiving organizations share knowledge and experience in their field and cooperate to help students achieve their educational goals, develop their personal social projects and climb the career ladder. to 18:00, from Monday to Friday) should be held.

Who can participate?

  • The most active youth of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan between the ages of 18-30;
  • Young people who have several years of volunteering experience and are active in youth organizations, national associations and other university and local events.

Application Process: Any questions regarding the Young Civil Society Leaders Internship or the application process can be sent in writing to CAYLAapply@eurasia.org.

To apply, applicants must provide information about the organization, including its name, contact information, priorities, and the duration of the internship. The receiving organizations must also state the reasons for accepting the intern and make a statement about their motivation for the internship.
The selected receiving organizations will be informed by the SICA team and will be asked to submit the following documents before the end of the allocation process:

1) Letter on the appointment of a trainer- maximum 1 (one) page. This letter must be signed by the authorized head of the organization on the company's letterhead, and a curator must be appointed for the intern.
2) Resume of the trainer- a maximum of 2 (two) pages.

If additional information or clarification is required regarding the applications, the applicant will be notified in writing with the request to provide additional information and/or documents.

Applications will be evaluated according to the following criteria:
• Clarity of the organization's motivation to accept the intern
• Clear connection between the organization's activities and youth development
• Relevance of the specified mentor experience
• Description of the internship (responsibilities and time)
