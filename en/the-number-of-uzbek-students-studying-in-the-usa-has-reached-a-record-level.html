The number of Uzbek students studying in the USA has reached a record level

Today, the most relevant news in the field of education was:

Brief content:
According to the 2023 "Open doors" report on international education exchange, the number of Uzbek students studying in the United States in the 2022/2023 academic year is 1,000 increased.
According to the report, the number of international students in the United States exceeded 1 million last academic year, a 14 percent increase compared to the previous year, and a new high of 1,057,188 students.
In the 2021/2022 academic year, 188,753 American students studied abroad which is 13 times more than last year and more than half of the pre-pandemic level.

The number of Uzbek students studying in the United States has exceeded a thousand, the press service of the U.S. Embassy in Uzbekistan reports.

2023 "Open Doors" on international educational exchange According to the (Open doors) report, the number of Uzbek students studying in the USA in the 2022/2023 academic year has exceeded 1,000. This is a unique new record, and the indicator has increased by 72.6% compared to the previous academic year.

The report says that in the 2021/2022 academic year, 631 Uzbek students studied at US higher education institutions, and by the 2022/2023 academic year, there the number of studying Uzbeks was 1,089.

According to the report, the number of international students in the US last academic year exceeded 1 million, an increase of 14% compared to the previous year, and the new high was 1,057,188 students.

The Institute of International Education (IIE) and the US Department of State's Bureau of Educational and Cultural Affairs (ECA) The Open Doors 2023 program underlines that the US remains a top destination for international students. Continuing to recognize the United States' global leadership in higher education, these students come to America from nearly every country in the world.

In the 2021/2022 academic year, 188,753 American students studied abroad, a 13-fold increase from the previous year and more than half of the pre-pandemic level.
