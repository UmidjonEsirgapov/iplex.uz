Rules in Japanese Schools: Discipline and Discipline for Students

Japanese schools are known for their strict rules and demanding high discipline from students. Below you can familiarize yourself with the basic rules that apply in Japanese schools.

1. Prohibition of romantic relationships

Romantic relationships are not allowed in Japanese schools. Students are required to focus only on education.

2. No school cafeteria

Japanese students do not have school cafeteria. Meals organized by the state are brought to the school and students are obliged to use them and eat the whole meal.

3. No summer vacation

There is no summer vacation in Japanese schools. 5-week vacations are offered to students at different times of the academic year, and teachers continue their education even then.

4. Students act as janitors

There are no janitors in Japanese schools. Students clean the school themselves, which teaches them to maintain order and cleanliness.

5. Normal appearance requirement

Students are not allowed to use various hairstyles and varnishes. They should look normal and natural.

6. School uniform

After primary grades, students will start wearing school uniform.

7. Swimming is mandatory

Every Japanese student must know how to swim. Special swimming lessons are held in schools, and students who have not learned this art receive additional lessons.

8. Emphasis on equal talent

In Japanese schools, every student is considered equal. No one is allowed to stand out with his talent. The concept of "There is no student without talent" is a priority in the Japanese education system. — Official Data
