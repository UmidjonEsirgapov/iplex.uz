Training in Peace and Conflict: 5-day fully funded training in Kyrgyzstan by the OSCE Academy

Important news related to higher education institutions:

Summary:
This training will be held entirely in English.
Benefits: All expenses will be fully covered, including travel expenses, accommodation and food expenses.
Requirements for candidates: five Central Asian republics (including Uzbekistan), small and medium-level specialists of civil society, non-governmental non-profit organizations and government organizations from Afghanistan and Mongolia; Researchers, activists and experts from these countries can also apply; Completed a bachelor's or master's degree in the social sciences or other related field; Full participation in the training, with 5 days dedicated to intensive training (attending lectures, participating in all assignments in the morning, afternoon and some evenings). Admission processes are underway for the topic-based training. During this training, participants will be fully acquainted with the solutions and methods of changing the resistance in their directions. This training will be held entirely in English.

Benefits:

  • All expenses will be fully covered, including travel expenses, accommodation and food expenses will be fully covered.

Requirements for candidates:

  • Five republics of Central Asia. (including Uzbekistan), small and medium-level specialists of civil society, non-governmental organizations and government organizations from Afghanistan and Mongolia;
  • Researchers, activists and experts from these countries can also apply;
  • Graduated with a bachelor's or master's degree in social sciences or other related fields;
  • Dedicated 5 days to intensive training (participating in lectures, mornings, afternoons, and some evenings) participate fully in the training.

How to apply

If you wish to apply for the training, please send the following documents in single PDF format to Venera Karasartova, research and training assistant: training@osce-academy.net titled “Training in Peace and Conflict” without .CV (in English), no more than 2 pages;

  • Letter of interest (in English and no more than 600 words) explaining how this training will benefit your professional or academic career. In the letter of interest, please indicate your nationality for registration.

Note:

  • A short 2-minute video about the conflict situation you faced and what you did to resolve it.

Applications will not be accepted after the deadline (25.03.2022). Also, the winners will be announced at the end of March. — More information
