The number of applicants for the last 15 years: dynamics from 2011 to 2025

Demand for higher education: initial growth years (2011-2016)

More than 418 thousand applicants in 2011 Submitted documents to HEIs. This indicator steadily increased year by year:

  • 2012 - 432,017 people

  • 2013 - 480,407

  • 2014 - 543,155

  • 2015 - 605,836

  • 2016 - 663,304

During this period, the desire to get higher education was strong, but online systems were not yet established.

A period of sharp growth and a record year. (2017–2020)

The number of applicants increased rapidly between 2017 and 2020. Especially in 2019-2020, this indicator reached a record level:

  • 2017 - 729,950

  • 2018 - 675,049

  • 2019 - 1,066,925

  • 2020 - 1,484,085 people (record)

In 2020, online registration was launched for the first time due to quarantine. This created convenience for young people in all regions and dramatically increased the number of participants.

Natural decline and real market needs (2021–2025)

Starting from 2021, the number of applicants began to gradually decrease:

  • 2021 - 948,211

  • 2022 — 1,214,232 people

  • 2023 — 1,018,007 people

  • 2024 — 894,279 people

  • 2025 — 732,411 people

This is caused by the following factors:

  • Decreased demand for extramural education (the main need has been met);

  • Increase in the number of non-state HEIs;

  • Increased interest in technical schools and colleges;

  • Profession the activity and popularization of educational centers;

  • A conscious approach to higher education is being formed.

The attitude towards higher education is changing in society

Now the concept that "higher education is a guarantee of success in life" is gradually replaced by the idea of "acquiring practical skills and a profession". is releasing. Many young people are more inclined to study a profitable profession than a diploma.

Conclusion

In 2011-2025, the number of applicants increased twice, and then gradually decreased again. These changes are closely related to reforms in education policy, digitization, and changes in values ​​in society.
