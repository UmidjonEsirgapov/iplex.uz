More than 10 years have passed since these words were said. Following my teacher's advice, I tried to study seriously and go abroad to see the world. As a student, based on my past years of experience, I was convinced that my teacher was right. Searching, finding opportunities, and certainly achieving his goal...

It is not easy to get into the world's best educational institutions. The demand for quality things will be strong. This means that there are many people who want to study in these educational institutions, and accordingly, the conditions of admission are difficult and the prices of education are expensive. First of all, you need to gather enough information about your chosen field. Get complete information on what colleges or universities offer study in this field, what documents are required for admission. Nowadays, all the information about this can be easily found on the university web pages. In the next step, you need to prepare these documents.

As you know, foreign students are required to take special TOEFL or IELTS tests to check their English language proficiency. If you're applying for a master's or graduate program, there are additional tests in the US, such as the GRE or GMAT. You must submit these. Another important aspect of this process is finding grants to cover the cost of your studies. An ordinary Uzbek child like you and me cannot afford to come to America and live or study in these expensive cities. But do not lose hope, because there is a solution. One of the unique advantages of studying abroad is that there are many special programs that offer educational grants if your financial situation does not cover the costs of living and studying here. Usually, each university has special grant programs for students, especially for international students.

In addition, there are grant programs that cover all expenses in full or partially for special foreigners or for some countries, for example, from Uzbekistan. For example, the American government has established special educational programs for Uzbeks in more than 15 areas that provide full grants.
