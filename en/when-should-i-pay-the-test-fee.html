Fee for Tests for Applicants: Important Information and Deadlines

Entrance to higher education institutions fee for tests is a hot topic that interests many applicants every year. Find out about the important aspects of this process, payment deadlines and who is exempt.

Payment Terms and Amount

The test is free for those who have graduated this year. This benefit is for graduates to test their knowledge. creates additional convenience.

Those who graduated from the educational institution in previous years must pay 187,500 soums to participate in the tests. This amount is half of the "minimum" (the amount of the basic calculation).

Consequences of non-payment

What will happen if you do not make the payment? Applicants who have not paid the prescribed fee for the tests registration is considered not passed and will not be put to the tests. Therefore, it is important to complete this process on time.

Possibility of Paying the Fee in Installments

For the convenience of the applicants, it is allowed to make the payment in installments. However, the full amount of the fee must be paid by June 25 at 11:59 p.m.. The date of registration is not important, the main condition is that the payment is made in full by the deadline.

Important Notice!

We recommend not to postpone the payment until the last date. Prevention of surprises in banking and online payment systems jobs, or due to the increase in last-minute payers, the load on the servers may increase. This may cause you to be unable to make a payment. Therefore, completing the payment before the due date is a guarantee of peace of mind for yourself.

Who is Exempted from Fees?

Some categories of applicants are exempted from the test fees. These are:

  • Those who graduated from the educational institution in the current year.
  • Alumni of the House of Mercy and Children's Towns.
  • Persons on full state support.
  • Persons with disabilities.
  • Persons included in the unified register of social protection and their children.
  • Young people included in the **"Youth register"**.

Conclusion
