Applications for the Humphrey Fellowship program in the USA are being accepted. This was reported by the country's embassy in Uzbekistan.

The program allows for 10 months of study at the master's level without a scientific title.

Scholarships are allocated on a competitive basis to active community specialists who have management skills and work in state and public organizations or the private sector. are offered a graduate-level course of study that matches their professional interests.

At the same time, program participants are encouraged to participate in professional development activities.

These include consultations and collaborations with US faculty and experts, site visits, workshops, research projects, and useful strategy development processes.

Applicants to the program have: Must have:

  • Bachelor's degree
  • At least five years of professional work experience;
  • Completed higher education equivalent to a US bachelor's degree;
  • Leadership qualities;
  • Proficiency in English.

Also, have resided in the US for more than six months within the last five years. Those who do not will not be able to participate in this program.

Economic development, finance and banking, communication and journalism, law and human rights, agriculture, natural resources are among the required fields of study. Details on directions are here.

Official transcripts in English and two letters of recommendation must be submitted online. A certificate of English language proficiency is not required. However, it can be attached if available.

Selection Process

After the application deadline, all applications received will be reviewed by a panel of embassy staff and former fellows.

Approved candidates will be invited to an interview at the US Embassy. The best candidates are recommended for scholarships by the embassy.

The grant covers:

  • expenses for university entrance exams;
  • J-1 (Exchange Program) visa assistance;
  • round-trip economy class airfare;
  • medical insurance;
  • accommodation. expenses;
  • pre-study introduction program.

Applications should be sent through the link until June 9 of this year.
