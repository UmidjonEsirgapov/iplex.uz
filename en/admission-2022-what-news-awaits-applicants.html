The applicant assessment criterion has changed, which also changes the scope of 5 fields of study.

Now, like last year, applicants are not given 3.1 points for both subjects in the specialty block, but 2.1 points are given to the 2nd subject. Officials explained why such a change was introduced.

On April 26, a press conference was held with the participation of officials of the State Test Center and the Ministry of Higher and Secondary Special Education on the issues of this year's admission. At the conference, it was explained that the criteria for evaluating applicants has changed this year.

"If the same points were given to 2 subjects in the specialty block last year, this year the first subject will be given 3.1 points, and the second subject will be given 2.1 points. There is a reason for such a change. For example, mathematics. Young people with higher knowledge of physics should enter the field of mathematics. In the previous year, there were cases of the opposite: there were cases when a child who knew physics better entered mathematics.

In addition, giving the same points after the end of one semester leads to situations where students try to transfer their studies to the direction they know better. Therefore, the highest point in the field of study was taken as a criterion, and this does not cause inconvenience to the applicant," said Abduvali Khalikov, head of the general department of the Ministry of Higher and Secondary Special Education. What is targeted admission?

Targeted admission is a quota allocated due to the lack of personnel in some territory and directions.

It is up to the applicant to apply for the targeted quota or not: if he wishes, the document at the time of submission, he must specify that he wants to participate in the target admission.

The target quota is different from the general quota, in which it is possible to study on the basis of a state grant even with a lower score. But later, the student must work in his region for a certain period of time and only after that he will receive his diploma.
