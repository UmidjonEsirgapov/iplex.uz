What awaits freshmen in their first week at university?

About the expected changes in the education system:

Brief content:
The new academic year at the university begins with an opening event for first-year students.
During this event, students are given the necessary information about their faculty and majors, and they are also divided into groups.
A tutor (group leader) is assigned to each group, and he deals with students' questions, suggestions and problems.

For first-year students, the new academic year at the university begins with an opening event. During this event, students will be given the necessary information about their faculties and majors, and they will also be divided into groups. Each group is assigned a tutor (group leader), who deals with students' questions, suggestions and problems.

A number of school concepts change their names in the university:

  • Class - course,
  • Classroom - auditorium,
  • Director - rector,
  • Class leader - tutor.

Lessons also change significantly. 45-minute school lessons will be replaced by 80-minute lessons, and subjects will be divided into theoretical and practical parts. For example, public speaking is theoretical in the first lesson, and practical in the next. — Details here

Advice for first-year students:

  1. Be active - Do not miss various clubs and seminars at the university in addition to classes.
  2. Network - Establishing relationships with teachers and professors is for your future career. It is important.
  3. Pay attention to your health - Poor diet can lead to stomach diseases as a student, so maintain a healthy lifestyle.
  4. Organize independent study - At the university, teachers only give guidance, the rest depends on you to work on yourself.
