Russia Increases the number of quotas for students from Central Asian countries in the 2023-2024 academic year. This was announced today, May 16, by the Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Russian Federation, Mikhail Galuzin, at the III Central Asia Conference of the Valdai International Debate Club.

Galuzin said that in the 2023-2024 academic year, the quota for teaching Central Asian students will be significantly increased, with an additional 200 places for citizens of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan, and for Tajiks. an additional 152 places will be allocated for Uzbek students.

Also, the deputy minister noted that currently about 185,000 students from Central Asian countries are studying in the Russian Federation, of which about 68,000 students are being educated at the expense of the Russian budget.

In addition, Galuzin will open branches of leading Russian universities in a number of Central Asian countries in the near future. He added that it is planned to open.

He said that in Kyrgyzstan it is planned to open branches of the leading higher educational institutions of Russia: Lomonosov Moscow State University, Kazan Federal University and the Higher School of Economics, and in Uzbekistan the Russian Humanitarian University, the Moscow State University of Geodesy and Cartography and the North Caucasus Federal University. In addition, the project to establish a joint Russian-Turkmenistan university in Ashgabat is currently at the stage of agreement.

"All this will be a solid addition to the 25 branches of our universities operating in the region," concluded Galuzin.

We remind you that earlier, Galuzin, who participated in the III Central Asia Conference of the Valday international discussion club, said that some of the Central Asian countries may join the Western sanctions against Russia. he said. — Official Data
