A new benefit for students: Dormitory fees are paid by the state

Today, the most relevant news in the field of education was:

Brief content:
From 2026, students will be covered by the state for the cost of living in student housing. approved.
Children of families included in the "Social Register" Children of families included in the "Social Register" studying in the form of full-time education can use this privilege.
How will the payments be made?

Starting from 2026, students will be covered by the state for TTJ expenses

2026 On January 28, the Government's decision No. 32 was adopted, and the procedure for covering living expenses of certain categories of students in **student residences (TTJ)** from the state budget was approved. This decision is an important measure of support for children of families in need of social protection.

Which students can use this benefit?

Children of families included in the "Social Register"

This benefit can be used by children of families included in the "Social Register" who are studying in the form of full-time education. This process is not affected by the fact that the student studies on a grant or contract basis.

Students studying in the form of full-time education

According to the decision, only students studying in the form of full-time education can use this opportunity. Forms of external or distance education are not included in this procedure.

Procedure of placement in student accommodation

Admission to TTJ without queue and priority

According to the decision, privileged students are admitted to student accommodation without queue and priority. This will significantly reduce problems related to accommodation for students.

What if there is no state dormitory?

If there is no TTJ in a state higher education institution, students can be accommodated in residences built on the basis of public-private partnership. In this case, the living expenses are also covered by the state.

How are the payments made?

Payment by plastic card every month

Payments for the living expenses of students at TTJ are transferred to the student's bank plastic card by the 15th of every month. This process is carried out on the basis of a transparent and convenient system.

Conclusion

Government Decision No. 32 creates a great opportunity for children of families in need of social protection. The government covers the students' living expenses in the TTJ, making their education process easier and reducing financial pressure. This decision serves to ensure equality and social justice in higher education.
