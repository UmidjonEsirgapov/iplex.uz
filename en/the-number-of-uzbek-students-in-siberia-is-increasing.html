The number of Uzbek students in Siberia is increasing

Foreigners have increased among the students of the leading universities of the Siberian region of Russia. A number of universities have lost their attractiveness for the youth of Kazakhstan. But the number of applicants from Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan and Kyrgyzstan is increasing. At the same time, Siberian universities are trying to ensure the flow of students from China.

The admission campaign at Siberian universities has ended. According to its results, changes were noted in the "geographical" composition of applicants from foreign countries.

According to the information given by the vice-rector for educational affairs of Novosibirsk State Technical University (NDTU) Sergey Chernov, foreigners submitted a total of about 3,000 applications for admission. About 2,500 of them were admitted to the university. "For the second year in a row, Kazakhstan fell to second place. Uzbekistan was the first," Chernov said.

This year, 1,150 foreigners entered Tomsk State University (TSU), which is equal to last year's figure. According to Yevgeny Pavlov, the head of the admissions department of TSU, the number of applicants from Kazakhstan has decreased here, but it still ranks first in terms of the number of foreigners admitted to the university - about 500 people. "The number of applicants from Kyrgyzstan has doubled. That's more than 100 people. "Uzbekistan and Tajikistan have maintained their indicators," said Pavlov.

In 2023, the number of foreign applicants from the CIS countries to Krasnoyarsk Siberian Federal University (SFU) has slightly decreased. However, at the same time, according to Margarita Kuznetsova, the responsible secretary of the selection commission, "Mongolia and Turkmenistan are entering the market." Alexander Denisevich, head of the admissions department of Tomsk Polytechnic University (TPU), said: "This year, we are noticing a significant increase in demand from Turkmenistan." If their number was 347,000 on October 1, 2021, it reached 362,000 a year later. In 2023, the Russian government increased the number of quotas for foreign students to 30,000.

At the same time, Kazakhstan remains in first place in terms of the number of foreign students in Russian universities. According to the Ministry of Education and Science of Russia, 61,000 citizens of Kazakhstan are studying in Russian higher education institutions and their foreign branches in bachelor's, specialty and master's degrees. The next places are occupied by Uzbekistan (48.7 thousand), China (32.6 thousand), Turkmenistan (30.6 thousand) and Tajikistan (23.1 thousand). — Official Data
