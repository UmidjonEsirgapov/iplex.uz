Denmark: Partially funded grant program for undergraduate studies

Important news for students and applicants has been announced.

Summary:
Brief about the grant program: Aalborg University (AAU) in Denmark announces a partially funded grant program called Nordplus Scholarships for outstanding international students to study in one of the university's undergraduate courses in the academic year 2021-2022.
University Times It ranks #201-250 in the ranking of international universities published by Higher Education (THE).
Benefits of the scholarship: Scholarships in the amount of DKK 7,000 (US$1,110) per semester will be allocated to students who win the scholarship.

Brief about the scholarship program:

Aalborg University (AAU) in Denmark is an excellent international university announces a partially funded Nordplus Scholarships program for students to study in one of the university's undergraduate courses in the academic year 2021-2022. The university is ranked #201-250 in the ranking of international universities published by Times Higher Education (THE).

Benefits of the grant:

Stipends in the amount of DKK 7,000 (1,110 US dollars) per semester will be allocated to the students who won the grant. And students are required to study with excellent grades to keep the grant.

Requirements:

  • To be able to apply for the grant, candidates must first apply here to one of the university's undergraduate courses and be accepted;
  • After that, this link will be able to apply separately;
  • Candidates must have one of the following language certificates:
    • IELTS (academic test + special online home test): 6.5 overall band score;
    • TOEFL (internet-based + TOEFL iBT Special Home Edition): 85;
    • C2 Proficiency (CPE): 180/ level C1;
    • C1 Advanced (CAE): 180/ grade C;
    • ECPE: Passed test including certificate
