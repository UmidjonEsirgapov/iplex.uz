Fact 4. In Europe, exams similar to the modern exams appeared in medieval universities to obtain bachelor's and master's degrees. The results of these exams are amazing: For example, at the beginning of the 16th century at the University of Leipzig, 30 out of 100 people passed the intermediate exam, and only 6 people managed to reach the final exam. Fact 5. Exams first appeared in the Russian Empire during the time of Peter the Great. The school of E. Fact 6. Many people know that Lev Tolstoy did not have a higher education, but few know why. The fact is that young Leo was afraid of a difficult exam. He studied at the Faculty of Law, although he was not an exemplary student, and in the spring of 1849 he began to take the exam for the candidate of legal sciences (certified lawyer) in St. Petersburg. He successfully passed two exams, but decided not to pass the third one and left for his village. It must be admitted that the lack of a diploma did not affect the future fate of the great writer.
