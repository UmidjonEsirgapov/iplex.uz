The IES Program is a fully and partially funded exchange program in Germany for all students

Useful information for applicants and students:

Summary:
Good knowledge of English or German is required to participate in the program.
For the summer semester, applications can be submitted from April 1 to June 15.
COPERNICUS BERLIN covers the following expenses of the program participants: One-time welcome bonus pocket money is given every month; 1 semester is free of charge and insurance, study trips during the program, activities, language courses and accommodation are paid directly by COPERNICUS BERLIN; Travel and medical insurance costs are not covered.

International Excellence Scholarship Program (IES) - provides students from Eastern and South-Eastern Europe, the Caucasus and Central Asia with the opportunity to study in Germany for free for one semester.

Students admitted to this program study at the best German universities (HU Berlin, FU Berlin, HWR Berlin, They study at Potsdam University). They will have the opportunity to live with German-speaking families for 6 months, as well as get to know German culture through cultural events and social meetings. Good knowledge of English or German is required to participate in the program. Applications are accepted for winter semester from October 1 to December 15. For the summer semester, you can apply from April 1 to June 15.

Benefits of the program.

COPERNICUS BERLIN offers full and partially funded grants under the IES program.

Fully funded grant. It covers the following expenses of the program participants:

  • They are given a one-time welcome fee (350 euros) and monthly pocket money (1080 euros);
  • Travel and medical insurance expenses are covered;
  • 1 semester free study and insurance, educational trips during the program, activities, language courses and accommodation. accommodation fees are paid directly by COPERNICUS BERLIN;

Partially funded grant. In this case, COPERNICUS BERLIN covers the following expenses of the program participants:

  • They are given a one-time welcome fee and monthly pocket money;
  • 1 semester of free study and insurance, study trips, activities, language courses and accommodation during the program are paid directly by COPERNICUS BERLIN; In this case, travel and medical insurance costs are not covered.

All participants study at the university for free, undergo an 8-week internship, and are given the necessary advice. Most importantly, they are provided with free accommodation by German families.

Requirements:

  • Candidates from Albania, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Belarus, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Georgia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Kosovo, Moldova, Mongolia, Montenegro, North Macedonia, Russia, Serbia, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, Ukraine, Must be studying at a bachelor's, master's or doctorate level in one of the countries such as Uzbekistan; must not be older than 28; must have at least B1 German or B2 English; must be socially active. Required documents:
    • CV;
    • School grades and their English or German translation;
    • University grades and their English or German translation.

    NOTE! A language certificate is not required when submitting a document. Candidates can be admitted to the program even without a certificate by presenting themselves during the interview.
