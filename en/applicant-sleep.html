What is sleep? When we sleep, our body is in full "self-healing, self-repair" mode, and while we dream, our brain is busy repairing all the parts of our body. Just as a disease is cured, sleep also cures the "diseases" of tiredness acquired during the day in the human body. The body needs enough time, i.e. enough sleep, for this self-restoration process to be fully implemented. If the body does not rest enough time, it will not be able to fully restore itself, the fatigue will not be fully repaired, and as a result, we will be sleepy and walk around like a sick person during the day. Many things may seem more interesting to us when we are awake than when we are sleeping, but we must not forget that the time a person spends in sleep is very useful for his health. How many hours of sleep is enough for someone depends on our "biological clock" located in our body. Everyone has such a watch. For some people, 9 hours of sleep per day is considered the average rest time, while others can stay refreshed all day even with 7 hours of sleep. If even 1 hour of sleep falls short of the level required by your biological clock, this situation can cause you to lose focus during the day, lose your memory, get easily distracted, and even overeat and gain weight.

Human sleep patterns are very difficult to change. For example, if you have been going to bed at 10 pm for several months, but suddenly you change your bedtime to 12 pm or even later, your body will have a hard time adjusting to this change. Therefore, it is necessary to regulate and organize sleep as much as possible.

Mostly, applicants study hard during the week, study without sleeping at night, and on weekends, for example, on Sunday, they try to sleep all day long. With this, they try to pay off the "debt" of a week's sleep. That's right, if you've gone into debt by cutting back on sleep for a week, you can pay it back by getting more sleep on the weekend. But remember, you can't get rid of a week's worth of sleep debt in one day! Therefore, try not to have too much sleep debt.

HOW LONG SHOULD YOU SLEEP? The quality of sleep is more important than the duration of sleep. You can sleep 9 hours in uncomfortable conditions and feel tired during the day. But a person who rests for 5 hours in a very comfortable, dark, cool, quiet and noiseless room can feel much better than you. Therefore, pay attention not to the number of hours of sleep, but to the quality. Keep your sleeping area clean, make sure the room is airy, dark and protected from noise. No one should wake you up unnecessarily during your sleep. If your sleep is interrupted several times during the night, your sleeping pattern may be disturbed and as a result, your body will not be able to complete the process of self-restoration.

How do you know your biological clock and how many hours of sleep you need per night? The following situations indicate that a person did not sleep enough at night and generally does not observe the sleeping hours:

  • if an alarm clock is necessary to wake up on time;
  • if it is necessary to nap for a few minutes even after turning off the alarm clock;
  • if it is very difficult to get up in the morning;
  • mornings are relaxed and only if he is busy with dreams about lying down;
  • falls asleep in warm rooms, meetings and classes, especially when the teacher is talking;
  • falls asleep in the car and after eating;
  • if it is not possible to continue working without taking a nap;
  • falls asleep quickly in the evening and falls asleep while watching TV;
  • rest if you need to sleep all day on weekends;
  • falls asleep within 5 minutes of going to bed.

If you have at least three of the above situations, then you are not getting the hours of sleep you need. This can be very dangerous for you. For now, you can continue to study while struggling with the above conditions, but soon the above condition will cause your health to deteriorate. Try to organize your sleep time as much as possible.
