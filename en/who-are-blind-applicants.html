Who are blind applicants?

Today, the most relevant news in the field of education was:

Brief content:
According to the law, the following categories are considered blind applicants: Persons with a certificate of completion of secondary education (on the basis of the 11th grade) given by special boarding schools for the blind; Persons with a diploma for the blind issued by specialized vocational colleges for persons with disabilities; Persons who have studied at a special boarding school for the blind for 9 years and have a diploma of a 3-year secondary special vocational educational institution; Individuals who have a certificate or diploma from an ordinary school or lyceum, a college, and have a certificate of group 1 visual disability.
Entrance exams for this category of applicants will be held until July 26.

According to the law, the following categories of visually impaired applicants are:

  • Persons with a certificate of completion of secondary education (on the basis of 11th grade) issued by special boarding schools for the blind;
  • Persons with a diploma issued for the blind by specialized vocational colleges for persons with disabilities;
  • 9 years of study at a special boarding school for the blind and 3 years of secondary special, persons with a diploma of a vocational educational institution;
  • persons with an ordinary school or lyceum, college certificate or diploma, with a certificate of group 1 visual disability.

Entrance exams for this category of applicants will be held until July 26. — Source
