Three twin children of the same family became students on the basis of a state grant

In the Yusupov family living in the city of Tashkent, three twin children - Fatima, Zuhra and Zahro - were recommended to study on the basis of a state grant in the first attempt. According to Yusupova, the investment spent on children's future always pays off.

Three twin daughters of the Yusupov family became students of prestigious universities in Tashkent.

Three twin children of the Yusupov family living in Tashkent - Fatima, Zuhra and Zahro - were recommended to study on the basis of a state grant at the first attempt.

In particular, Fatima entered the University of Oriental Studies, Zahro and Zuhra entered the University of World Languages.

According to the head of the family, history and law teacher Sanjar Yusupov, it was not easy to achieve and required a lot of effort. From the 7th grade, we began to study mathematics and English in depth. When we graduated from the 9th grade, we saw that our daughters were interested in foreign languages, and then we directed them in that direction," said the father.

After the results of the test exams came out on August 21, the happy father said that he was very excited.

"We waited a week for the results, my daughters were not satisfied with anything, they were excited. We waited until August 21, but the results did not come out. In the middle of the night there was a noise, and when I opened my eyes, they said, "We have entered the study." I still didn't believe it. After seeing the results with my own eyes, I believed... (my passion) is hard to explain. It is a great happiness for parents that their child is enrolled in a grant, I hope that every parent will feel this," said Sanjar Yusupov.

According to the words of the housewife Shahlo Yusupova, the investment spent on the children's future always pays off. — Source
