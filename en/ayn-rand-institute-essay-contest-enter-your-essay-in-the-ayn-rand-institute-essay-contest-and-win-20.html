Have you ever read the works of Ayn Rand in your life? Right now, there is an opportunity for you to participate in the Ayn Rand Institute essay contest and win a cash prize. Since 1986, ARI has been announcing a selection of essays on Ayn Rand's scientific works. Prizes in the amount of $2.2 million have been awarded to the winners of the contest so far. This year, over $40,000 in cash prizes will be awarded to hundreds of winners. Candidates will be asked to write an essay on one of 3 topics. Learn more about the topic names here.

Evaluation criteria: The written essays should be based on the student's ability to think critically, whether they fully agree with the writer's ideas, and the essays should be clear, clear, and well-organized. The winning essays must also reflect the spirit of nationalism of the national anthem of ARI.

Requirements:

  • This competition is for all citizens of the country;
  • The candidate must be at least 13 years old, that is, a student of the 8th, 9th, 10th, 11th, 12th grade (if some candidates are younger than 13 years old, they can participate if you intend to write essays@aynrand.org);
  • Essays must be written between 600-1200 words;
  • Each candidate has the right to submit only 1 essay;
  • Candidates must write all essays themselves, otherwise they will be excluded from the competition if they are found to have copied;
  • All essays It should be uploaded by August 2022;
