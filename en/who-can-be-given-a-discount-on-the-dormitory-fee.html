To whom are the benefits for the dormitory fee?

The benefits for the dormitory fee are determined based on the decision of the Higher Education Institution (HEI) Council. These benefits are provided in two main forms based on the social status and needs of students.

Those who are completely exempt from payment

Students in the following categories are 100% exempt from the dormitory fee:

  • Students with group 1-2 disabilities due to eye diseases;

  • Child orphans (both parents died ) and orphans (one of their parents died);

  • Children of orphanages;

  • Students in need of social protection.

Those who receive a 50% discount

The following students have the right to receive a discount of half of the dormitory fee:

  • Disabled people of the 2nd group according to general diseases;

  • Chairman of the Student Council, floor captain, and student-employees responsible for the dormitory library who perform their duties in an exemplary manner.

Conclusion

Reliefs in the dormitory fee are important for strengthening the social protection of students and creating financial relief during their studies. Every student should know his/her rights and apply to the Higher Education Council if necessary. — Official Data
