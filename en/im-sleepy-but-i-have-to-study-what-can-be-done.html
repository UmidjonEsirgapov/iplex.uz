I'm sleepy, but I have to study! What can be done?

Any applicant or student can fall into a situation where they need to study while sleepy. In this case, try the following 8 methods to maintain attention and study effectively. — Source

1. Short rest

  • Close your eyes for 10-15 minutes and take a short rest to restore energy.
  • If there is time, a "power nap" - that is short sleep (15-20 minutes) can be effective.

2. Provide room ventilation and fresh air

  • A well-ventilated room improves brain activity and reduces fatigue.
  • Clean and cool air reduces sleepiness.

3. Fluid consumption

  • Drinking water or light tea refreshes the body.
  • Avoid drinking a lot of coffee or energy drinks because they can cause sleep disturbances.

4. Physical activity

  • Standing up, walking for a few minutes or doing simple exercises improves blood circulation and increases concentration.
  • A few stretching exercises are also good.

5. Increase the light

  • Sufficient lighting in the room reduces the production of sleep hormones.
  • If possible, studying under natural light is more effective.

6. Diet regulation

  • Eating light and nutritious foods is necessary.
  • Avoid eating heavy foods because they can cause sleepiness.

7. Focusing on something else

  • Taking a short break from the lesson, for example listening to your favorite music or watching a short video will help you concentrate.
  • Breaks should not exceed 5-10 minutes, otherwise efficiency may decrease.

8. Regulating the sleeping pattern

  • Sleeping and waking up at the same time every day helps the body to establish a routine.
  • Lack of sleep significantly reduces the effectiveness of studying, so it is important to follow the routine.
