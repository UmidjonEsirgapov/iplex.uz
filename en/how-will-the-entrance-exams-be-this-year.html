How will the entrance exams be this year?

Useful information for applicants and students:

Summary:
Compulsory subject block: - Mother tongue (Uzbek, Russian or Karakalpak language) - 15 questions, 2.1 points for each; - Mathematics - 15 questions, 2.1 points for each; - History of Uzbekistan - 15 questions, 2.1 points for each.
The maximum score will be 280.5 points; ❗️Pay attention!
applies equally to all forms of undergraduate education (full-time, part-time, evening); In the mandatory block, there will be no test questions from the subject of literature among the 15 questions from the subject of the mother tongue (Uzbek, Russian or Karakalpak language).

It is certain that there will be a test from 5 subjects this year, this is determined by the President's decision. (Last year's pandemic exception has been canceled, it doesn't mean it will happen every year.)

Block of compulsory subjects:

- Mother tongue (Uzbek, Russian or Karakalpak language) - 15 questions, 2.1 points for each;

- Mathematics - 15 questions for each 2.1 points;

- History of Uzbekistan - 15 questions, 2.1 points for each.

Total possible points - 94.5 points.

Up to 1 minute is allotted for each question in the subjects of the mother tongue and the history of Uzbekistan, and up to 1.5 minutes in mathematics.

Subjects in the main block corresponding to the field of study (varies depending on the field):

  • Subject 1 - 30 questions, 3.1 points for each;
  • Science 2 - 30 questions, 3.1 points for each.

Total possible points - 186 points.

A total of 2 hours will be given for 2 subjects in the main block.

The maximum score will be 280.5 points;

❗️Be careful! For example, an applicant applying for the mathematics course will take a test in mathematics both in the compulsory block and in the specialty block. The main difference between these two is that all the questions in the mandatory block are simplified, at the easiest level, and the questions in the specialty block are of different complexity (easy, average, difficult); As before, this new procedure is not related to military, religious, private, non-state and foreign HEIs.

- 3 compulsory subjects are not applicable for master's degree.

- This procedure in the form of 3+2. applies equally to all forms of undergraduate education (full-time, part-time, evening);

There will be no test questions from Literature among the 15 questions from the subject of the mother tongue (Uzbek, Russian or Karakalpak language) in the mandatory block. — Official Data
