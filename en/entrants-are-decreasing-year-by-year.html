The number of applicants is decreasing year by year: analysis of official statistics (2011–2024)

2011-2020: A period of stable growth

The number of applicants who applied to higher education institutions increased year by year between 2011 and 2020. During this period, the following indicators were observed:

  • 2011: 418,598 people

  • 2012: 432,017 people

  • 2013: 480,447

  • Year 2014: 543,155 people

  • Year 2015: 605,845 people

  • Year 2016: 663,304 people

  • 2017: 729,950 people

  • 2018: 814,952

  • 2019: 1,066,926

  • 2020: 1,484,085

2020 was a record year with more than 1.48 million applicants.

2021-2024: The years when the sharp decline began

After the peak in 2020 the number of applicants began to decrease:

  • 2021: 948,211 people

  • 2022: 1,214,232 people

  • 2023: 1,018,007 people

  • 2024: 894,279 people

As of 2024, the number of applicants has decreased by 589,806 people over the last four years. This means a decrease of about 40%.

What could be the reasons for the decrease?

  • Demographic changes: The decrease in the birth rate leads to a decrease in the number of young people.

  • Economic factors: Most of the young people have funds for study on a fee-contract basis.

  • Professional guidance: In recent years, the demand for technical schools, colleges and vocational schools has increased.

  • Those going abroad: The decrease in the number of applicants is also influenced by the number of people who prefer to study abroad.

Conclusion

Based on statistical data, it can be said that the number of applicants has been decreasing since 2020. This situation requires new approaches for the education system, including the creation of motivational programs for applicants and simplified financial opportunities. — Source
