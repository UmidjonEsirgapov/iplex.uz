Rules for using mobile phones in educational institutions

The use of mobile phones in educational institutions for pupils and students is strictly regulated. These rules are aimed at maintaining discipline, not disrupting the lesson process and protecting the educational environment.

Prohibitions on storing and charging the phone

  • It is forbidden to hang the phone around the neck, keep it in the chest or pants/skirt pockets.

  • The phone battery cannot be charged in the electrical network of the educational institution.

Prohibited content and actions on the phone

  • Showing videos and photos that promote violence, cruelty or pornography.

  • Distributing photos and videos that harm the reputation of an educational institution, vandalism or violence.

Prohibitions on the use of mobile phones during classes

  • Talking on the phone, sending SMS/MMS and other types of messages.

  • Put the phone on the table or listen to music during the lesson.

  • Use functions such as photo and video display, calculator, calendar, voice recorder.

  • Take photos or record videos.

  • Using other services.

Measures applied in case of violation of the order

If these rules are violated, any teaching staff has the right to take away the mobile phone, make a notification and hand it over to the director or deputy director. The phone is then returned to the parent.

Conclusion

The misuse of mobile phones has a negative impact on the educational process. Therefore, pupils and students should follow the established rules, and parents should explain this to their children. — More information
