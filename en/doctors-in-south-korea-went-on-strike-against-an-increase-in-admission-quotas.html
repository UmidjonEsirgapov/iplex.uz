They went on strike against the government's increase in admissions in medical universities.

South Korea plans to increase admissions to medical schools to 2,000 per year (currently 3,058 students are accepted) and therefore to add 10,000 additional doctors to the labor market by 2035 to cope with the aging population.

Striking doctors claim that medical educational institutions will not be able to cope with the sharply increased quota. According to them, the increase in numbers will only increase the state's medical expenses. As in the current situation, even with more students studying, doctors will still find themselves in high-demand fields such as dermatology and plastic surgery, and there will be a shortage of specialists in low-paid fields such as pediatrics, obstetrics and emergency medicine. — Read on infoedu.uz
