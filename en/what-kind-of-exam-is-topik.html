What is TOPIK?

TOPIK (Test of Proficiency in Korean) is a Korean language proficiency test designed to assess the ability of people whose first language is not Korean to speak and understand Korean. The TOPIK test determines the level of language learners' reading, writing and listening comprehension in Korean. The test has three different levels: beginner, intermediate and advanced levels. Accuracy is further expressed by six separate grades.

The purpose of the exam:

  • To promote the Korean language among non-Korean speakers and overseas Koreans and to set a clear goal for language learners;
  • To assess the level of Korean language proficiency and the possibility of using the TOPIK exam results for admission to Korean educational institutions and job search

Who organizes the TOPIK test?

The National Institute of International Education (NIIED), which operates directly under the Ministry of Education, Science and Technology, is responsible for the overall administration of the test, including supervising the institutions that administer the test locally and abroad.

Who is TOPIK for?

This comprehensive test is designed for non-native speakers of Korean, such as Korean language learners, international students who want to study in Korea, and individuals who want to work in Korean companies and government institutions.

This test is also a great opportunity to obtain official certification for those who want to use Korean in their careers. Candidates who have passed the test will know which aspects of the language they should pay more attention to in future studies.

How to register for the TOPIK test?

The first way to register for the test is to register online at www.topik.go.kr .

In addition, TOPIK is available in many countries. tests are held in the buildings of Korean Embassies and Korean cultural centers. Some Korean affiliate institutions also accept applications. You can also get information about such organizations from the above site. To register, you need to visit an embassy or a partner institution with 2 passport-sized photos and a registration fee (it varies by country).

When will the test be held?

The TOPIK test is held 6 times a year - once only in Korea (January), 4 times in Korea and overseas (April, May, July, October and November). In most countries, the TOPIK I test is given in the morning and the TOPIK II test in the afternoon. The test is always held on Sundays (in some countries also on Saturdays).

TOPIK test structure:

TOPIK test consists of two levels - TOPIK-I and TOPIK-II. TOPIK-I, i.e. elementary level, has two sub-levels - 1 and 2, TOPIK-II, i.e. Intermediate-Advanced level, consists of 4 levels - 3, 4, 5 and 6. Test takers are graded from 1 to 6.

Eligible test takers are awarded an official certificate with one of six grades: Lower Beginner (Level 1), Advanced Beginner (Level 2), Lower Intermediate (Level 3), Upper Intermediate (Level 4), Advanced Advanced (Level 5), and Advanced (Level 6).

1 and Levels 2 - Beginner Level

Levels 3 and 4 - Intermediate Level

Levels 5 and 6 - Advanced Level

TOPIK-I (levels 1-2) consists of only two sections - reading and listening. TOPIC II (Levels 3-6), which is a combined Intermediate-Advanced test, consists of 3 sections - Reading, Listening and Writing.

Each section is worth 100 points, with a total of 200 points for Beginner and 300 points for Intermediate-Advanced. The test includes composition, listening, and multiple-choice sections.

The test consists of two 90-minute sessions with a 30-minute break in between. The first session tests vocabulary and grammar and writing, and the second session tests listening and reading.
