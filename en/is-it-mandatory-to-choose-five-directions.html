Is it mandatory for applicants to choose 5 courses?

Applicants can choose 5 courses to enter higher education institutions. But this number is not a strict requirement. Many people ask "Do you have to choose five directions?" is asking the question. We clarify this issue below.

✅ ANSWER: No, it is not mandatory to choose five courses

According to the rules established by the State Testing Center (DTM), the applicant must choose at least one course of study. The choice of the remaining courses is at the discretion of the applicant.

The system will also give a warning when selecting courses through the personal cabinet: you can choose 1, 2, 3 or 4 courses as you wish. There is no rule that forces you to choose exactly 5.

Choose more courses - more opportunities

Although it is not necessary to choose five courses, choosing more courses increases your chances of becoming a student. Because if you don't pass the first competition, you will still have the opportunity to enter other universities through the next ones.

For example, if you choose only one course and don't get into it, you won't have the opportunity to participate in another competition. But if you choose 3-4 directions, you will be able to try your luck in each of them.

Conclusion

It is not mandatory for applicants to choose five directions. The minimum requirement is to choose at least one course. However, it is recommended to choose more routes to increase your chances.
