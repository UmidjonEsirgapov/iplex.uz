Certificate Validity Period

The validity period of national and international certificates is an important criterion for applicants. The validity period of each certificate is calculated according to:

  • National Certificates:

    • The validity period of national certificates is determined by the date of registration of the applicant. For example, if the certificate expires on June 20, the certificate will be considered if the applicant registers before June 20. However, if the applicant registers on or after June 21, the certificate will not be taken into account.

  • International Certificates:

    • For international certificates, the date of uploading the certificate to the program is taken as a criterion. For example, if the certificate expires on June 20, the certificate will be considered if the applicant uploads it to the program by June 20. However, if the applicant uploads the certificate to the program on or after June 21, the certificate will not be taken into account.

Certificate Verification Procedure

During the process of registering the applicant's certificates, various requirements will be imposed and they will be assigned the appropriate status:

  • National Certificates:

    • If if the applicant has a national certificate, these certificates are automatically displayed in the applicant's personal cabinet. These certificates are not required to be added to the program.

  • International Certificates:

    • Applicants with international certificates should visit the my.uzbmb.uz site and enter the certificate information into the program.

The validity of the certificates included in the program and compliance with the specified requirements are determined by the Knowledge Assessment Agency. are checked and given the following statuses:

  1. "Checking":

    • The certificate has been entered into the program and is currently being checked. This process takes some time.

  2. “Verified”:

    • The certificate has been confirmed to be valid and meet the requirements for granting the privilege. This certificate will be taken into account in the admission process.

  3. “Not approved”:

    • The validity of the certificate has not been confirmed or it has not met the requirements for granting the privilege. In this case, the applicant will be able to reload the certificate, if it was loaded incorrectly.
