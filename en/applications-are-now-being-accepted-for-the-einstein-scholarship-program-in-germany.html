Applications are now being accepted for the Einstein Scholarship Program in Germany

Useful information for applicants and students:

Summary:
Participants will receive a stipend of 10,000 euros and free accommodation and transportation expenses.
Photo: KeepTravel Applications are being accepted for the Einstein Scholarship Program in Germany.
Applications until May 15 of this year. must be sent via the link.

Participants will be awarded a stipend of 10,000 euros, free accommodation and transportation expenses will be covered.

Candidates up to 35 years old from all over the world can participate. They must have a university degree in the humanities, social sciences or natural sciences.

Candidates for the 2024 program must send a CV, a two-page project proposal and two letters of recommendation.

Accepted participants will receive a stipend of €10,000 and free accommodation and travel expenses.

At the end of the fellowship period, participants will present their project at an open lecture at the Einstein Forum and the Wittenstein Foundation.

A successful application must demonstrate the quality, originality, and relevance of the proposed project, as well as high intellectual development of the applicant.

The proposed project does not have to be completed during the program, but is a long-term project. may begin.

Applications should be sent through the link by May 15 of this year. The duration of the program is 5-6 months.
