What are the benefits of national certification?

Important news for students and applicants has been announced.

Summary:

Applicants Benefits If an applicant scores 86% or higher on the National Certification Exam: The maximum score is given in the entrance exams in the relevant subject.
National certificate concession for compulsory subjects If the applicant has chosen mother tongue as a compulsory subject and obtained 60% or higher in the national certificate exam, he/she will be given: maximum score (11 points) in compulsory mother tongue.

National certificate is not only for teachers, but also for applicants is also important. This certificate gives a number of benefits depending on the exam result. Below we will introduce the main advantages of the national certificate.

1. Benefits for teachers

If a teacher scores 80% or higher in the national certification exam:

  • A 20% bonus will be added to the monthly salary.

2. Benefits for applicants

If the applicant achieves 86% or higher in the national certificate exam:

  • Maximum points will be given in the entrance exams for the relevant subject.
  • If Block 1 subject – 93 points,
  • If In the case of the subject of the 2nd block - 63 points,
  • A maximum of 11 points is given out of the 10 compulsory tests.

If the applicant gets a result from 60% to 85%:

  • Points are calculated in proportion to the result.
    • For example, For a result of 80%, 80% of 93 points - 74.4 points will be given.

3. National certificate exemption for compulsory subjects

  • If the applicant chooses mother tongue as a compulsory subject and scores 60% or higher in the national certificate exam, he/she will be awarded:
    • Maximum points (11 points) in compulsory mother tongue.
    • This national certificate is also valid for compulsory subject does.

Conclusion

Having a national certificate gives a great advantage to teachers and applicants. Teachers will receive a 20% salary increase, and applicants will receive maximum or percentile points in entrance exams. This certificate is also preferential for compulsory subjects. — Source
