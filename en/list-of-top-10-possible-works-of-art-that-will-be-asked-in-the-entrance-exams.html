Important for entrance exams: list of 10 main works of art in 2025

The list of works of art approved by the Education and Qualification Assessment Agency has been announced for applicants who will take the exam in the main direction of mother tongue and literature. These works can be found in the exam in the form of test questions. Below you will get acquainted with these 10 important works.

10 works of art that you may encounter in the exam

1. Abdulla Qadiri - Past Days

This work of Abdulla Qadiri, one of the founders of the National Novel School, is considered one of the masterpieces of Uzbek literature. The work depicts love, social injustice and changing society.

2. Abdulla Qadiri - Scorpion from the Altar

Social problems, injustice and human values ​​are covered in depth in this work, and it is an important analytical resource for applicants.

3. O'tkir Hashimov - Between Two Doors

This work, which shows modern family and social problems, is very popular among applicants.

4. O'tkir Hashimov - There is light, there is shadow

This work highlights humanity, honor and complex choices in life. In terms of language and style, the work is convenient for analysis in exams.

5. Mirmukhsin is an Architect

Historical novel that talks about the art of architecture and the life of historical personalities.

6. Cholpon - Night and Day

This work in an enlightened spirit raises the ideas of nation, will and freedom. Comprehensible to applicants and enriched with values.

7. Abdurauf Fitrat - Abdulfayzkhan

This work in the spirit of modernism embodies historical and spiritual values. Nationalism is strongly reflected in Fitrat's work.

8. Abdulla Oripov - Sohibqiron

This work, written in a poetic style, shows the harmony of historical images and artistic thinking. It can be found in questions on poetry in the exam.

9. O'tkir Hashimov is The last victim of war

A story that includes the themes of war tragedy, humanity and kindness. Questions can be given on the basis of analytical or idea.

10. Abdulla Qahhor's stories (all)

Kahhor's stories such as "Sinchalak", "Pomegranate", "Pulling out a toothache" are among the brightest examples of Uzbek literature. Their artistic style and ideas can be the basis of many test questions.

Conclusion

It is extremely important to study the above 10 works of art for the applicants who are preparing for the literature test. The theme, idea, character of the characters and artistic tools covered in each work should be carefully studied. The Agency for Assessment of Knowledge and Skills is said to create questions based on this list, so a thorough study of these works is recommended. — More information
