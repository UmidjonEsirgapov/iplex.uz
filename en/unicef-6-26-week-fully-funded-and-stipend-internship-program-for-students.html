General Information:

This internship program announced by UNICEF is open to students from all over the world. The program also provides an opportunity to exchange experience for international students who are currently studying at one of the bachelor's, master's, and doctoral levels, or who have not completed it more than 2 years ago.

Who can apply for the program?

Candidates are required to meet the following eligibility criteria in order to be able to apply for the young graduate internship program project:

  • Candidates Must be an undergraduate, graduate, or doctoral student from any country, or a graduate of one of these programs;
  • Enrolled in or graduated from an undergraduate, graduate, or doctoral program within the last two years;
  • Must be fluent in at least one of the official languages of UNICEF - English, French, or Spanish;
  • Last university/education must have excellent academic records;
  • Candidates must not be under 18 years of age.

Program Benefits:

The UNICEF internship program for young graduates can last from 6 to 26 weeks and includes:

  • Monthly stipend;
  • Supplement for living expenses paid by UNICEF or a partner organization;.
  • A one-time payment as a contribution to travel and visa costs.

Application:

Firstly, candidates should choose one of the available practice areas here. After that, they can apply for the internship program of their choice here.

Note: Application deadlines vary in different fields. You can find out about it from the information of your chosen field.

Admission is carried out throughout the year.
