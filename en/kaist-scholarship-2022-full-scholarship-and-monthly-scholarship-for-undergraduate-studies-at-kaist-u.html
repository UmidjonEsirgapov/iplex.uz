Brief about the grant and the university:

KAIST (Korean Advanced Science and Technology Institute) is a national research university located in Daedeok, South Korea. KAIST was established by the Korean government in 1971 as the country's first public, research-oriented scientific and engineering institution. KAIST Scholarship 2022 is a fully funded scholarship program in South Korea and is intended to cover all tuition and living expenses. KAIST University itself ranks #41 in the ranking of world universities announced by THE (Times Higher Education). You can get information about all courses taught by KAIST University here. If you also want to study at such a prestigious university absolutely free, then hurry up!

Note: KAIST International Student Undergraduate Scholarships grant program is valid for undergraduate programs. All types of programs can be found here.

General details of the grant:

  • Country: South Korea;
  • University: KAIST University;
  • Grant type: International full grant;
  • Benefits: Full the tuition fee is covered and the student is provided with free insurance and a stipend of 350,000 Korean Won (about US$300) per month;
  • Duration: 8 semesters (4 years)
  • For whom: For all international students who wish to study at the undergraduate level at KAIST University;

Note: The demand for this grant is high, and the grades of the student who won it must not fall below 2.7 in the 4.3 point system!

Dates for sending documents:

Usually, the documents for the grant are accepted at 3 separate times (early, regular, late track) and at the same time Applications for the "Fall 2022 Late Track" are being accepted here. Acceptance of applications for this stage began on March 1, 2022 and required documents must be submitted by the deadlines indicated in the table below:

Start of receiving applications Deadline for submission of applications and documents Recommendation (Recommendation Letter) Deadline
March 1, 2022 May 27, 2022 May 30, 2022

Required Documents list:

  1. Application form (to be completed here);
  2. 1 letter of recommendation (only one teacher must be listed as the person providing the recommendation.
    The recommendation can be from the candidate's class teacher, academic advisor, or one of the specific subject teachers. The recommendation must be written entirely in Korean or English. The deadline for sending the recommendation is in the table above listed);
  3. High School Profile (you can find more details here);
  4. Language certificate: a certain score for the certificate is not set as a mandatory result, but is recommended. You can see the recommended language level in brackets (It is strongly recommended that applicants achieve above TOEFL iBT 83, TEPS(NEW) 326, IELTS 6.5 and TOEIC Listening & Reading 720 or higher.);
  5. SAT/ACT (Standardized Official Tests) results are not mandatory, but if available, these test results must be officially sent online by the test center to KAIST University (instead of them, an official statement about being an international Olympiad participant can also be submitted);
  6. Passport copy;
  7. Financial statement;
  8. Can help the candidate's application Other documents (optional).

You can download and review the complete instructions and additional documents here.

International candidates must meet the following 2 additional requirements:

  • Applicant's father and mother are both Korean citizens. It is not necessary;
  • Candidates of Korean origin must have completed primary and secondary school abroad (excluding international schools in Korea).
