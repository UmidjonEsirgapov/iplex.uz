A hackathon with a prize pool of $55,000

Useful information for applicants and students:

Brief content:
The goal of this hackathon is to increase and develop the interest of citizens in using open data.
The annual hackathon "Open Data Challenge" is aimed at the development of open data in the information technologies, entrepreneurship and economy of Uzbekistan.
The field of open data - is a tool that enables the development of the economy of Uzbekistan through innovative projects led by talented young people.

On December 3-5 this year, at the IT Park with the support of the Ministry of Information Technologies and Communications Development of the Republic of Uzbekistan, the coordinator of the projects of the OSCE in Uzbekistan, the Dubai Future Foundation, the IT Academy, the Republic of Uzbekistan A new hackathon "Open Data Challenge 2021" with a prize fund of $55,000 will be launched in cooperation with the Anti-Corruption Agency and the State Statistics Committee of the Republic of Uzbekistan. The purpose of this hackathon is to increase and develop citizens' interest in using open data.

The annual hackathon "Open Data Challenge" is aimed at the development of open data in the information technologies, entrepreneurship and economy of Uzbekistan. The field of open data is a tool that enables the development of the economy of Uzbekistan through innovative projects led by talented young people. To participate in the hackathon, one must have a certificate of completion of the "One Million Uzbek Coders" online course in one of the four directions. 11,000 US dollars will be awarded to the first places in each category, and prizes will be awarded to the winners.

This year, the "Open Data Cup" will also be held for the projects developed during the previous year's "Open Data Challenge" hackathons (from 2018 to 2020).

More information can be found at https://datahack.uz/ possible.

After registration for participation is completed, hackathon moderators will select projects that meet the competition criteria. The winner of the Open Data Cup competition will receive a cash prize of $6,000.
