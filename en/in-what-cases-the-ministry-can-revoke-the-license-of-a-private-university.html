In what cases the Ministry can revoke the license of a private university:

Today, the most relevant news in the field of education was:

Brief content:
The Ministry may apply to the court to revoke the license of a non-state university in the following cases: ⬅️ when the licensee does not eliminate the circumstances that led to the suspension of the license within the period set by the licensing body or the court; ⬅️ when the illegality of the licensing authority's decision to issue a license is determined; ⬅️ when the licensee regularly violates the terms and conditions of the license during a year (two or more times) or once grossly, if these violations caused damage to the lives and health of citizens, the rights and legal interests of individuals and legal entities, damage to public safety, and damage to the environment. Decision No. 80 of the Cabinet of Ministers The decision clearly states in which cases the licensing body (ministry) can apply to the court to cancel the license.
