Can a probationary period be used for hiring graduate students?

Useful information for entrants and students:

Brief content:
The issue of establishing a trial period when students enter a job after graduating from higher education institutions is clearly defined in the labor legislation.

The issue of determining the trial period when students enter work after completing vocational training is clearly defined in the labor legislation. The probationary period is a period of up to three months, which is given to determine the employee's ability to work and to check the feasibility of future cooperation.

In what cases is the probationary period not used?

The probationary period is not set for employment for the following categories of persons:

  • Pregnant women and women with children under the age of three.
  • On the basis of a state grant. graduates who have studied and are employed by referral within three months after graduating from a higher education institution.
  • Persons who belong to the socially needy categories, for example, those who are employed by referral to reserved jobs.
  • Those who are employed for the first time within a year from the date of graduation from secondary special, vocational or higher education institutions graduates.

What students should know about the probationary period

If you are entering employment for the first time as a graduate of a higher education institution, you are prohibited by law from being placed on probation. Based on the legislation, it is aimed at facilitating the adaptation of graduates to work and protecting their rights. — Official Data
