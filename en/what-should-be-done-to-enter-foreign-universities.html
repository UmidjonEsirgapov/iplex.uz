The process of applying to foreign universities can seem daunting and difficult, as the process is different from applying to local universities. It will be necessary to pass additional exams, translate and certify all documents, apply for universities and scholarships. We have prepared a useful guide for you, in which we have outlined the steps to successfully apply to foreign universities.
Step 1 - Study the opportunities.
First of all, you need to understand what opportunities are available in general. For this, it is effective to create a table and record all the results of your search there.

1. Think about which countries you want to study in.
-Think about which countries you want to live in and where you want to study; If the education is very expensive, this is not the end, you should also pay attention to the availability of scholarships; The list should contain objectively acceptable countries that you would like to enter.

2. Think about what field you want to study.
Maybe it will be several fields, maybe you want to change your major or get a second degree after your bachelor's degree. In any case, you need to decide on a major. For this, pay attention to the following points.

-Explore the fields you like;
-Find out what majors are promising in the countries you have identified;
-Find out what majors are in demand in the labor market;
-Find out what is best for you.
Conclusion: select several areas that have.

3. See which universities offer majors in the fields of your choice.
-Look at the QS ranking of the best universities in the world in the regions and countries of your choice;
-Add the programs you like to the table;
-Check the university where the program you like is taught: it should be a well-known university with a good reputation!
Conclusion: choose the programs you like. At this stage, there may be a lot of them. All of them should be tabulated or listed.

Step 2 - Choose Programs.
Now you need to choose the countries and programs you will apply for.

-Check the requirements for each program you like on the university website. Here you need to take into account your capabilities and the requirements of the university. Consider whether you are ready to take additional tests or complete a year at a university in your home country;
-Read detailed descriptions of the programs. List of subjects, additional options, decide if you like this learning format;
-Find out the cost of applying. The cost varies, but sometimes it goes up to almost $100 per program;
-Look at tuition fees and scholarships.
Summary: You should have a list of programs and universities to apply to.

Step 3 - Scholarships.
You will need scholarships for programs that you cannot afford to pay tuition for. The sooner you start your search, the better, as you will have more time to prepare. However, as a general rule, you should apply for funding 6-12 months before the planned start of your studies.

How to find a suitable scholarship?
-On the websites of universities.
-On the websites of public scholarships such as DAAD, CampusFrance, Fullbright, etc.

Requirements.
At this stage, find out which scholarships match your requirements. you must understand. What is often expected of candidates?

-Academic performance. As a general rule, candidates are expected to have good or excellent grades, so the higher the grade point average of the certificate or diploma, the better.
-Nationality. Often scholarships are reserved specifically for students from certain countries, for example there are many scholarships for third world countries where citizens of other developed countries cannot apply.
-Other achievements. Although not stated in the terms, any scholarship will have a bonus. It can be volunteering, creating your own project, internship or research at school or university.
-A motivation letter is required for almost all scholarships. Special attention should be paid to the question of how to write a motivation letter, because the decision on funding will largely depend on how and what you write and what impression you make.
Conclusion: you need to have a list of scholarships in countries that meet the requirements and funding. the steps do not seem so complicated and confusing. Good luck!
