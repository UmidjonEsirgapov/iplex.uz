Teachers' daily life: complexities and statistics

Teachers are the foundation of the education system, but not many people know about their daily routine and health. Below are interesting but sad facts about the daily life of teachers.

1. Work process and physical activity

  • Teachers spend 79% of their day on their feet.
  • On average, they have to speak 3.5-4 hours out loud per day.
  • Nominal working hours 6-7 hours, but the actual duration is 10-12 hours is sufficient.

2. Health and nutrition problems

  • More than half of teachers suffer from vitamin deficiency. This is due to their salary and schedule, which does not allow them to eat properly.
  • Each fifth teacher eats only two meals a day, while proper nutrition requires four or five light meals a day.
  • 50% of teachers sleep less than six hours a day, which negatively affects their work performance. shows.

3. Stress and mental health

  • 2/3 of teachers are under constant stress.
  • The highest level of stress is observed in the rector, principals and their deputies. Teachers of physical sciences, linguistics and elementary school also suffer from high stress levels.

4. Homework and social responsibilities

  • 85% of teachers are women. This means that they take on not only educational tasks, but also responsibilities such as housework and child rearing.

5. Rest and leisure

  • 80% of teachers do not have the opportunity to rest in sanatoriums or by the sea. And such rest plays an important role in restoring their health.
  • Only 14% of teachers find time to do morning exercises.

Conclusion

Teaching profession requires not only imparting knowledge, but also great physical and mental strength. These statistics show that necessary measures should be taken to support those working in the field of education and to improve their quality of life. — Official Data
