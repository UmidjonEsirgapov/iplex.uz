Postgraduate Courses in Development (EPOS)

Today's most relevant news in the field of education is:

Summary:
Scholarships offer opportunities for foreign graduates who are nationals of developing and newly industrialized countries in all disciplines and have at least two years of work experience to obtain a master's degree and, in exceptional cases, a doctorate, as well as a German university diploma (master's/PhD) at a state or state-recognized German university.
Under certain circumstances, scholarship holders may have the following additional benefits: • Monthly rent subsidy; • Monthly stipend for accompanying family members.
Eligibility: • Applicants meet the necessary academic requirements and can successfully complete a study program in Germany (above average score in the first academic exam - highest score in the third exam, language skills) • Applicants have a bachelor's degree in a relevant discipline (usually a four-year course); • Candidates have at least two years of work experience; • Candidates have an IELTS/TOEFL certificate.

General information:

Through development-oriented master's programs, DAAD helps train professionals from developing and newly industrialized countries. Well-trained local experts in contact with international partners play an important role in the sustainable development of their countries. They are the best guarantee for a bright future with low levels of poverty, quality education and universal health. The scholarships offer the opportunity for foreign graduates who are nationals of developing and newly industrialized countries in all disciplines and have at least two years of work experience to obtain a master's degree and, in exceptional cases, a doctorate, as well as a German university diploma (master's/PhD) at a state or state-recognized German university.

What costs are covered?

Individual scholarships for Master's courses in Germany only are listed in "List of all Master's courses awarded with application deadline for academic year 2023/24".

Funding period:

From 12 months Up to 42 months (depending on the study program)

Benefits:

• Depending on the academic level, 861 Euros for masters, 1200 Euros for PhDs monthly fee;

• Payments for health, accident and personal liability insurance;

• For travel allocated funds, if these costs are not covered by the candidate's home country or other funding sources.

Under certain circumstances, scholarship holders may receive the following additional benefits:

• Monthly rent subsidy;

• Monthly allowance for accompanying family members.

Choice:

• Candidates meet the necessary academic requirements and can successfully complete a study program in Germany (above average result in the first academic exam - highest result in the third exam, language skills)

• Candidates have a bachelor's degree in a relevant discipline (usually a four-year course);

• Candidates have at least two years of work experience;

• Candidates have IELTS/TOEFL certificate. (Certificate of German language proficiency for courses taught in German)

• Candidates are expected to demonstrate motivation for development and to take social responsibility, to initiate and continue the process of change in their personal and professional environment after the study/scholarship program.
