"We absolutely do not support the proposals made by the mayor of Yunusabad district" - Tashkent Institute of Chemical Technology

Today, the most relevant news in the field of education was:

Brief content:
Yunusabad district governor Bahodir Abduvaliyev announced that the Lyceum of Chemical Technology is working at 30 percent capacity, and 3 plots of land in the area will be sold because 70 percent are empty. On average, 26 students can be accommodated in each classroom. Today, the part of the building where the academic lyceum operates has 400 seats.
It is noted that in the "dead and unused pool" area of the lyceum mentioned by the mayor Bahadir Abduvaliyev, it is planned to build a dormitory for the students of the Tashkent Institute of Chemical Technology.
The Mayor of Yunusabad District, Bahadir Abduvaliyev, said that 3 plots of land in the area are being sold because 70 percent are empty. It is noted that the building of the Lyceum is currently being used in full. A dormitory for students will be built in the area, which the Mayor called a "dead and unused pool".

On April 9, during the visit of Tashkent city mayor Shavkat Umrzakov to Yunusabad district, district mayor Bahadir Abduvaliyev proposed to re-establish a school on the basis of the academic lyceum at the Tashkent chemical-technological institute. stated that he was working.

Tashkent Institute of Chemical Technology denied this. According to the press secretary of the institute, Egamnazar Abulbaqiyev, there is no information about the visit of the district governor to this academic lyceum, the building of the educational institution is currently working at full capacity. The academic lyceum of the chemical and technological institute was established in 1974, taking into account the placement of 35 students in each classroom.

Part of this building (with a fence in the middle and an open gate on the other side) was given to the children's school of music and art No. 13.

"According to the norms established for the academic lyceum, the part of the building in which the academic lyceum operates today has 400 seats due to the accommodation of an average of 26 students in each classroom. [Therefore, 268 students study in the building] at the same time. In addition, a 120-bed dormitory has been created for students from the regions of the republic, and currently the capacity of the existing buildings is being fully utilized," the comment says. was discussed in the presence of the mayor and supported by the city administration.

“This area is included in the list of land areas allocated on the basis of the right of permanent use in accordance with the relevant decision of the Cabinet of Ministers on July 6, 2023, and the construction of a student residence for the Tashkent Institute of Chemical Technology is planned on this plot of land.

Today, based on the decision of the Cabinet of Ministers, in this area Organizational work on the construction of a student residence is underway. In particular, after the relevant conclusions of the competent authorities, the design and construction works are carried out in the prescribed manner. Therefore, the Tashkent Institute of Chemical Technology absolutely does not support the proposals made by the governor of the Yunusabad district and considers it appropriate to thoroughly study the situation before making such proposals public", - the institute said in its statement. calling it an "unused pool" and announced that it will be sold through an auction and that 22.5 billion soums ($65,000 per square meter) are expected to be attracted.
