What is a social incentive grant (IRG)?

A social incentive grant (IRG) is an educational aid provided in the form of a state grant on the basis of a privilege established by the law, which:

  • to students admitted as part of a targeted admission,

  • on the basis of grants allocated by state bodies and organizations

  • also applies to those studying on the basis of industry and field grants.

Simply speaking, if someone entered the grant through an additional quota, he is considered to have received IRG. (TM) graduates were given additional points. This caused dissatisfaction among many applicants. After that, this privilege was canceled and instead they were allocated a separate additional quota.

Through the additional quota, TM graduates also had the opportunity to study on the basis of a state grant in their own special field without disturbing other applicants.

How will the grants be distributed?

Starting from 2025, all students will receive a grant based on the grant. the study period is redistributed from year to year. This means that:

  • Those who have studied well will continue their studies on the basis of a grant;

  • Those with poor study results will be transferred to a payment-contract basis.
