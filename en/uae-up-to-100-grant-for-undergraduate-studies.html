Brief about the grant and the university:

Khalifa University of the United Arab Emirates is located in Abu Dhabi, it is a very young university founded in 2007. THE (Times Higher Education) is ranked #351-400 in the ranking of world universities.

The President's Scholarship grant program offered by this university is intended to encourage excellent students from all over the world up to 100% based on CGPA indicators.

Who can apply for the grant? will receive:

All citizens of the country can apply for this grant program. To be able to apply for a scholarship, students must have applied for one of the undergraduate courses at the "College of Engineering and College of Science" faculties of Khalifa University. It is not necessary to submit a separate application for the grant program. All applications received for bachelor's programs of the aforementioned faculties will also be considered for the grant program. You can apply for one of the undergraduate programs here.

Requirements:

In order to be admitted to one of the undergraduate programs of Khalifa University, candidates must meet the following requirements:

  • The candidate must have graduated from high school (college/high school) no more than 2 years ago;
  1. EmSAT subjects - Mathematics 1000, Physics 800, Arabic language 700 and English 1250 are conditionally accepted for the "Foundation" program of the university; Or:
  2. EmSAT subjects on - Mathematics 1250, Physics 800, Arabic language 700 and English language 1400 will be fully admitted to the first year of the bachelor's program if they show the minimum result, details here;
  • IELTS 6.0/TOEFL iBT 79 and EmSAT English 1400
  • Regarding the EmSAT exam and its procedure learn more here.
  • Learn more about other requirements for international students here.

Grants for students admitted to the university's undergraduate level are allocated based on their GPA in the following percentages:

CGPA 3.0 –3.29 CGPA 3.0 – 3.29 < 2.0. You can find the complete list of documents required for applications here.
