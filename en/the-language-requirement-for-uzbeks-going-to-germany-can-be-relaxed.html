The language requirement for Uzbeks going to Germany can be relaxed

Useful information for applicants and students:

Summary:
Minister of Poverty Alleviation and Employment Behzod Musayev met with Klaus Mangold, President of the German company Mangold Consulting Gmbh and Managing Director, Dr. Christoph Mangold.
Conditions for foreign workers, vocational training and Uzbek citizens are in high demand in Germany. issues of recruitment were discussed.
At the meeting, it was mentioned that there is a great demand for mechatronics, logistics, and truck drivers not only in medicine, but also in metal processing enterprises.

Minister of Poverty Alleviation and Employment Behzod Musayev met with Klaus Mangold, president of the German company Mangold Consulting Gmbh, and managing director Dr. Christoph Mangold.

the conditions for workers abroad, vocational training and attracting Uzbek citizens to jobs in high demand in Germany were discussed.

Today medical specialists from Uzbekistan are working in Germany. It was noted that there is still a great demand for labor force in this direction. It was noted that more than 1,000 hospitals in Germany lack medical staff and Uzbekistan is ready to fill this gap. However, language problems are slowing down this process, and it was suggested to relax the language requirements in this regard. In particular, it was stated that it is necessary to shorten the duration of German language training courses.

At the meeting, it was mentioned that there is a great demand for mechatronics, logistics, and truck driving courses in metal processing enterprises. It was agreed to develop the legal basis for the faster implementation of all agreements. — Read on infoedu.uz
