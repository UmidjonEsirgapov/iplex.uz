Remembering the Most Difficult Information: Valuable Tips

Remembering complex facts, quotes or dates can be a big challenge for every reader and student. With the following tips, you can effectively remember even the most difficult information.

Outlining is Important

Outlining, that is, writing down important information, is an integral part of the learning process. Note-taking not only helps to organize information visually, but also improves brain function. When you outline a topic, you create a visual range that helps you remember the information for a long time. The main advantages of this method are as follows:

  1. Visualization: By writing down information, you give it a clear form, which makes it easier to memorize it.
  2. More sensitivity: The process of writing by hand or recording on a computer activates the brain and strengthens the memory of information.

Writing by hand and recording on a computer

Both methods have their own advantages and disadvantages.

  • Writing by hand:
    • Improves writing ability and helps the brain to receive information more deeply.
    • Even though the speed of writing is limited when writing by hand, this method helps to remember details better.
  • Note on the computer:
    • Enables writing faster and makes it easier to quickly edit information.
    • Reliable storage of information using cloud resources and connection from anywhere.
    • However, there is a risk of working with distractions (games, internet).

General Tips for Writing an Abstract

  1. Basic Notes: Record the main points of the teacher during the lesson. Don't try to write everything down, just note down the important parts.
  2. Write Clearly: Write your summaries in a way that you can understand, in your own words as much as possible.
  3. Mark New Words: Highlight new or difficult words. This makes it easier to remember them later.
  4. Ask Questions: If you miss something or don't understand, ask the teacher to repeat or explain. This will help you to understand the information completely and clearly.
  5. Rewrite the text: After the lesson, rewrite your notes cleanly. With this method, you will absorb the information more firmly.
  6. Abbreviations and Symbols: Learn to write using abbreviations and symbols. This method saves time and allows you to read abstracts faster.

Find the Best Approach

Each person needs to find an approach that works for him to remember new information. Some people prefer to write by hand, while others are more comfortable taking notes on a computer. Choose the method that works best for you and improve the learning process according to your experience. — Official Data
