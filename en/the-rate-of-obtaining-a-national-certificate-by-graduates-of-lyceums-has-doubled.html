The rate of obtaining a national certificate by graduates of lyceums has doubled

About the expected changes in the education system:

Brief content:
This year, more than 3,000 graduates of academic lyceums received a national certificate in foreign languages.
This figure is twice as much compared to last year, according to the results of tests conducted by the Ministry of Knowledge and Skills Assessment Agency in May of this year to determine the level of foreign language proficiency. According to the report, 3,248 students of the 2nd level of academic lyceums received a national certificate for foreign languages at the B1-C1 level.
In total, 1,579 students received a B1, 1,504 B2, and 165 C1 level national certificates.
