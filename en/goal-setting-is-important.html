In 1979, a remarkable study was conducted at Harvard University. All MBA graduates are asked the following question:

  • "Do you have a specific goal in life that you always strive for?"

The answers were as follows:

  • 84% of the graduates said they did not have a goal.
  • 13% of the graduates were able to state their goals, but they did not have a clear strategy or plan to achieve them.
  • 3% of the graduates admitted that they have a goal that drives them and, unlike others, they wrote down and made a clear strategy or plan to achieve it.

Later, the researchers followed their lives for 10 years and came to the following interesting conclusion:

  • 13% of the graduates with a vision of a goal are surfing. Compared to 84%, they were 2 times more successful in life.
  • 3% who wrote down a goal and strived towards it every day were 10 times more successful than 97% graduates.

Conclusion

If a person sets a goal during his life and strives for it, he will use his hidden potential. It can be used 10-20 times more effectively. — Read on infoedu.uz
