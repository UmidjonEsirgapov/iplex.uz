If the students studying on the state grant re-enter, will there be a contract?

About the expected changes in the education system:

Brief content:
In particular, a clear procedure has been established for students studying on the basis of a state grant.
Conclusion If a student studying on a state grant wants to receive higher education again, a grant will not be allocated for that stage and he will study under a contract.

If a student studying on a state grant resubmits again, will he have a contract?

Some news in the higher education system based on the Presidential Decree of May 25, 2024 was introduced. Especially for students studying on the basis of a state grant, a clear procedure has been established for the cases of re-submission.

What did the previous year's writing say?

Last year, the following message appeared in the personal office of some applicants:

"Because you were recommended for studentship on the basis of a state grant in previous years, you cannot participate in the competition for a state grant this year."

This writing caused confusion among many people. This issue was clarified by the new presidential decree.

What will change according to the new regulation?

Secondary and further higher education - on the basis of a contract

According to the presidential decree, now:

  • Secondary and further higher education is carried out only on a fee-contract basis.

  • A student who received a state grant for one stage of higher education, if he reads this stage again, the grant will not be allocated to him.

In what cases will the state grant not be allocated?

  • If the student studied with a state grant in the 1st stage, and then to the 1st stage again the following year if he begins to transfer to another direction - he studies on the basis of a contract.

  • But later, i.e. from the 2nd level, if his mastery is high, he has the right to return to the state grant.

Who does this apply to?

For students currently studying

  • The current student can leave his place and re-submit as an applicant.

  • However, repeated grant will not be allocated for the stage he studied on the basis of the previous grant - he will have to study under a contract.

What if he studied under a grant for two years or more?

  • Even if such a student can re-apply for studies, he can transfer to a state grant only in the 3rd or 4th year stages.

  • So, starting stages in a new direction will be only in the contract.

Conclusion

A student who studied with a state grant can return to higher education. if he wants to study, a grant is not allocated for that stage and he studies under a contract. However, based on a high learning rate, there is a possibility to switch to a state grant in the next courses. Although this procedure existed in the previous legislation, it was officially strengthened by the Presidential Decree and is being used in practice. — Source
