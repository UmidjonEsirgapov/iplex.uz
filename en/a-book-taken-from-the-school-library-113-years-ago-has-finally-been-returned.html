A collection of works of George Byron taken from a school library in Cumbria, England, was returned to the school 113 years later. This was reported in the Guardian newspaper. The book was received by Leonard Eubank, who was a school student in 1911.

Leonard Eubank was born in 1893 and graduated from school in 1911. He later entered Queen's College, Oxford University. Four years later, during World War, despite his poor eyesight, he enlisted in the war and was killed in action on February 23, 1916.

Leonard Eubank was recognized in the school's hero list as "the best, honest and loyal Englishman". According to the representatives of the school, this book, taken from the library before the First World War, after a century and more than ten years, has finally returned to its place.
