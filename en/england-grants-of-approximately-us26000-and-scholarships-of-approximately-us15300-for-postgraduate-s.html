Brief about the grant program:

The ongoing IDS Graduate Scholarship 2021 program is offered to international students from low- and middle-income countries. Citizens of countries included in this list (Uzbekistan is also on this list) can apply for this program. For one of the grants this year, there will be a special place for 1 female candidate from Asian and African countries, supporting female candidates to also apply for this grant.

Grant Benefits:

Up to £18,500 (about US$26,000) towards tuition fees and living expenses for the winning candidates. Grants in the amount of up to 11,000 pounds (about 15,300 US dollars) will be allocated.

Requirements for candidates:

Only citizens of the countries on this list (Uzbekistan is also on this list) can apply for the program. Candidates must:

  • be distinguished by having achieved achievements in the field of development and fully mastering the necessary academic skills;
  • willingness to work for educational, economic, social or cultural development in their home countries, should be able to demonstrate this by highlighting past experiences and future plans;
  • in high-income (developed) countries must not be studying or working;
  • all must meet the IDS grant and University of Sussex entry requirements;
  • candidates must have a certificate of English proficiency. Language level requirements vary by university course. Full details can be found here.
