Central Asian Bureau for Analytical Reporting (CABAR.asia) The Central Asian Bureau for Analytical Reporting is a regional analytical, information and educational platform for Central Asia. Its main goal is to develop journalistic analysis and conduct training on new mass media. The Office of the Institute of War and Peace in Central Asia (IWPR CA) announces the start of accepting applications for young researchers from Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan and Uzbekistan to participate in the 4th CABAR.asia School of Analytics. will be taught during:

  • Writing analytical articles;
  • What tools to use for data visualization;
  • Using artificial intelligence in the field of analysis;
  • Getting acquainted with data processing and analysis tools;
  • How to effectively promote analytical articles.

In addition to the theoretical part, Meetings will be held with practitioners in the fields of political science, public administration, international relations, economics and mass media. Well-known journalists and political experts from Europe, the CIS and Central Asia will participate as trainers.

The program will be held on November 15-20 in Almaty. Depending on the pandemic situation, the procedure may change.

All expenses will be covered during the program. Classes are held in Russian.

Requirements:

  • The candidate must be between 21-40 years old;
  • Be a citizen of Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan and Uzbekistan;
  • Social sciences (political science, international relations, Bachelor or Master degree in economics, journalism) or other relevant fields;
  • Work experience in government, international, non-profit organizations or mass media is preferred;
  • Interest in topics and research on the problems of the Central Asian region;
  • Must be able to participate in the program in November 2021.

Qualification will take place in 2 stages:

In stage 1, participants will go through an online list and upload the following information:

  • CV/biography (no more than one page);
  • motivational letter of 250 words;
  • Two topics for an analytical article with initial plans (1 should not exceed one page, plans should have a problem, relevance, short structure, preliminary recommendations and conclusions);
  • References to published analytical materials should be provided (if available);

Selected candidates will be interviewed at the 2nd stage.
