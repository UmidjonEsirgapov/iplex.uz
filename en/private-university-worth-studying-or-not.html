Why do applicants hate private universities?

Many private universities advertise when the results of the entrance exams for public HEIs are released. This is natural. But it is often observed that applicants react with hatred towards them. Why?

Actually, when choosing a university abroad, applicants are interested in a specific professor or program and apply for that direction. And in our country, many people apply for the name of OTM, state status or simply "admission". For this reason, private universities are often undervalued.

Advantages of private universities

1. Competitive Environment

The proliferation of private universities increases competition in the education system. This is just a useful process.

2. Don't lose a year

If you couldn't get into a public higher education institution, a private university is an alternative way for you. Later, if you want, you can transfer your studies to the state HEI.

3. Education in foreign languages

Many private universities teach in English or other foreign languages. This creates an additional opportunity in the international labor market.

4. Freedom and flexibility

Private HEIs give students more freedom. There are less strict restrictions like public universities.

Disadvantages of private universities

  • Tuition costs are more expensive compared to public higher education institutions.

  • The diploma of some private universities may not be recognized everywhere.

  • Not all higher education institutions are the same in terms of quality, so you need to be careful when choosing.

Conclusion

Studying at a private university is a personal decision. What matters is your knowledge and aspirations. If you have internal motivation and a passion for learning, you can find success regardless of whether you study at a private or public higher education institution.
