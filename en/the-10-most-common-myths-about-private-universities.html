TOP-10 myths about non-public higher education institutions

Today, there are different views and opinions about non-state higher education institutions. Many people think that private higher education institutions are of lower quality than public higher education institutions, but this is a far from the truth concept. Such institutions are distinguished by their innovative approach, high scientific level, modern educational programs and experienced professors.

At the same time, there are many myths about non-governmental higher education institutions, which prevent us from understanding their real potential and importance. Below, we'll break down ten of the most common myths about private higher education institutions and determine how true they are.

Myth 1: Private universities are only for the rich

Truth: Private universities often offer a variety of scholarships, grants, and contract-paying education loans. Underprivileged students can also get an education through these opportunities.

Myth 2: Private universities do not provide quality education

Truth: Private universities often offer high quality education because the market requires them to be competitive. This is why many non-state universities are popular with modern educational programs, advanced technologies and qualified professors.

Myth 3: Diplomas of non-state universities are not recognized

Truth: Many non-state universities have international accreditation, and their diplomas are recognized by many countries and organizations. These universities pay great attention to international cooperation.

Myth 4: Non-state universities are opened only for profit

Truth: In addition to profit, non-state universities work to provide students with high-quality education and prepare them to be useful people for society. They are engaged in research and academic achievement.

Myth 5: Non-public universities lack adequate student support

Truth: Non-public universities often offer comprehensive support services to students, such as academic advising, career services, psychological counseling and housing.

Myth 6: Non-public universities provide fewer opportunities than public universities

Truth: Many private universities offer various opportunities for students, such as international exchange programs, internships, and research opportunities.

Myth 7: Private universities lack social life

Truth: Private universities also have many social clubs, sports events, cultural events, and student organizations exist that enrich students' lives.

Myth 8: Non-public universities focus only on academics

Truth: Non-public universities often emphasize practical education and offer many practical training and internships to prepare students for the job market.

Myth 9: Non-public universities offer education only in special fields

Truth: Non-public universities offer education in a wide variety of fields, including science, arts, business, technology and many other fields.

Myth 10: Contract prices at private universities are too high

Truth: Contract prices may be high in non-state universities, but many universities offer various financial aid programs and installment payment options to reduce fees. — Official Data
