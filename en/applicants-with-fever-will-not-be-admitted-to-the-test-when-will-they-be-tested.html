Applicants with fever will not be admitted to the test. When will they be tested?

About the expected changes in the education system:

Brief content:
On the day of the entrance exam held for the 2020/21 academic year, the applicant will not be admitted to the exam if his body temperature is higher than 37 degrees.
"Applicants who have passed the medical examination of the Agency of Sanitation, Epidemiology and Peace and have not been diagnosed with the coronavirus will be tested on the fourth day after the completion of the national tests.

If the applicant's body temperature is higher than 37 degrees on the day of the entrance exam for the 2020/21 academic year, will not be included in the exam. After the tests are fully completed, an exam will be held for such applicants on the fourth day.

According to the briefing held at AOKA, in order to ensure the safety of the applicants during the tests during the coronavirus pandemic, an applicant whose body temperature was found to be higher than 37 degrees upon entering the territory will not be included in the tests, and the responsible employees of the Sanitary-Epidemiological and Peace Agency and the State Agency a document will be drawn up and a medical examination will be conducted in the presence of a representative of the test center.

This procedure is determined by the statement of the State Commission No. 1.

"For those applicants who have passed the medical examination of the Agency of Sanitation, Epidemiology and Peace and have not been diagnosed with the coronavirus, the tests will be conducted on the fourth day after the completion of the tests for the republic.

Based on the conclusion presented by the SES, the tests will be conducted. Applicants who are not admitted due to high body temperature will be warned by DTM at least one day before the test (by sending a message to their personal office and phone).

If an applicant is found to have a high body temperature even in the exams held 4 days after the end of the tests, he will not be able to participate in the exam," the briefing newsletter says. — Official Data
