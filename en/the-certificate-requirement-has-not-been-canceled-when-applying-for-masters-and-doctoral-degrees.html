The certificate requirement has not been canceled when applying for master's and doctoral degrees

Misinterpretations in social networks have been clarified

On the basis of Presidential Decree No. PF-114 adopted on July 19, 2025, some regulatory legal documents have been amended. In particular, the fourth paragraph of Clause 6 of Resolution No. PQ-5117 of May 19, 2021 was deleted. The requirement of a foreign language certificate was provided for the first time in this paragraph.

On this basis, a false conclusion appeared in social networks that this requirement has now been completely cancelled. However, this is contrary to the truth.

Decree No. PF-81: Requirements are clearly defined

According to Presidential Decree No. PF-81 published on May 24, 2024, the requirement for a foreign language certificate for admission to graduate and post-graduate education is defined as follows:

  • For foreign languages - at least C1 level;

  • Social, economic, humanitarian and technical fields - at least B2 level;

  • Other educational areas and culture and in sports specialties - at least at the B1 level.

Current requirements are still valid

The Ministry of Higher Education, Science and Innovation stated that the requirement for a foreign language certificate based on Decree No. PF-81 is still valid. With Decree No. PF-114, only the previous legal bases were changed, but the requirement itself was not canceled.

Conclusion

National or international certificates confirming the level of knowledge of a foreign language are still mandatory for admission to master's and doctoral studies. Making a decision based on false interpretations circulating on social media can lead to problems in the admissions process. It is recommended to follow the official sources and carefully study the requirements for submitting documents. — Official Data
